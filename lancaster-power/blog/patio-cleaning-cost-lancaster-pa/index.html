What Affects Patio Cleaning Costs in Lancaster

The size and condition of your patio drive most of the price variation. A well-maintained 200-square-foot concrete pad that gets regular cleaning is a quick, inexpensive job. A 400-square-foot flagstone patio covered in black algae with years of organic buildup embedded in the joints is a different story.

Surface Material

Concrete is the most forgiving — it can handle higher pressure without damage and responds well to surface cleaner equipment that cleans uniformly without streaking. Pavers and brick require more controlled pressure to avoid displacing joint sand or blasting out mortar. Natural stone like bluestone or flagstone is the most delicate — we use lower pressure hot water methods and sometimes a chemical pre-treatment to lift stains without etching the surface.

Biological Growth

Lancaster's humid summers create ideal conditions for algae, moss, and mold growth on any shaded or north-facing patio surface. Significant biological growth requires a chemical pre-treatment (usually a sodium hypochlorite or eco-friendly algae remover) to kill the organism before power washing. This adds time and a modest cost but gets dramatically better results than pressure alone.

Stain Severity

Ground-in grease from outdoor grills, rust stains from metal furniture, tannin stains from fallen leaves and wood, and efflorescence from concrete all respond differently to different treatments. We assess the stain type before we start and choose the right approach. Some stains require a separate degreaser or rust treatment — we'll flag those upfront if the quote needs to account for them.

Patio Cleaning vs. Patio Sealing

Many Lancaster homeowners who get their patio cleaned for the first time realize how good it can look — and then ask about sealing it to keep it that way longer. Sealing makes sense on concrete and paver patios where you want to slow down the re-soiling process and protect against freeze-thaw damage. We can clean and seal in the same visit for concrete and most paver surfaces. Natural stone sealing is more specialized and we'll discuss options based on what you have.

How Often Should You Clean Your Patio in Lancaster?

Most Lancaster patios benefit from professional cleaning every one to two years depending on how much shade they get and how much organic debris falls on them. Patios under trees or along north-facing walls with limited sun may need annual cleaning to stay ahead of algae and mold. Patios in full sun in open areas can often go two years between professional cleanings with good results.

After cleaning, a regular rinse with a garden hose every few weeks through pollen season and leaf-fall significantly reduces buildup between professional cleanings.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does patio cleaning cost in Lancaster, PA?

Most patio cleaning jobs in Lancaster run $150 to $450 depending on size and surface material. A standard 200-400 sq ft concrete or paver patio typically costs $175 to $350. Heavy biological growth or specialized surfaces like natural stone may cost more.

What's the best way to clean a paver patio in Lancaster?

Paver patios require lower-pressure washing than concrete to avoid displacing joint sand. We use controlled pressure with a wide fan tip or surface cleaner attachment, often with a pre-treatment for algae or mold. After cleaning, re-sanding and sealing is recommended to restore the joint material.

Can you clean and seal my patio in the same visit in Lancaster?

Yes — for concrete and most paver surfaces, we can clean and apply sealant in the same appointment. The cleaning needs to dry fully before sealer goes down, so same-day service works best in dry weather with good sun exposure. We'll discuss options at the time of booking.

How do you remove algae and mold from a patio in Lancaster?

We pre-treat biological growth with a sodium hypochlorite or eco-friendly algaecide, let it dwell to kill the organism, then pressure wash it away. This method gets better results than pressure alone and reduces how quickly the growth returns.

How often should I have my patio professionally cleaned in Lancaster, PA?

Most Lancaster patios benefit from professional cleaning every 1 to 2 years. Patios under trees or in shaded areas may need annual cleaning to stay ahead of algae and mold. Regular garden hose rinses between professional cleanings help extend the time between full service visits.
