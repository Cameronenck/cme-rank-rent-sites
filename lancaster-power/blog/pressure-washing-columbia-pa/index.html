House Washing in Columbia

Most of the call volume we get from Columbia borough is for house washing on older homes. The combination of age, the river climate, and mature street trees means that a lot of homes here are running algae streaks down their siding or brick faces well before the owner notices from the street. By the time the green or gray discoloration is obvious from the sidewalk, the biological growth has typically been working for a season or more.

For the vinyl and painted wood siding found on a lot of Columbia's post-war and more recently renovated homes, we use soft washing — low pressure with biodegradable cleaning solution that kills algae and mildew at the root level rather than just knocking it off the surface under high pressure. The difference matters. High pressure on siding forces water behind panels, cracks caulk around window and door frames, and can strip paint from wood trim. Soft washing avoids all of that and produces cleaner, longer-lasting results.

For the older brick and stone construction that's common throughout the original borough grid — especially the Federal and Victorian-era homes on the blocks closer to the river and the historic commercial district — we dial the pressure back significantly and use solutions appropriate for aged masonry. Old mortar joints in Columbia borough are not candidates for aggressive high-pressure washing. We've done enough older Lancaster County properties to know the difference between a brick face that can handle standard cleaning and one that needs a very light hand. If we see something that concerns us before we start, we'll say so.

Driveway and Concrete Cleaning in Columbia

A lot of Columbia's residential properties have older concrete — narrow driveways, rear alley access, and tight side-yard parking pads that see constant freeze-thaw stress through every Pennsylvania winter. That repeated cycling opens up the concrete surface over time, making oil staining and algae penetration progressively worse. Standard pressure washing addresses surface-level staining, but embedded oil and grease require pre-treatment with a commercial degreaser before washing to actually pull the contamination out of the pores rather than just diluting it into the rinse water.

We use a commercial rotary surface cleaner on flat concrete surfaces. It covers a consistent path at controlled pressure and eliminates the striping marks you get from sweeping a wand. Oil spots are called out and pre-treated before we start — no surprises on the final price. Concrete sidewalks, back patios, and steps are easy add-ons when the equipment is already on-site, and the added cost is minimal compared to booking a second trip.

Deck and Fence Washing in Columbia

Decks on Columbia properties deal with a heavy pollen season from the mature trees throughout the borough, plus the added moisture that comes with being close to the river. A deck that hasn't been cleaned in a season or two typically shows gray weathering on the wood surface, mildew collecting in the board gaps, and algae on any sections that stay consistently shaded. If you're planning to restain or reseal, cleaning is the required first step — stain applied over biological growth or oxidized wood just doesn't bond properly.

We match the pressure to the material. Pressure-treated pine and softer woods get lower PSI to avoid raising the grain and roughening the surface before staining. Composite decking gets handled even more carefully — high pressure will damage composite material and void warranties on newer products. We've done enough deck prep jobs in Lancaster County to know which materials need what treatment before we start.

Vinyl privacy fencing is one of the most satisfying quick wins we do — it goes from gray-green and grimy to bright white in one pass and the transformation is immediate. Wood privacy fences take a bit more time but are absolutely worth cleaning before applying fresh stain or sealant.

Roof Soft Washing in Columbia

The dark gray-black streaking that runs down roof slopes in Columbia is Gloeocapsa magma — a cyanobacterium that establishes itself in the limestone filler of asphalt shingles. It's widespread in Lancaster County and accelerates noticeably in humid, shaded conditions like those common near the Susquehanna. Pressure washing a roof removes the surface discoloration but doesn't kill the organism, damages shingle granules, and voids most shingle manufacturer warranties. It's the wrong tool.

Soft washing is the manufacturer-recommended method: low pressure and a specialized chemical solution that kills the growth at the cellular level. The dead material rinses off over the following weeks of rain. Done correctly, results last three to five years. We carry the appropriate chemical mix and equipment for roof soft washing and have done it on a wide range of pitch angles and shingle types across Lancaster County properties.
