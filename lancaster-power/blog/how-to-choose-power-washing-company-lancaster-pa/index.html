How to Choose a Power Washing Company in Lancaster PA — What to Look For

Published June 16, 2026 | By Lancaster Power Washing Pros

Look, you need someone to power wash your driveway or house. You hop on Google, and there are a dozen contractors claiming they're the best. Prices range all over the place. Some have tons of reviews, some have none. How do you actually pick the right one?

I'll be straight with you. After years of doing this work in Lancaster PA, I've seen the difference between contractors who take pride in their work and ones who just want your money and your property gets damaged. This post is going to walk you through what actually matters when you're hiring someone for power washing.

Insurance Is Non-Negotiable

This is the first thing I ask people to check, and it's the one most homeowners skip. You need to ask: are you insured? Specifically, do you have general liability insurance?

Here's why it matters. Power washing equipment is powerful. A contractor mistakes the pressure, angles the wand wrong, or uses too much force — and suddenly there's water in your walls, damage to your siding, or a cracked window. If they're insured, their policy covers it. If they're not, you're stuck trying to sue them personally to recover costs. That usually doesn't end well.

Good contractors carry a minimum of $1 million in general liability coverage. They should be able to show you a certificate of insurance before they start work. If someone can't or won't, don't hire them. The price savings aren't worth the risk.

They Should Understand Different Surfaces Need Different Approach

Here's the thing that separates pros from amateurs: good contractors know that every surface is different.

Vinyl siding? You don't pressure wash that with 2,500 PSI. That forces water behind the panels and into the wall cavity. Soft washing (low pressure, cleaning solution) is the right approach. Concrete driveway? That can take 2,500–3,500 PSI and a surface cleaner attachment. Roof? Soft wash, no question. Wood deck? Depends on the wood's age and condition.

When you call and ask "how much for house washing," a good contractor won't quote you without understanding what surfaces you need cleaned. They'll ask questions: What's your siding made of? How old is it? Any loose or damaged areas? They want to know because the job method matters.

If someone just says "yeah, $200," that's a red flag. They're going to treat every surface the same way and damage some of them.

Look For Established Local Presence and Real Reviews

I'm not saying hire someone who's been around for 20 years automatically. But you want someone with real presence in Lancaster PA and a track record you can verify.

Check Google Business Profile reviews. But don't just look at the star count. Read the actual reviews. Look for reviews with details: "cleaned my driveway, took three hours, looks great," not just "5 stars." Look for photos from real customers showing before/after work. And look for consistency — if most reviews say the job was done fast and clean, they're probably doing it right. If the pattern is "did a good job but showed up late," you know what to expect.

Three to five years of established business is a solid marker. If you can't find any reviews at all, that contractor hasn't built credibility yet. That doesn't mean they're bad — maybe they're new — but you're taking on more risk.

Get a Written Estimate and Ask About Method

Any contractor worth hiring will give you a written estimate. Not a text quote, not a phone call number. Something in writing that breaks down the work and the price.

And on that estimate or in the estimate conversation, a good contractor will explain method. Something like: "We'll soft wash your siding at 300 PSI with biodegradable cleaner to kill the algae, rinse thoroughly, then pressure wash the driveway at 2,800 PSI with a surface cleaner." You can follow that. It makes sense. They're showing they think about the job.

If the estimate is vague ("we'll power wash your house for $150") and the contractor can't or won't explain how, that's not professional. Walk away.

Don't Hire Based on Price Alone

This deserves its own section because it's the mistake I see most often.

You get three estimates. One is $150, one is $250, one is $300. You hire the $150 guy because, hey, why pay more for the same thing?

Here's the problem: the $150 guy is either rushing (one hour, probably skipping important steps), using cheap equipment (which does a poor job), or both. The $250-$300 range is where you find contractors who take time, use quality equipment, and stand behind their work.

Get three estimates, yes. But if one is way cheaper, ask why. The answer is usually "we work fast," which translates to "we don't take the time to do it right." The middle price range, from a contractor with solid reviews and good communication, is almost always your best bet.

They Should Be Easy to Communicate With

I know this sounds simple, but it's important. When you call or text with a question, do they respond the same day? Are they straightforward about what they can and can't do? Do they sound like they care about your specific property and situation, or do they sound like they just want to book you?

A good contractor will take time to understand your needs. They'll ask questions. They'll give you a realistic timeline. They won't pressure you into a same-day booking. And they'll be clear about what's included and what isn't.

If the communication feels off — delayed responses, evasive answers, high-pressure sales — that's a sign of how the actual job will go too.

Avoid Red Flags

A few things should send you running:

  • Cash only, payment upfront. Professional contractors accept cards and don't ask for full payment before work starts.
  • No insurance or won't provide proof. Non-negotiable.
  • No reviews or all recent reviews with similar phrasing. Could be fake.
  • No clear answer about method or equipment. They don't know what they're doing.
  • Pressure to book immediately. Good contractors have work lined up. They don't need to rush you.
  • Quotes vary wildly. If one contractor quotes $600 and another quotes $1,500 for the same job, one of them is lying about scope.

Bottom Line

Hiring a power washing contractor comes down to three things: (1) Are they insured? (2) Do they understand the job and explain it clearly? (3) Do they have real, verifiable work in Lancaster PA?

Price matters, but it's not everything. A cheap job that damages your property costs way more than paying slightly more for someone who does it right. Trust your gut. If they communicate well, answer your questions, and have reviews from people like you, you're probably in good hands.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I ask a power washing company before hiring them?
Ask three things: (1) Are you insured? Get proof of general liability — minimum $1 million. (2) What's your PSI and approach for my surface? They should adjust pressure based on the material — vinyl siding gets 500 PSI, concrete gets 2,500 PSI. (3) Do you use cleaning solutions? On some jobs (algae, mildew), a biodegradable cleaner does more work than pressure alone. Any contractor who won't answer these questions directly isn't worth hiring.
How do I know if a power washing company is legitimate?
Legitimate contractors will: (1) Give you a written estimate. (2) Have insurance and be able to show you the certificate. (3) Show you their equipment — you want to see a quality machine and hose setup, not a cheap rental unit. (4) Have Google reviews with real photos and detailed feedback. (5) Answer questions about method — they can explain why they'd soft wash your roof and pressure wash your driveway. If someone wants cash upfront or can't explain their process, that's a red flag.
What's the difference between insured and uninsured power washing companies?
An insured company carries general liability insurance (typically $1-2 million coverage). If they damage your property, their insurance pays. An uninsured company doesn't — you'd have to sue them directly to recover costs, which is expensive and often fruitless. Never let an uninsured contractor near your home. The price difference is usually small (maybe 10-15%), but the protection difference is huge. Always ask to see their certificate of insurance before they start work.
Should I hire the cheapest power washing company?
No. The cheapest quote usually means they're rushing, using low-quality equipment, or skipping important steps like soft washing delicate surfaces. What you want is fair pricing from someone who takes time to do it right. Get 3 estimates. If one is 50% cheaper than the others, ask why. The answer is usually 'we're faster and cut corners' — not what you want. Look for someone in the middle price range with good reviews and solid communication.
How long has your power washing company been in business?
Experience matters, but it's not everything. A contractor with 5 years in business and a solid reputation is better than someone with 15 years and no reviews. That said, look for someone with at least 2-3 years of established operation. They should be able to show you past work (photos of before/after on similar properties in Lancaster PA), and ideally they'll have Google reviews from customers in your area.
