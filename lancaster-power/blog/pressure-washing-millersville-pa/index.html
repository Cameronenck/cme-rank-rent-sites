House Washing in Millersville

The most common call I get from Millersville homeowners is for house washing on properties that have gone a year or two without attention and are starting to show it. Green algae creeping up from the foundation line, gray mildew streaks running down the north-facing gable ends, and black biological staining on soffits and window frames — all of it common, all of it worse than usual here because of the shade load. Mature oaks and maples around the university and throughout the older residential blocks keep moisture on siding surfaces longer than open-lot properties in newer developments. That moisture time is exactly what algae and mildew need to establish.

For vinyl siding — which covers a large percentage of the homes on the residential streets south and west of campus — I use soft washing. Low pressure, biodegradable cleaning solution, thorough rinse. It kills the biological growth at the root rather than blasting the surface discoloration off temporarily. High-pressure washing on vinyl forces water behind panels, cracks caulk around window and door frames, and doesn't actually address what's causing the staining. The results from soft washing last significantly longer, and there's no surface damage.

For the older homes closer to campus with painted wood siding, aluminum, or brick, I dial back the pressure further and adjust the chemistry accordingly. Painted wood surfaces especially need careful handling — too much pressure raises the grain and lifts paint edges. I'd rather take a little longer on the job than create a repair situation for the homeowner.

Driveway and Concrete Cleaning in Millersville

A lot of Millersville's older housing stock has concrete that's been through forty or fifty Pennsylvania winters. The freeze-thaw cycling that comes with every Lancaster County winter opens concrete pores progressively over time, and those opened surfaces collect oil, algae, and embedded grime that straightforward washing doesn't fully address. Oil staining on driveway aprons and parking pads requires pre-treatment with a commercial degreaser — we apply it, let it work into the contamination, then wash. Skipping that step just redistributes the staining into the rinse water rather than removing it.

We use a commercial rotary surface cleaner on flat concrete. It produces even coverage across the full surface width without the zebra-stripe pattern you get from sweeping a single wand. For older, heavily weathered concrete, the results are noticeable — you won't get it looking like new, but the removal of biological staining and embedded surface dirt is substantial and makes a real difference in curb appeal.

Deck and Fence Washing in Millersville

Decks on Millersville properties deal with a significant pollen load from the university's campus trees and the heavy residential canopy throughout the borough. A deck under a large oak accumulates debris in the board gaps and can go from clean to visibly weathered and stained in a single growing season. If you're planning to restain or reseal the wood, cleaning comes first — stain applied over algae, oxidized wood fiber, or mildew residue bonds poorly and fails early. We see plenty of restain jobs that look worse than expected three months in because the surface wasn't properly prepped beforehand.

We match the pressure to the decking material. Pressure-treated pine and cedar get lower PSI to avoid raising the grain. Composite decking gets handled more carefully still — high-pressure washing damages composite surfaces and voids manufacturer warranties. Once we know what you have, we set up accordingly.

Roof Soft Washing in Millersville

The dark streaking running down roof slopes throughout Millersville is Gloeocapsa magma — a cyanobacterium that feeds on the limestone filler in asphalt shingles. It's present on roofs all over Lancaster County, but it establishes faster on homes with heavy tree canopy because the shade keeps the shingle surface damp longer. If your home sits under mature oaks or maples and hasn't had the roof cleaned, there's a solid chance those streaks are already forming even if you haven't noticed them from the ground yet.

Soft washing is the correct approach: low-pressure application of a specialized chemical solution that kills the organism at the cellular level. The dead material washes off over the following weeks of rainfall. Pressure washing a roof removes the visible staining but doesn't kill the growth, damages shingle granules, and voids most manufacturer warranties. We soft wash only — no exceptions on roof work.
