Concrete Cleaning Cost in Lancaster PA — 2026 Pricing Guide

Updated May 2026 • 6 min read • Lancaster Power Washing Pros

Concrete surfaces — driveways, sidewalks, patios, and parking lots — take a beating in Lancaster County. Between road salt tracked in all winter, spring pollen and algae growth, summer barbecue grease, and general vehicle oil drips, most Lancaster PA concrete needs a proper cleaning at least once a year to stay safe, functional, and presentable. The question most homeowners and property managers want answered is simple: what does it actually cost?

Concrete cleaning in Lancaster PA runs $80 to $200 for a standard residential driveway and $0.15 to $0.35 per square foot for commercial surfaces. Here is the breakdown of what drives the price and what results you can realistically expect.

What Determines Concrete Cleaning Cost in Lancaster PA

Concrete cleaning is not a flat-rate service across all surfaces. Several variables shape the final price:

  • Square footage. The primary driver. A 500-square-foot driveway takes significantly less time than a 1,200-square-foot driveway with a turnaround area. Commercial parking lots are quoted by the square foot.
  • Surface condition. Standard dirt, moss, and algae clean up quickly. Oil stains, rust stains, paint, and deeply embedded organic growth require pre-treatment with specialty degreasers or chemical cleaners — these add time and cost to the base service.
  • Concrete type and age. Stamped concrete and exposed aggregate require lower pressure and more careful technique than standard broom-finish concrete. Older, porous concrete that hasn't been sealed tends to hold stains more aggressively and may need two passes.
  • Access. A straight, open driveway is the easiest situation. Tight side yards, decorative edging that needs protection, or surfaces surrounded by planters and furniture add setup time.
  • Add-on services. Sealing after cleaning, oil stain treatment, or rust removal are common add-ons that Lancaster PA homeowners combine with the cleaning to extend results.

Oil Stain Removal on Lancaster PA Driveways

Oil stains are the most common problem on Lancaster County residential driveways, and they are also the most misunderstood from a cleaning expectation standpoint. A fresh oil drip from a weekend ago can often be cleaned to near-invisible with a hot-water pressure wash and degreaser treatment. An oil stain that has been there for five years has penetrated deep into the concrete pores and may only lighten — not fully disappear — regardless of what cleaning technique is applied.

We are upfront about this at the estimate stage. We will tell you what result is realistic for your specific stains so you are not paying for cleaning expecting a result that is not possible without concrete grinding or replacement.

Concrete Cleaning vs. Concrete Sealing in Lancaster PA

These two services are often paired but serve different purposes. Cleaning removes what is on the surface. Sealing protects the surface going forward. A sealed driveway repels oil, water, and road salt instead of absorbing them — which means the next cleaning is faster and easier, and the concrete lasts significantly longer.

Sealing typically adds $0.25 to $0.60 per square foot to the cleaning price, which on a standard driveway means an additional $100 to $300. For most Lancaster PA concrete surfaces that are in decent structural shape, sealing is worth the investment. For concrete that is heavily cracked, heaved, or deteriorating, sealing cosmetically without addressing the structural issue first is money not well spent — and we will tell you that honestly.

Can pressure washing remove oil stains from concrete in Lancaster PA?
Pressure washing alone will lighten oil stains but rarely removes them completely. Effective removal requires a degreaser pre-treatment applied before pressure washing. Deep-set stains may need two treatment cycles. We use commercial-grade degreasers that outperform hardware store products.
How often should I clean my concrete in Lancaster PA?
Most residential driveways in Lancaster County benefit from cleaning every one to two years. Properties with heavy traffic or tree overhang may need annual cleaning. Commercial concrete typically needs two to four cleanings per year to maintain appearance and safety.
Should I seal my concrete after cleaning in Lancaster PA?

Sealing after cleaning is strongly recommended. A quality sealer prevents oil, water, and road salt from penetrating the concrete, makes future cleaning easier, and extends surface life. We offer sealing as an add-on service.
