Soft Washing vs. High-Pressure Washing

Not every surface should be blasted with high pressure. Over the years, I've learned that Leola properties often include delicate siding, older brick, and well-installed vinyl that demand a smarter approach.

Soft washing uses low pressure (50–200 PSI) combined with biodegradable cleaning solutions to safely remove algae, mold, and grime without risk of damage. This method is ideal for vinyl siding, painted wood, stucco, and older stone.

High-pressure washing (2,500–4,000 PSI) is reserved for concrete, asphalt, brick, and heavily soiled commercial surfaces. I use high pressure strategically and carefully to avoid etching, splitting, or gouging.

When I visit your Leola home, I assess each surface and recommend the safest, most effective method. Your property's condition is my priority.

Leola's Unique Cleaning Challenges

After years of working in Leola, I understand the specific issues this area faces:

Seasonal humidity: Spring and fall bring fog and dew that speed up algae growth, especially on wood and older concrete.

Professional cleaning every 1–2 years keeps these challenges in check and protects your property's value and appearance.
