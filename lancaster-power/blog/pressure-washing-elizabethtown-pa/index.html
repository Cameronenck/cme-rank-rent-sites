Deck and Fence Washing in Elizabethtown

Wood decks need pressure washing before any staining or sealing, and most benefit from at least annual cleaning just to stay ahead of mildew and gray weathering. We adjust pressure based on wood species — softer woods like pine need lower pressure to avoid raising the grain. Composite decking gets even lower pressure. The goal is getting the surface clean without leaving fuzz or texture damage that shows up later when you apply a finish.

Vinyl fencing cleans up fast and looks dramatically better after washing — most of the time it's a 15-minute job bundled with a house wash at minimal extra cost. Wood privacy fencing takes longer but the difference is worth it, especially if you're planning to restain it.

Roof Soft Washing in Elizabethtown

The black streaks you see on roofs throughout Lancaster County are almost always Gloeocapsa magma — a cyanobacterium that feeds on the limestone filler in asphalt shingles. Pressure washing a roof is a bad idea; it strips granules and voids manufacturer warranties. The right method is soft washing — a low-pressure chemical application that kills the algae and lets it rinse away with rain over the following weeks. Results last three to five years versus the streaks coming back the following season with a pressure-only approach.
