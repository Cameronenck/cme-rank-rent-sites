What Pressure Washing Does (And Why Your New Holland Home Needs It)

Pressure washing isn't just about making your home look clean — though it absolutely does that. It removes the buildup that can actually damage your property over time.

Algae and mold don't just look bad. They retain moisture, which accelerates wood rot, concrete deterioration, and paint peeling. On vinyl siding, mold can weaken seals and let water penetrate behind panels. On driveways, algae makes concrete slippery and contributes to surface breakdown. Gutter buildup traps water, damaging fascia boards and causing foundation issues.

Professional pressure washing in New Holland removes all of that — restoring your home's appearance and preventing costly damage down the road.

Pressure Washing Vs. Soft Washing: Which Does Your New Holland Home Need?

Not all surfaces can handle high pressure. I adjust my approach based on what needs cleaning.

High-pressure washing (2500+ PSI) works great for driveways, concrete, and hardy surfaces. It blasts away stubborn stains and grime quickly.

Soft washing (below 500 PSI) uses lower pressure combined with eco-friendly cleaning solutions. This is what I use on roofs, vinyl siding, painted wood, and delicate surfaces where high pressure would cause damage.

Many New Holland homes have a mix — your driveway might need high pressure, while your roof and siding need soft washing. I assess each surface and use the right technique.

How Often Should You Pressure Wash Your New Holland Property?

It depends on your specific situation, but here's what I typically recommend:

  • Annual: Properties with heavy tree coverage, north-facing surfaces, or moisture issues. These areas grow algae and mold faster.
  • Every 2-3 years: Most New Holland homes fall here. One good cleaning every other year keeps your property in top shape.
  • As-needed: After storms, after fall leaves and debris, or when you notice visible staining.

I recommend getting your driveways and concrete surfaces cleaned every 1-2 years. These get stained and slippery faster, especially in our humid Lancaster County climate.

Why Hire a Professional for Pressure Washing in New Holland?

Sure, you can rent a pressure washer from a hardware store. But here's what you're risking:

  • Property damage: High pressure without experience can strip paint, puncture siding, and damage wood. I've seen homeowners accidentally cause thousands in damage with a rental unit.

  • Time and hassle: Equipment rental, setup, learning the technique, disposing of the water — it's a full day of work.

I bring professional equipment, years of experience knowing which technique works on each surface, eco-friendly cleaning solutions, and insurance. You get a better result, faster, and safer.
