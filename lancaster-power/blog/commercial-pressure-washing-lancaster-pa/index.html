What Commercial Pressure Washing Covers

Commercial pressure washing is not a single service -- it is a category of exterior cleaning tailored to the scale and surface demands of business properties. Lancaster Power Washing Pros handles the full range:

  • Storefronts and facades -- brick, stucco, EIFS, glass canopies, awning frames, and sidewalks in front of retail locations
  • Parking lots and parking structures -- oil stains, tire marks, gum, and general ground-in dirt across large paved areas
  • Warehouses and industrial buildings -- exterior walls, loading aprons, dock doors, and compactor pads that accumulate heavy contamination
  • Restaurants and food service exteriors -- grease buildup around exhaust vents, dumpster pads, and drive-through lanes where health code compliance matters
  • Fleet vehicle washing -- box trucks, vans, trailers, and equipment that need regular cleaning to maintain DOT appearance standards and protect paint

Each surface type requires different pressure settings, temperatures, and chemical treatments. That is the core reason commercial work demands professional equipment rather than a rented consumer unit.

Commercial vs. Residential: Why It Is Not the Same Job

Homeowners sometimes wonder if their neighbor's driveway cleaning crew can handle a commercial job. The answer is usually no, and here is why:

Commercial surfaces are larger, dirtier, and often contaminated with substances -- motor oil, grease, chemical spills -- that require hot water extraction and professional-grade detergents. A residential soft-wash rig running 3 GPM cannot cover a 40,000-square-foot parking lot in any practical timeframe. Commercial equipment typically runs 8 to 12 GPM with heated water and surface cleaners that are several feet wide.

Liability is another factor. Commercial jobs often require proof of insurance, sometimes at $1 million or $2 million general liability. Many residential operators carry far less. Lancaster Power Washing Pros is fully insured for commercial work throughout Lancaster County.

Commercial Pressure Washing Pricing in Lancaster PA

Commercial pricing varies more than residential because job scope, surface condition, access requirements, and frequency agreements all affect the final number. Most commercial work is quoted per square foot for large flat surfaces or as a flat project rate for facades and specialty cleaning.

Storefront facade cleaning $150 - $400 per visit Varies by linear footage and surface material
Parking lot (flat surface) $0.05 - $0.12 per sq ft Lower end for maintenance cleans; higher for heavy oil/stain removal
Loading dock / compactor pad $200 - $600 per area Grease and chemical contamination adds cost
Fleet vehicle washing (per unit) $40 - $150 per vehicle Depends on vehicle size; bulk discounts available for 5+ units
Restaurant exterior / dumpster pad $175 - $500 per visit Grease treatment and degreaser application typically included
Warehouse exterior walls $0.08 - $0.18 per sq ft Metal panel, concrete block, and painted surfaces each vary

Ongoing service agreements typically reduce per-visit rates by 10 to 20 percent compared to one-time jobs. For businesses that need monthly or quarterly cleaning, locking in a contract rate makes financial sense.

Business Benefits Beyond Appearance

Clean exteriors generate real, measurable business returns -- not just aesthetic ones.

Slip and Fall Liability Reduction

Algae, mold, and grease create dangerously slick surfaces on sidewalks, loading docks, and drive-through lanes. Regular pressure washing removes these hazards and can reduce premises liability exposure. Some commercial property insurers look favorably on documented maintenance programs.

Compliance and Code Requirements

Restaurants and food processing facilities in Lancaster County are subject to health inspections that include exterior areas, dumpster enclosures, and grease trap surrounds. A cleaning schedule with documented service dates demonstrates proactive maintenance to inspectors.

Property Value and Tenant Retention

Commercial landlords who maintain clean exteriors command higher rents and retain tenants longer. Tenants notice when common areas, parking lots, and building facades are kept up -- and they notice when they are not.

Equipment and Surface Longevity

Grease, road salts, and biological growth accelerate surface degradation. Regular cleaning removes these corrosive agents and extends the life of pavement sealers, painted surfaces, and masonry -- reducing long-term capital expenditure.

How Often Does Your Business Need Commercial Pressure Washing?

Frequency depends on traffic volume, surface type, and the nature of your business. General guidelines for Lancaster-area businesses:

  • Restaurants and food service: Monthly or quarterly for dumpster pads and grease-prone areas; semi-annual for facades
  • Retail storefronts in high foot-traffic areas: Quarterly minimum; bi-monthly for locations on King Street and Penn Square
  • Warehouses and light industrial: Semi-annual for exterior walls; quarterly for loading aprons
  • Office parks and professional buildings: Annual or semi-annual, timed around spring and fall
  • Fleet vehicles: Monthly or on a rolling cycle depending on vehicle count and route conditions
