Soft Washing vs. Pressure Washing: What's the Difference?

This is the question I get most often, so let me explain.

Pressure Washing uses high-pressure water jets (2,500–4,000 PSI) to blast away dirt, grime, and buildup. It's aggressive and fast. Perfect for concrete driveways, sidewalks, and brick surfaces that can handle the force.

Soft Washing uses lower pressure (500–1,000 PSI) combined with biodegradable cleaning solutions to gently remove algae, mold, and mildew. It's safer for vinyl siding, painted wood, roofs, and delicate surfaces. The solutions do the heavy lifting—the water just rinses.

For most Akron homes with vinyl or wood siding, I recommend soft washing. It's gentler, lasts longer, and won't leave pressure marks or damage. For driveways and concrete? Full pressure washing makes sense.

Akron's Unique Cleaning Challenges

Living in Akron, you face some specific weather and environmental factors that affect how often you need professional cleaning.

Humidity & Mold: Lancaster County's humid summers create perfect conditions for algae and mold. North-facing walls and shaded areas are the worst. I usually recommend annual soft washing for these spots.

Tree Coverage: Akron's tree-lined streets are beautiful, but trees drop pollen, seeds, and moisture on your roof, gutters, and exterior surfaces. If your property is surrounded by mature trees, plan for more frequent cleaning.

Road Dust & Salt Spray: Properties near Route 272 accumulate road dust and residue faster. Winter salt spray also builds up on lower siding and foundation areas.

Clay Soil: Our local clay soil holds moisture, which promotes mold and mildew growth. Deck staining and foundation mold are common issues I address regularly.

Seasonal Pattern: Spring (after pollen season) and fall (after leaf drop and summer growth) are the best times to clean. Winter is the worst—wet, cold, and unsafe conditions. Summer works fine, though high temps can speed up algae return.
