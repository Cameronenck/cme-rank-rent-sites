House Washing in Ephrata

The most common call we get in Ephrata is house washing — specifically dealing with the green and black biological staining that builds up on siding over time. Algae, mildew, and mold are the main culprits. North-facing walls, spots under tree canopy, and areas behind downspouts are usually the worst. Left alone, that growth holds moisture against the surface and gradually breaks down paint adhesion, caulk, and even the siding material itself.

We soft wash siding in Ephrata instead of using high pressure. The soft wash process involves applying a biodegradable cleaning solution at low pressure — the solution does the work by killing the biological growth at the root. After a dwell period, we rinse everything clean. The result lasts significantly longer than a straight pressure wash because you're eliminating the organism, not just scraping the surface. In six months, a pressure-washed surface that wasn't treated will show regrowth. A properly soft-washed surface typically stays clean for two to three years.

For older Ephrata borough homes with wood trim or original painted brick, soft washing is the only responsible approach. High pressure blasted against those surfaces forces water into gaps, strips paint, and can crack aging mortar. We don't take shortcuts that cause damage.

A typical Ephrata house wash runs $150–$400 depending on size and story count. Most single-story ranches come in on the lower end; two-story colonials with attached garages run higher. We give free on-site estimates — call 717-807-7599 and we'll schedule a time to take a look.

Driveway and Concrete Cleaning

Concrete driveways in Ephrata take a beating. Winter road salt migrates in on tire treads. Oil drips accumulate near the garage door. Tree debris and organic matter build up in the textured surface over years and turn into embedded black staining. By spring, most driveways that haven't been cleaned in a few seasons look like a different color than when they were poured.

We use surface cleaner attachments on all concrete work — a spinning nozzle head that keeps consistent pressure across the full width of each pass. This eliminates the zebra-stripe patterns you see when someone uses a handheld wand on concrete. The result is an even clean from edge to edge. For heavily stained concrete, we apply a pre-treatment to loosen embedded grime before the wash begins. On older concrete that's become porous, we can also apply a sealer after cleaning to protect the surface and make future cleanings easier.

Driveway cleaning in Ephrata typically runs $80–$200 depending on square footage. We also clean walkways, patios, pool surrounds, and parking areas at the same rate.

Roof Soft Washing in Ephrata

If you've got black streaks running down your roof, that's algae — specifically Gloeocapsa magma. It spreads from spore contact, feeds on the limestone filler in asphalt shingles, and works its way down from the ridge over time. It's most visible on north and west-facing slopes where the surface stays damp longer and gets less UV exposure to inhibit growth.

You cannot pressure wash a roof. The pressure strips the granule coating off asphalt shingles, shortens the life of the roof by years, and voids most manufacturer warranties. The correct treatment is soft washing — a low-pressure biocide application that kills the algae without touching the surface aggressively. The staining doesn't disappear overnight; it fades over the next few weeks as rain washes away the dead organisms. After a proper soft wash treatment, most roofs stay clean for three to five years.

We carry full liability insurance and are comfortable working on pitched roofs in Ephrata — ask us for a certificate of insurance before hiring any contractor for roof work. Roof cleaning in Ephrata runs $300–$600 for a typical single-family home.

Deck and Fence Cleaning

Wood decks in Ephrata go through real weathering cycles. Hot summers, wet springs, and hard winters leave them gray and mildewed. The gaps between boards fill with organic debris. Algae makes the surface slippery. And if you want to re-stain or seal the deck, cleaning has to happen first — stain applied over contaminated wood peels within a year.

We wash decks with a wood-safe detergent at controlled, moderate pressure. For decks that have never been treated or cleaned, we often apply a wood brightener after washing to restore the natural tone before refinishing. Deck washing in Ephrata runs $100–$300 depending on size and condition. Vinyl fencing, wood fencing, and composite decking all get the same careful treatment.
