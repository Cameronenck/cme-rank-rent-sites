Deck Size and Material Affects the Price

The two biggest cost drivers are square footage and what your deck is made of. Here is what each factor means for your quote:

  • Wood decks (pressure-treated, cedar, redwood): These can handle higher PSI washing but often benefit from a brightener treatment afterward to neutralize the pH and open the grain before staining. Wood decks are the most common type we service in older Lancaster neighborhoods.
  • Composite decks (Trex, TimberTech, Azek): These require a soft wash approach. High-pressure washing can scuff, etch, or void the manufacturer warranty. Soft washing is typically a bit more expensive per square foot but produces better results and protects your investment.
  • Deck size: Most companies price by square footage or use tiered brackets (under 200 sq ft, 200 to 400, 400 to 600, etc.). Larger decks cost more in absolute terms but often less per square foot as the setup cost is spread across more surface area.
  • Stain buildup and mold severity: A deck that has been neglected for three or four seasons may need a pre-treatment soak and multiple passes, which adds time and cost. If the mold has worked its way into wood grain, a brightener treatment is almost always necessary.

What Is Included in a Professional Deck Cleaning?

When you hire Lancaster Power Washing Pros, a standard deck cleaning service includes:

  • Pre-rinse to remove loose debris and surface dirt
  • Application of appropriate cleaning solution (mildewcide for wood, pH-neutral cleaner for composite)
  • Pressure or soft wash based on deck material and condition
  • Railing and spindle cleaning (included at no extra charge)
  • Post-wash rinse and visual inspection

We do not charge extra for moving patio furniture if it is already cleared to the side. If you want us to move and replace a full outdoor set, we ask that you let us know when booking so we can factor that into the quote.

How to Choose a Deck Cleaning Company in Lancaster PA

Lancaster County has no shortage of power washing companies, and pricing varies widely. Here is what to look for before you book:

  • Ask about their method for your deck material. If they quote a composite deck and never mention soft washing, that is a red flag. High-pressure washing a Trex deck can cause irreversible surface damage.
  • Check for local references. A crew that knows Lancaster County homes understands that older Craftsman-era wood decks need a gentler touch than newer pressure-treated builds. Ask if they have done decks in your neighborhood or town.
  • Verify they carry liability insurance. Pressure washing at height or adjacent to landscaping and windows carries real risk. Any legitimate contractor should be able to provide a certificate of insurance.
  • Get the quote in writing. Know exactly what is and is not included. Some companies quote deck boards only and charge extra for railings, stairs, and lattice.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does deck cleaning cost in Lancaster PA?
Deck cleaning in Lancaster PA typically costs between $150 and $600 depending on the size of the deck, the material, and how much stain or mildew buildup is present. A standard 400-square-foot wood deck usually runs $200 to $350 for a full pressure wash and rinse.
What is the difference between pressure washing and soft washing a deck?
Pressure washing uses high-pressure water to blast away dirt and grime and works well on sturdy wood decks. Soft washing uses lower pressure combined with a cleaning solution, which is safer for composite and Trex decks and more effective at killing mold and algae at the root rather than just blasting it off the surface.
How often should I have my deck cleaned in Lancaster PA?
We recommend cleaning your deck once a year, ideally in the spring before patio season. Lancaster County gets significant rainfall and humidity from spring through fall, which accelerates mold, algae, and mildew growth on wood and composite surfaces. If your deck sees heavy use or is shaded, twice a year may be warranted.
Can deck cleaning damage my wood or composite deck?
High-pressure washing at the wrong PSI can absolutely damage wood grain, strip stain, or etch composite boards. That is why we use the appropriate technique for each material. Composite and Trex decks require a soft wash approach, while traditional pressure-treated wood can handle higher pressure with the right nozzle and technique.
Should I seal or stain my deck after cleaning?
Yes, if your deck is wood. After cleaning, a brightener treatment opens the wood grain so stain or sealer penetrates more evenly. We recommend staining or sealing every two to three years on untreated wood decks in Lancaster PA. A clean, well-sealed deck lasts significantly longer and resists rot and UV damage better.
