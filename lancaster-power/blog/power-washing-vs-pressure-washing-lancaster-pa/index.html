Power Washing vs Pressure Washing in Lancaster PA: Which Method Is Right for Your Home?

Published June 20, 2026 | Lancaster Power Washing Pros

People throw around "power washing" and "pressure washing" like they're the same thing. They're not. And the difference matters — a lot. Use the wrong method on the wrong surface, and you can damage your home. Use the right one, and your property will look incredible for years.

I've been cleaning homes and businesses across Lancaster County for years. I see the damage that comes from mixing these up, and I see the results when someone finally calls with the right approach. Let me break down what each one is, when to use it, and why it matters for your specific property.

The Core Difference: Heat and Pressure

Here's the simple version:

Pressure washing uses high-pressure cold water — typically 2,500 to 4,000 PSI (pounds per square inch) — to blast dirt, algae, and buildup off surfaces. It's pure mechanical force. The water is cold because that's all you need for most dirt removal.

Power washing uses the same high pressure, but heats the water to 130–180°F before spraying it. That heat changes the game. Hot water dissolves grease, breaks down mold more effectively, and sanitizes the surface. On some jobs, the heat does most of the work; the pressure just helps rinse it away.

In practical terms: pressure washing is best for loose dirt and debris on hard surfaces. Power washing tackles stubborn buildup — grease, heavy mold, and staining that cold water can't budge alone.

When to Use Pressure Washing

Pressure washing with cold water is your answer for:

  • Concrete driveways — High pressure with a surface cleaner attachment is perfect for concrete. In Lancaster, where we get a lot of snow and road salt, driveways take a beating. Pressure washing removes salt, dirt, and some of the discoloration. Cost is typically $80–$150 for a standard two-car driveway.
  • Sidewalks and patios — Same logic. Hard surfaces can handle the pressure, and cold water is all you need for general cleaning.
  • Brick and stone — If the mortar is solid and the surface is in good condition, pressure washing is safe and effective on brick and stone. We're careful with pressure levels on older homes in Lancaster.
  • Loose dirt and pollen — If your deck, siding, or house just needs a general wash of accumulated dust and pollen — not mold or algae — pressure washing does it quickly and affordably.

When to Use Power Washing

Power washing — the heated option — makes sense for:

  • Grease stains — Restaurant patios, commercial kitchen exhaust areas, areas with heavy oil buildup. Cold water won't cut through it. Heat does.
  • Heavy mold and mildew — If your property has thick black mold or stubborn mildew, heat accelerates the kill. We often combine power washing with a biodegradable treatment for best results.
  • Commercial equipment — Loading docks, machinery, industrial surfaces that accumulate years of grime.

But — and this is important — most residential jobs in Lancaster don't actually need power washing. They need soft washing, which is different from both.

The Real Winner for Most Homes: Soft Washing

Here's what I tell every homeowner who asks about power or pressure washing: for your house, deck, and roof, neither of the above is what you want. You want soft washing.

Soft washing uses low pressure (150–500 PSI) combined with biodegradable cleaning solutions. The chemical does the heavy lifting; the low pressure just rinses it away gently. This method is what professionals actually use on homes across Lancaster because it works and it's safe.

For vinyl siding, soft washing prevents water from being forced behind panels. For roofs, it kills the algae and bacteria (usually Gloeocapsa magma — the black streaks you see) without stripping shingle granules. For wood decks, it eliminates the problem at the root instead of just blasting the surface.

Why This Matters in Lancaster County

Lancaster has a specific climate. We're humid. Summer runs long. We get snow, ice, and road salt in winter. All that means homes here accumulate algae and mold faster than drier climates. A roof that looks fine in April can have visible black streaks by August. Decks green up by mid-summer.

When you're fighting that kind of growth, soft washing wins. The biodegradable solutions kill the organism at the root. The low pressure means zero risk to your siding or shingles. The results last 3–5 times longer than pure pressure washing.

That's why we soft wash almost everything on Lancaster homes, and we pressure wash only the surfaces designed to handle it — concrete, well-maintained brick, stone.

One More Thing: Don't DIY This on Your Roof

I mention this because I see damage from it constantly. High-pressure washing a roof — whether cold or hot — tears granules off shingles, voids your warranty, and can actually shorten your roof's life by years. Roofs need soft washing. Professional soft washing.

Bottom line: Pressure washing works great for concrete and hard surfaces. Power washing handles grease better than cold water. But for your house, deck, and roof, soft washing is the right answer — it works, it's safe, and the results last. When in doubt, ask. We give free estimates and we'll recommend the method that's actually right for your property.
