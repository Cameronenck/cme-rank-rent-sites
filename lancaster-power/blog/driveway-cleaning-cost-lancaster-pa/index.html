Driveways in Lancaster County take a beating. Road salt from winter, algae and mold from humid PA summers, oil drips, and just general traffic grime accumulate in ways that pressure washing addresses effectively. Here's what driveway cleaning actually costs in Lancaster and what drives those numbers.

Driveway Cleaning Cost in Lancaster PA

For most residential driveways in Lancaster County, expect to pay:

  • Single-car driveway (approx. 200–300 sq ft): $80–$175
  • Two-car driveway (approx. 400–600 sq ft): $120–$280
  • Large or long driveway (600–1,200 sq ft+): $200–$450
  • Concrete with staining or heavy buildup: Add $50–$150 for pre-treatment

These are stand-alone driveway cleaning prices. Most homeowners in Lancaster bundle driveway cleaning with house washing or patio/walkway cleaning — doing multiple surfaces in one visit typically reduces the per-surface cost by 15–25%.

What Affects Driveway Cleaning Prices

Surface type matters. Concrete, asphalt, paver, and exposed aggregate surfaces all have different cleaning requirements. Concrete takes higher pressure. Pavers need careful technique to avoid sand displacement. Asphalt needs lower pressure to avoid surface damage. A contractor who uses the same settings on everything is cutting corners.

Condition and staining. Standard traffic grime and algae come off with straightforward pressure washing. Oil stains, rust stains from iron minerals in PA water, and heavy mold growth require pre-treatment with the right cleaning chemistry before washing. This adds time and material cost but makes the difference between actually clean and just wet.

Driveway length and slope. Long driveways and steep grades take longer. Properties where the truck can't get close to the work area require longer hose runs and more setup time.

Post-wash treatment. Some homeowners add a concrete sealer after pressure washing. Sealing extends the time between cleanings and protects the surface from salt and stain penetration. This is a separate service that adds $100–$300 depending on driveway size but is worth considering if you haven't sealed recently.

Concrete vs. Asphalt: What You Need to Know

Concrete driveways are the most common in Lancaster County and respond well to pressure washing. The main issue is efflorescence — white mineral deposits — and organic growth in the winter-to-spring transition when moisture is high. Both come off with proper cleaning.

Asphalt driveways need more care. Too much pressure or the wrong nozzle angle damages the surface — you can actually pull aggregate out of older asphalt. We use lower pressure and controlled technique on asphalt. If your asphalt has significant cracking or exposed aggregate, we'll flag that before we clean so there are no disputes about pre-existing damage.

Paver Driveways

Paver driveways are common in newer Lancaster County construction. They look great when clean but the joints between pavers need attention. Pressure washing removes the polymeric sand in the joints if done incorrectly. We use surface cleaner equipment (a spinning, enclosed nozzle) that provides even cleaning without blasting the joints out. After cleaning, re-sanding the joints with polymeric sand keeps them locked and resistant to weed growth — that's an additional cost but often worth doing after a thorough cleaning.

How Often Should You Clean Your Driveway in Lancaster?

Annual or bi-annual cleaning is the right cycle for most Lancaster County properties. PA winters with road salt create significant buildup, and the humid summers accelerate algae and mold growth. Properties surrounded by trees or in shaded areas may need more frequent attention. Letting algae and mold go for multiple seasons makes it harder to clean and can cause surface degradation on concrete.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to pressure wash a driveway in Lancaster PA?
Most residential driveways in Lancaster run $80–$280 depending on size. A standard two-car driveway (400–500 sq ft) is typically $120–$200. Driveways with heavy staining or oil spots may need pre-treatment which adds to the cost.
How long does it take to pressure wash a driveway?
A standard two-car driveway takes 45–90 minutes including setup and cleanup. Larger driveways or those with significant staining take longer. Most residential driveway cleanings are done in under two hours.
Will pressure washing remove oil stains from my Lancaster driveway?
It depends on the age and depth of the stain. Fresh oil stains respond well to degreaser pre-treatment followed by pressure washing. Old, set-in oil stains may lighten but won't disappear completely. We'll give you an honest assessment of what's achievable before we start.
Should I seal my driveway after pressure washing in Lancaster PA?
For concrete driveways, sealing after cleaning is a good investment — it extends the time between cleanings and helps prevent road salt penetration. Most concrete sealers in the Lancaster area last 2–3 years. We can apply sealer the same day after the surface dries, or schedule it as a follow-up.
Do you clean paver driveways in Lancaster County?
Yes. We use surface cleaner equipment on pavers to avoid blasting out the joint sand. If the joints need re-sanding after cleaning, we offer polymeric sand re-filling as part of a complete paver restoration service.
