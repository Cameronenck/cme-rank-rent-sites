What Actually Affects the Price

Contractors aren't making these numbers up out of thin air. A few things genuinely change what a driveway cleaning costs:

Square Footage

The biggest factor. More surface area means more time, more water, more fuel. A 250 square foot single-car slab takes about 45 minutes with a surface cleaner. A 700 square foot double-wide with an extended apron takes closer to two hours. The price reflects that labor difference directly.

Surface Condition

A driveway that got cleaned last spring needs a straightforward wash. One that hasn't been touched in five years — black from algae growth, green at the edges from moss, and permanently stained under the car — takes more passes, more pre-treatment, and more time. Heavy buildup means a higher price, no way around it.

Oil and Grease Stains

Regular concrete cleaning doesn't include aggressive degreasing by default. If you've got motor oil stains, hydraulic fluid, or old rust stains from cars parked in the same spot for years, that takes a separate degreaser application, dwell time, and additional rinse passes. We charge a bit more for that — usually $25 to $60 extra depending on how bad it is — and we'll tell you upfront what's realistic. Old, deep-set stains may lighten but not disappear completely.

Surface Type

Concrete is the most common driveway surface in Lancaster PA and handles high-pressure cleaning well at 2,500 to 3,500 PSI. Asphalt needs a lighter touch — too much pressure strips the aggregate and accelerates wear. Pavers require careful attention to the joints so you're not blasting out polymeric sand or mortar. Each surface type changes the approach, and that affects the price.

Accessibility

If there's a tight gate, significant landscaping along the edges, or a steep hill that slows the equipment down, the job takes longer. Straightforward open driveways with easy hose access are always the fastest to quote.

How We Actually Clean Driveways

I want to walk you through what a real professional driveway cleaning looks like — because not every contractor does it the same way, and the differences show up in the result.

We start by connecting to your exterior hose bib or bringing water from our tank if there's no spigot accessible. We wet down any plants or landscaping beds near the driveway edges before we start — the last thing I want to do is hit your flower beds with overspray. Then we do a surface cleaner pass, which is a spinning dual-nozzle attachment that cleans in a consistent overlapping pattern instead of leaving stripe marks or tiger-stripe lines across the concrete. That's a tell for an amateur who's just running a wand back and forth.

After the surface cleaner pass, we detail the edges with a turbo nozzle — that strip along the grass or beds that the surface cleaner can't quite reach. On paver driveways, we're careful with edge pressure because the joint material is vulnerable to direct high-pressure hits.

If there are oil stains, we apply degreaser first, let it dwell for 10 to 15 minutes, agitate if needed, then do our cleaning pass over the top. For staining that's years deep, we may do two passes.

When we're done, the driveway should look genuinely clean — not streaky, not half-washed. If there's something we couldn't get out, we'll tell you before we pack up.

Bundling Saves You Money

We're already there with the equipment running — adding another 200 square feet of concrete takes 20 minutes and costs a fraction of what you'd pay for a separate trip. Most Lancaster homeowners who call for a driveway end up getting the driveway plus the front walk for $140 to $180 total, which is strong value compared to separate jobs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does driveway cleaning cost in Lancaster PA?

Most residential driveways in Lancaster PA run $80–$200 for a professional power wash. A single-car concrete driveway in good condition is usually $80–$120. A double-wide or long two-car driveway runs $130–$200. Pavers cost a bit more — $150–$250 — because of the detailed edge work involved. Heavily stained or neglected driveways may cost more depending on condition.

How long does a driveway cleaning take?

A standard single-car concrete driveway takes about 45–60 minutes with a surface cleaner. A double-wide or long driveway runs 60–90 minutes. Add 15–20 minutes if there are heavy oil stains that need pre-treatment dwell time. We clean the edges with a turbo nozzle after the surface cleaner pass, so you don't end up with an untouched strip along the sides.

Can you get oil stains out of a concrete driveway?

Yes, in most cases. Fresh oil stains — anything less than a few months old — come out very well with a degreaser pre-treatment followed by hot or high-pressure washing. Old, deep-set stains from years of parking in the same spot may lighten significantly but not disappear completely. We'll tell you honestly what to expect before we start — no surprises on the result.

How often should I have my driveway cleaned?

For most Lancaster County driveways, once a year is the right call — typically in spring after the salt and sand from winter roads has had a chance to accumulate. If your driveway is under heavy tree cover or sits on the north side of the house where it stays damp, you may see algae and mildew building up faster and benefit from cleaning every spring without fail.

Is it worth sealing a driveway after power washing?

For concrete, sealing after a professional cleaning is a smart move — it protects against future oil penetration and makes the next cleaning easier. For asphalt, sealcoating after cleaning extends the surface life and blocks UV damage. Pavers also benefit from a polymeric sand reapplication and sealing after washing. We don't offer sealing ourselves, but we're happy to tell you which local contractors do good work.
