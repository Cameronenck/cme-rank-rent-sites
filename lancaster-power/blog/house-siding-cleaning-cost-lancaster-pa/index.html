House Siding Cleaning Cost in Lancaster PA

Published June 19, 2026 | Lancaster Power Washing Pros

Home / Blog / House Siding Cleaning Cost

If your vinyl siding is looking dull, streaked, or covered in green algae buildup, you're probably wondering: how much is this going to cost?

Here's the honest answer: siding cleaning in Lancaster County typically runs $150 to $500 depending on your home's size and how much buildup we're dealing with. But there's more to it than just square footage. I'm going to walk you through exactly what affects the price and what you should expect to pay.

What Most Lancaster Homeowners Pay

Over the last five years cleaning homes across Lancaster County, I've noticed the pricing breaks down pretty consistently:

Single-Story Ranch Home (1,200–1,600 sq ft)

$200–$350 — Most common in our market. This is your standard one-level home with light-to-moderate algae buildup.

Two-Story Colonial (2,000–2,800 sq ft)

$300–$500 — Takes longer to reach the upper level and more product for cleaning. Heavy buildup pushes toward the top.

Ranch with Heavy Shade (lots of trees)

$250–$400 — Algae and mold buildup is thicker from limited sunlight. Needs stronger treatment and more time.

All estimates include setup, cleanup, and a final rinse. We don't charge extra for gutter splash either—we protect them.

The Biggest Price Factor: How Dirty Is It Really?

The real variable isn't always your square footage. It's how much algae and mold is sitting on that siding.

I've cleaned homes that look terrible but only have surface-level dirt—they clean up fast at the lower end of the price range. I've also cleaned homes that don't look too bad but have thick algae embedded in the vinyl—those take longer and cost more.

During the estimate, I'll walk around the house and check:

  • Shading — North-facing walls and areas under tree coverage have more buildup because they stay damp. That's extra cleaning time.
  • Algae depth — If it's been three years since the last clean, the algae is thick. Recent cleaning? It'll be faster.
  • Siding condition — If the vinyl is already chalky or damaged, we use gentler soft wash instead of higher pressure. Gentle takes longer.
  • Dirt versus algae — Dirt rinses off. Algae needs treatment. Big difference in time.

Why Soft Wash Costs More (But Saves Your Home)

You might see cheaper quotes from crews offering "pressure washing your siding." Be careful. High-pressure water on vinyl siding damages it. Water gets driven behind the siding, the vinyl cracks, and now you're looking at thousands in repairs.

We use soft wash on siding—low pressure (under 1,000 PSI) with biodegradable cleaning solutions that kill algae at the root without harming your home. Yes, it takes longer than blasting it with high pressure. That's why it costs a bit more. But it's the right way.

Factors That Push the Price Up

Two-story height — We need lifts or extended ladders for safety. More equipment, more time.

Heavy tree coverage — More algae means more solution, more dwell time, more careful rinsing.

Large home — Linear feet of siding matters. A 3,000 sq ft home takes noticeably longer than a 1,500 sq ft ranch.

Damaged or delicate siding — If the vinyl is old and brittle, we go slower. Takes longer but protects your investment.

Obstacles — Fences, gardens, AC units, cars in the driveway—anything that limits access adds time.

Factors That Keep the Price Down

Light buildup — If you've cleaned every couple years, the algae is thin. Rinses off faster.

Open layout — No obstacles, no tricky angles. We work faster.

Single story — Quicker to work, safer ladder placement, less equipment.

Recent clean — The fresher the last cleaning, the easier the next one.

Bundling Saves Money

If you're getting your siding cleaned anyway, consider adding your driveway, deck, or roof to the same visit. We can often bundle these services at a discount because we're already on-site with equipment. Most customers save 15–25% by bundling.

And honestly, if your driveway and siding are both dirty, you might as well do them together. One trip, one setup, one crew.

How Often Should You Clean?

If you're thinking about the cost, you're also thinking about how often you need to do this. Fair question.

Most Lancaster County homes look best with cleaning every 2–3 years. Homes in shaded areas with heavy tree coverage should go every 1–2 years to prevent thick algae buildup.

Regular cleaning costs less than emergency cleaning. A home that's been neglected for 5+ years costs more to clean than one that's been kept up. Build it into your maintenance budget.

Why Not Just DIY It?

I get asked this a lot. Yes, you can rent a pressure washer and do it yourself. You'll spend $50–$80 on the rental, buy cleaning solution, spend a full Saturday or two on the ladder, risk damaging your siding if the pressure is too high, and hope you rinse enough so water doesn't sit behind the vinyl.

Or call us, we handle it safely and thoroughly, and you get to spend your weekend doing something else. For most homeowners, the peace of mind is worth the cost.
