How Often Should You Power Wash Your House in Lancaster PA?

Published May 2026 • 6 min read • By Lancaster Power Washing Pros

One of the first questions homeowners ask me when I show up for an estimate is some version of "should I have called sooner?" Usually, the answer is yes. But the follow-up question — how often should you actually be washing your house — is one I get almost every job, and the honest answer is: it depends on the surface and where in Lancaster County you live.

I've been doing this work across Lancaster, York, and the surrounding communities for years. Here's how I think about wash frequency, broken down by surface, and why Lancaster's climate makes the timing matter more than most homeowners realize.

Why Lancaster PA Is Harder on Home Exteriors Than Most Places

Lancaster County has a genuinely tough climate for home surfaces. Humid summers, significant tree canopy in older neighborhoods like Manheim Township, Lititz, and Millersville, and north-facing walls that see almost no direct sun — these conditions accelerate the growth of algae, mold, and the black-streaking bacteria (Gloeocapsa magma) that show up on roofs and siding across the county every summer.

Add in Lancaster's heavy spring pollen load, the proximity to agricultural fields that push dust and organic particulate into the air, and the freeze-thaw cycles that grind grime into porous surfaces every winter — and you've got conditions that create real buildup faster than most Mid-Atlantic areas. That's not a scare tactic; it's just why most Lancaster homes that I haven't touched in 2+ years show visible algae or mold on at least one wall.

Recommended Wash Frequency by Surface

Different surfaces have different cycles. Here's what I actually recommend to homeowners across Lancaster County:

Surface Recommended Frequency Notes for Lancaster
Vinyl or aluminum siding Every 1–2 years Annual if shaded or near trees; every 2 years for sun-exposed sides
Concrete driveway Every 1–2 years Annual if parked cars drip oil or significant tree coverage overhead
Asphalt shingle roof Every 3–5 years Treat when black streaks first appear — don't let it go 7+ years
Wood deck or patio Every 1–2 years Annual in shaded yards; every 2 years for sun-exposed decks
Brick walkway or patio Every 2–3 years Sooner if you're seeing moss or dark discoloration in the mortar
Gutters (exterior face) Every 1–2 years Tiger-stripe staining on gutters indicates overdue cleaning
Commercial or stucco exterior Every 1–2 years Stucco is porous — don't let algae root in; annual is often worth it

The Best Time of Year to Wash in Lancaster PA

Timing matters more than most homeowners think. My strong recommendation for Lancaster: late spring, specifically late April through early June, is the ideal window for siding and driveways.

Here's why. Pollen season runs from roughly mid-March through late April in Lancaster. If you wash in March, you're cleaning before pollen coats everything — a waste of money. Wash after pollen drops in late April or May and you're starting fresh. You're also getting ahead of the humidity spike that hits Lancaster every summer and creates peak growing conditions for algae and mold.

Fall — September through October — is the second-best window. You're cleaning off the summer's biological buildup before it freezes into the surface over winter. Cold temperatures don't cause organic growth, but they also don't stop it from expanding. Removing it before freeze-thaw cycles set in protects your surfaces through the winter.

The one time I'd tell you to avoid: winter, when temperatures drop below 40°F. Cleaning solutions don't activate properly in cold, and wet surfaces become slip hazards when they refreeze.

Signs Your House Is Overdue — Don't Wait for These

Most homeowners wait until the problem is obvious. By then, some of the damage has already been done. Here's what to watch for:

  • Green or black streaking on siding. Green means algae. Black typically means mold, mildew, or the Gloeocapsa magma bacteria mentioned above. Both are biological — they're feeding on your surfaces and spreading.
  • Dark stripes on your roof. If your shingles show dark vertical streaks running down the slope, that's the bacteria. It won't stop on its own and gets worse every season. A soft wash will kill it and clear the roof.
  • Slippery concrete or pavers. That film you feel underfoot on a wet morning is algae. On a driveway or walkway it's a slip hazard. On a pool deck, it's a real liability.
  • Gutter faces with brown or black tiger stripes. This staining comes from overflow debris and algae growth on the exterior face of gutters. It washes off clean but doesn't go away on its own.
  • Deck wood that looks gray and rough. Sun and moisture oxidize wood over time. A cleaning and brightening treatment restores color and opens the grain before you restain. If you skip this, your stain won't absorb properly.

The Downside of Washing Too Often

Less is a real consideration on some surfaces. On wood decks and siding, excessive pressure washing — more than once a year — can raise the grain, strip protective finishes, and accelerate weathering. Soft washing with cleaning solution is gentler, but even that shouldn't be applied to roof shingles more than once a year at the maximum.

For concrete driveways and brick, washing more than once a year is usually just unnecessary cost. Concrete is durable and a thorough annual or biennial clean is plenty for a residential driveway that isn't getting heavy industrial traffic.

The goal is matching the wash cycle to the actual buildup cycle for your specific property — not following a rigid calendar regardless of what you're seeing. A shaded house surrounded by mature oaks in Lititz is going to show algae faster than a sun-drenched home in a newer development in East Hempfield. Frequently Asked Questions

How often should you power wash vinyl siding in Lancaster PA?

For most Lancaster homes with vinyl siding, once a year is the right cadence — typically in late spring after pollen season winds down. If your home has significant shade or is surrounded by mature trees, you may see algae and green buildup return faster and benefit from washing every 9–12 months. Soft washing (low pressure plus cleaning solution) is the correct method for vinyl — high-pressure washing can force water behind panels and cause damage.

How often should you clean your driveway in Lancaster PA?

A concrete driveway in Lancaster should be pressure washed every 1 to 2 years depending on traffic and tree coverage. If you have overhanging trees dropping tannins and organic debris, or you park vehicles that drip oil, annual cleaning is worth it. A thorough pressure wash with a surface cleaner removes embedded dirt, oil stains, and organic growth before it has a chance to permanently stain the concrete.

How often do roofs need to be cleaned in Lancaster PA?

Most Lancaster homeowners need roof cleaning every 3 to 5 years. The black streaks that appear on asphalt shingles are caused by Gloeocapsa magma bacteria — a real organism that spreads and feeds on your shingles. A professional soft wash kills it at the root. Once treated, most roofs stay clean for 3 to 5 years. Don't let it go longer than 5–7 years or the buildup can cause premature granule loss and shorten roof lifespan.

What time of year is best to power wash a house in Lancaster PA?

Late spring — May or early June — is the ideal window for most Lancaster homes. Pollen season is winding down, temperatures are above 50°F (which is important for cleaning solutions to work properly), and you're getting ahead of the summer humidity that accelerates algae and mold growth. Fall is a solid second choice if you missed spring. Avoid washing in winter when temperatures drop below freezing — cleaning solutions don't perform well and wet surfaces can become dangerous.

Can you power wash your house too often?

For most surfaces, washing more than once a year is unnecessary and, on some materials, counterproductive. Excessive pressure washing on wood siding or decking can raise the grain, accelerate weathering, and strip finishes. On roofs, even soft washing uses chemicals that should not be applied more than once a year. The goal is matching the wash frequency to the actual buildup cycle — which in Lancaster is typically annual for siding and driveways, every 3 to 5 years for roofs.
