How Often Should You Power Wash Your House in Lancaster PA?

Published May 2026 • 6 min read • By Lancaster Power Washing Pros

This is one of the most common questions I get from Lancaster homeowners, and the answer isn't the same for every surface. The frequency that makes sense for your driveway is different from what your siding needs, which is different from your roof. Here's the honest schedule, broken down by surface type and the specific conditions in Lancaster County.

House Siding: Every 1–2 Years

In Lancaster County, the combination of humidity, tree coverage, and north-facing exposures means algae, mold, and mildew build up faster than in drier climates. For most homes, washing the siding every 1–2 years keeps it looking clean and prevents the green and black staining that eventually etches into porous surfaces.

Houses with significant shade — mature trees close to the structure, north-facing walls — tend toward the annual end of that range. Houses in full sun with good air circulation can often go two full years between washings.

Soft washing siding (low pressure + cleaning solution) is the correct method. High pressure on vinyl or wood siding forces water behind panels and can cause water intrusion problems. See our house washing services for how we approach each material type.

Driveways and Sidewalks: Every 1–2 Years

Concrete in Lancaster sees road salt from winter maintenance, organic staining from leaves and tree sap, and oil drips from vehicles. Without regular cleaning, that grime works into the pores of the concrete and becomes increasingly difficult to remove.

Annual or biennial washing, followed by concrete sealing every 2–3 years, is the most cost-effective maintenance schedule. Sealed concrete resists staining better, cleans more easily, and lasts longer before cracking from freeze-thaw cycles.

Decks and Fencing: Every 1–2 Years Before Sealing

Wood decks accumulate mildew, gray weathering, and tannin stains from wet leaves sitting on the surface. Washing before re-staining or sealing is essential — sealer applied over dirty wood traps contaminants and reduces adhesion, leading to premature peeling.

A clean, bright deck that's freshly sealed should last 2–3 years before needing the process repeated. The washing is not optional — it's the prep step that makes the sealer work.

Roofs: Every 3–5 Years (Soft Wash Only)

Roof algae (Gloeocapsa magma, the black streaking you see on asphalt shingles) is endemic to Pennsylvania. It grows from the ridge down the north and west faces of most roofs in Lancaster County. Left untreated, algae retains moisture and accelerates shingle degradation.

Soft washing the roof with a biocide solution kills the algae at the root level. Results typically last 3–5 years. Installing zinc strips at the ridge after treatment can extend the clean period significantly. High-pressure washing on a roof is never appropriate — it strips granules, voids warranties, and shortens shingle life.

After Major Storms and Events

Beyond the regular schedule, specific events warrant a washing:

  • After a major storm that deposited significant debris, pollen, or mud
  • Before listing a home for sale (curb appeal is real and immediate)
  • After construction or renovation work that left dust and debris on exterior surfaces
  • Any time significant organic staining (berries, leaves, bird activity) is visible on siding or hardscape

The most common mistake I see Lancaster homeowners make is waiting until staining is severe before washing. At that point, you may need multiple treatments or stronger chemistry to get results. Washing on a regular schedule when buildup is moderate is faster, cheaper, and more effective than letting things get bad.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I wash my driveway in Lancaster PA?

Concrete driveways in Lancaster typically need washing every 1–2 years. If you apply a concrete sealer after washing, you can stretch that to 2–3 years. Unsealed driveways in shaded areas get moldy faster and may need annual cleaning.

What time of year is best for power washing in Lancaster PA?

Spring (April–May) is the most popular time — washing off the winter grime and road salt residue before summer. Fall is also good for cleaning before winter. Avoid washing when temperatures are below 40°F, as water can freeze in cracks and cause damage.

Does power washing protect my home's value?

Yes, measurably. Clean siding, driveways, and roofs improve curb appeal and prevent premature deterioration from algae, mold, and mildew. Homes in Lancaster County that are well-maintained sell faster and for more money. Power washing before listing a property is one of the highest-ROI prep steps you can take.

Is annual house washing too often or not enough?

For most Lancaster homes, every 1–2 years is appropriate for siding. Homes with heavy tree coverage or on the north side of hills (more shade = more moisture = faster algae growth) may benefit from annual washing. Homes in full sun with good drainage can often go 2–3 years.

How do I maintain my roof between washings?

Zinc or copper strips installed at the ridge line leach metal ions down the roof in rain, inhibiting algae and moss growth. This is a cost-effective way to extend the time between professional roof cleanings. Ask us about zinc strip installation when you book a roof cleaning.
