House Washing Cost in Lancaster PA — What You'll Pay in 2026

Updated May 2026 • 6 min read • By Lancaster Power Washing Pros

House washing is one of the most impactful things you can do for curb appeal in Lancaster County — and one of the most misunderstood services in terms of pricing. The confusion usually comes from conflating house washing with pressure washing, which are different approaches with different results and different price points. I'll clear that up, then give you real numbers for Lancaster PA in 2026.

For most Lancaster County homes, professional house washing runs $250 to $700. Here's what determines where your home falls in that range.

House Washing vs. Pressure Washing — What's the Difference?

This matters more than most homeowners realize:

  • Soft washing uses low pressure (similar to a garden hose) combined with professional-grade cleaning solutions — surfactants, biodegradable detergents, and algaecides. The chemistry does the work; the pressure is minimal. This is the correct approach for vinyl siding, stucco, EIFS, wood siding, cedar shakes, and painted surfaces. High pressure on these materials strips paint, drives moisture behind siding, and can damage the material itself.
  • Pressure washing uses high-pressure water. Appropriate for concrete, brick, and hard masonry surfaces. Not appropriate for most home siding.

When someone asks about "house washing" in Lancaster, they almost always mean soft washing — cleaning the exterior surfaces of the home without damaging the siding. We use this approach exclusively for home exteriors. Driveways and walkways get pressure washing.

What Drives House Washing Cost in Lancaster PA

  • Square footage. A 1,500 sq ft Cape Cod takes less time and product than a 3,200 sq ft Colonial. Expect to pay roughly $0.12–$0.25 per sq ft of exterior surface area (not living space).
  • Stories. A single-story ranch is the easiest situation. Two-story homes require extensions and more time in some areas. Three-story or tall Colonial homes may require ladder work on certain sections.
  • Level of contamination. A home with light surface dirt and some green algae on the north side costs less than one with years of heavy mold, oxidation on the gutters, and black streaking across the full facade. Heavy contamination requires more product and more dwell time.
  • Siding type. Vinyl is the most straightforward. Stucco and EIFS require extra care and specific technique. Cedar and wood siding need gentler chemistry. Old painted surfaces get extra attention to avoid paint damage.
  • Add-ons. Gutter face cleaning, deck washing, fence cleaning, and driveway cleaning are often bundled with house washing at a discounted combined rate.

How Often Should You Wash Your House in Lancaster PA?

Most Lancaster County homes benefit from professional washing every 1–2 years. The specific factors that determine frequency:

  • Tree coverage. Homes under heavy tree canopy accumulate algae, mold, and organic debris much faster. If you're surrounded by mature trees, plan on annual washing.
  • North-facing surfaces. North-facing walls get less sun and stay damper, making them prime territory for algae and mold growth. These areas always look worse faster.
  • Road exposure. Homes near busy roads accumulate more dust, exhaust, and grime.
  • Climate cycles. Lancaster's humid summers accelerate biological growth. After a wet spring like 2026, many homes are showing growth they didn't have in early March.

If you can see green or black streaking on your siding from across the street, it's time.
