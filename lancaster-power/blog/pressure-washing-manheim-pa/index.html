Deck and Fence Washing

Wood decks in Manheim accumulate mildew, gray weathering, and embedded dirt that makes restaining difficult and shortens the life of any finish you apply. We pressure wash decks before any staining or sealing project, and we adjust the pressure based on the wood — softer species like pine need lower pressure to avoid raising the grain, and composite decking gets handled even more gently. The goal is a clean surface that takes a finish well, not just something that looks clean from a distance.

Vinyl fencing cleans up quickly and the visual improvement is dramatic — most of the time it gets added to a house wash for minimal extra cost. Wood privacy fences take more time but are well worth it, especially if they're due for a fresh coat of stain or paint.

Roof Soft Washing in Manheim

The black streaking on roofs throughout Lancaster County is almost always Gloeocapsa magma — a cyanobacterium that feeds on the limestone filler in asphalt shingles. Pressure washing removes the visual staining temporarily but damages shingles in the process and voids manufacturer warranties. The correct approach is soft washing: low-pressure application of a specialized chemical solution that kills the algae at the cell level, then the dead growth rinses off over the next few weeks of rain. Done properly, results last three to five years versus the streaks returning the following spring if you pressure wash instead.
