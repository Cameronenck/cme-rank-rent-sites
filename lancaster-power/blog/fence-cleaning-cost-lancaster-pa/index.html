Fence Cleaning Cost in Lancaster PA — What You'll Pay in 2026

Updated May 2026 • 6 min read • By Lancaster Power Washing Pros

Fences take a beating in Lancaster County. Green algae and mold from summer humidity, road grime near driveways, tannin stains from leaves, dirt splash from rain — a fence that was clean when installed starts looking rough within a few years. Fence cleaning is one of the more cost-effective ways to improve the appearance of your property without replacement, and most Lancaster homeowners are surprised at how good a cleaned fence looks afterward.

What Drives Fence Cleaning Costs

  • Fence material. Vinyl and aluminum clean faster than wood. Wood fences with deep grain can trap dirt and mold, requiring more passes and treatment time.
  • Condition and staining. A fence that's been neglected for 5+ years with established algae and mold colonies takes significantly more work than one that was cleaned 2 years ago.
  • Height. Standard 6-foot privacy fences are straightforward. Taller fences (8 ft+) take more time and equipment adjustment.
  • Access. Fences along property lines sometimes have restricted access on one side — adjacent properties, landscaping, tight yards. Cleaning from one side only takes more passes.
  • Surrounding landscaping. Dense plantings next to fences require careful work to avoid damaging ornamentals and gardens.

Soft Wash vs. Pressure Wash for Fences

This distinction matters for fence cleaning. High-pressure washing (2,500+ PSI) is appropriate for concrete and masonry but can damage wood fences, forcing water into gaps and potentially causing warping or premature deterioration of older wood.

For most fence cleaning in Lancaster, we use a soft wash approach: lower pressure (1,200–1,800 PSI) combined with an appropriate cleaning solution that kills mold, algae, and bacteria on contact. The chemical does the work; the water rinses it away. Results are thorough without the risk of wood damage from high pressure.

Chain link and metal fences can handle more pressure since they're not porous and won't absorb water. Vinyl is in the middle — it tolerates moderate pressure but doesn't benefit from the maximum.

Wood Fence Cleaning and Restoration

Wood fences in Lancaster's climate go through a natural weathering process: UV exposure grays the wood, moisture penetrates, mold establishes in grain channels, and tannins from leaves stain contact surfaces. Regular cleaning slows this process significantly, but neglected wood fences sometimes need more than a wash.

If your wood fence is gray and weathered but structurally sound, a proper cleaning followed by a wood brightener application restores much of the original color. After brightening, the wood is ready for sealing or staining, which dramatically extends the life of the fence and reduces future maintenance cycles.

We can assess your wood fence and let you know whether a standard cleaning is sufficient or whether a cleaning-and-brightener treatment makes sense before you invest in staining.

When to Clean Your Fence in Lancaster

Spring and early summer are peak times for fence cleaning in Lancaster County. Here's why timing matters:

  • Spring: After winter grime and before summer UV sets stains deeper into wood grain. Best time if you're planning to seal or stain wood — clean it, let it dry, seal it before the summer heat.
  • Late summer/fall: Before winter. A clean fence goes into the cold season in better condition and comes out in spring needing less work.
  • Avoid cleaning during freezing temperatures. Water in wood during a freeze can cause damage. Wait for consistently above-freezing conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does fence cleaning cost in Lancaster PA?
Fence cleaning in Lancaster County typically runs $150–$600 depending on length, material, and condition. Call 717-807-7599 for a free estimate.
Can you pressure wash a vinyl fence?
Yes. Vinyl fences respond well to soft washing — a lower-pressure approach with cleaning solution. High-pressure on vinyl can force water behind panels, so technique matters.
Should I seal my fence after cleaning?
For wood fences, sealing or staining after cleaning is strongly recommended. Clean wood absorbs sealant much better, and the treatment protects against future moisture damage.
How often should I clean my fence in Lancaster PA?
Most Lancaster homeowners benefit from cleaning wood fences every 2–3 years and vinyl fences every 3–4 years. Regular cleaning prevents mold from establishing and keeps the fence looking maintained.
