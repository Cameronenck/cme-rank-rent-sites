Why Strasburg-Area Homes Need Attention to Gutters

Strasburg gets the same 40 inches of annual rainfall as the rest of Lancaster County, but the style of housing here means the water management stakes can be higher. A lot of the homes in the borough were built in the late 1800s and early 1900s. Their foundations are stone or brick, and while those materials last, they're also vulnerable to sustained water infiltration. When gutters fail — either overflowing because they're clogged, or leaking at joints because the sealant dried out years ago — that water runs straight down the side of the house and pools at the foundation. Over years, that cycle causes real structural problems that cost a lot more to fix than a gutter replacement.

I've seen this play out more than once on older Strasburg properties. The homeowner thinks they have a basement seepage problem, they call a waterproofing company, they spend several thousand dollars on interior drainage systems — and the root cause was overflowing gutters that were directing water right at the foundation for the past decade. The gutters needed to be replaced and the downspouts needed to be extended three to four feet away from the house. That's a fraction of the cost of the waterproofing job. I'm not saying gutters are always the cause, but they're always worth ruling out first.

The Issue with Older Sectional Gutter Systems in Strasburg

If your Strasburg home was built before roughly 1995 and still has the original gutters, there's a high probability you're running on a sectional system — sections of aluminum or steel connected every 10 feet with brackets and silicone or rubber gasket seals. Those seals have a finite life. Once they fail, every joint becomes a drip point that runs water down your siding and fascia continuously during every rain event. The fascia behind those joint failures soaks up water and starts to rot, and once fascia is compromised, it won't hold gutter hangers correctly.

We fabricate seamless gutters on-site — each run is a single piece cut to the exact length of your roofline from a continuous roll of aluminum stock. The only joints in the system are at inside and outside corners and at downspout connections. That's it. No mid-run seals to fail, no annual re-caulking to keep up with. For older homes in the Strasburg borough, this is almost always the right call over patching a sectional system that's already failing in multiple places.

Common Gutter Problems on Strasburg Properties

After doing this work throughout this part of Lancaster County, here are the issues I see most often on properties in Strasburg and the surrounding areas:

  • Fascia rot on Victorian-era homes — The elaborate wood trim and cornice details on older borough homes can hide significant moisture damage behind the gutter line. We inspect all of it before we quote. If the fascia needs to come out and be replaced, that's a separate line item with full transparency before we start.
  • Undersized 4-inch gutters on older homes — Anything built before the mid-1990s may have 4-inch gutters that were code-compliant at the time but are undersized for the rainfall intensity Lancaster County sees now. We size up to 5-inch on every replacement project.
  • Farmhouse addition rooflines — A lot of properties just outside Strasburg Township have farm buildings or older houses that have been added onto over the decades. These often have irregular rooflines with valleys that concentrate water in ways the original gutter layout didn't account for. Valley areas need properly sized downspout placement to handle the volume.
  • Downspouts that terminate at the foundation — Standard on older construction. We extend all downspouts a minimum of three to four feet away from the foundation as part of every installation. Splash blocks and underground extensions are available for properties where grade or landscaping makes surface extensions tricky.
  • Tree debris from the Strasburg rail corridor — The mature tree canopy along the rail trail and through older residential blocks sheds heavily from late spring through fall. Homes with this kind of canopy overhead are prime candidates for micro-mesh gutter guards.

Gutter Guards Around Strasburg

The tree coverage in this part of the county is real — the oak, maple, and walnut canopy over the older neighborhoods and along the farm property lines means gutters fill up fast if they're left open. We install micro-mesh guards that let water through while blocking leaves, seed pods, and the small debris that can clog a standard 3x4 downspout. These aren't the foam inserts or plastic brush guards that you see marketed at big box stores — those trap debris in the medium itself and create a different maintenance headache. Quality micro-mesh installed correctly dramatically reduces cleaning frequency for the life of the system.
