The Gutter Installation Process — What Happens on the Day

When we show up for a gutter installation, we arrive early with scaffolding, tools, and our fabrication equipment. Here's how the day flows:

Step 1: Inspection. We remove your old gutters carefully to avoid damaging fascia or soffits. Once they're off, we inspect the fascia board underneath. If it's soft, rotted, or compromised by water damage, we let you know before we install new gutters. There's no point putting new aluminum on deteriorating wood — the fascia will fail and pull your new gutters down with it.

Step 2: Measurement and fabrication. We measure your roofline in sections, accounting for slopes, corners, and height variations. Those measurements feed directly into our seamless gutter machine, which extrudes aluminum stock to exact lengths with zero seams or weak points. Every section is custom-made for your specific roof.

Step 3: Hanging and pitching. Gutters must be pitched slightly — typically 1/2 inch of drop per 10 feet of run — so water flows toward downspouts rather than pooling. We use hangers every 24 inches to support the weight. The pitch is subtle enough that you won't notice it visually, but water will flow perfectly.

Step 4: Downspouts and discharge. Downspouts are positioned to move water at least 4 feet away from your foundation. We avoid routing gutters directly against walls or into basement window wells. If your current downspout locations are problematic, we reposition them during installation.

Step 5: Cleanup and walkthrough. We haul away all old gutter material and debris. We walk you through the new system, explain the warranty, and show you what routine maintenance looks like (typically twice-yearly cleaning unless you have guards).

Most Lancaster homes take 4 to 6 hours. Larger homes or those requiring fascia repair may take a full day.

How Much Does Local Gutter Installation Cost?

Seamless gutter installation in Lancaster PA costs $4 to $9 per linear foot installed. A typical Lancaster home with 150 to 180 linear feet runs $600 to $1,620 total. That includes removal of old gutters, new aluminum gutters, downspouts, hangers, and trim.

Factors that affect price: gutter material (aluminum vs. copper), color, roof complexity, and whether fascia repair is needed. Premium colors and copper are more expensive. If fascia is rotted and needs replacement, add $3–8 per linear foot.

Should You Add Gutter Guards?

If you have trees near your home, gutter guards are worth serious consideration. Leaves, twigs, and seeds clog gutters fast in Lancaster — especially in fall. Guards reduce cleaning from twice per year to once every 2–3 years. They cost $6–12 per linear foot installed, so most homeowners recoup the cost in labor savings within 3–4 years.

Different guard types work for different roofs. We'll recommend the best option for your specific situation during your estimate.
