Gutter Replacement Cost in Lancaster PA — 2026 Pricing Guide

Updated May 2026 • 7 min read • Lancaster Gutter Pros

When gutters fail in Lancaster PA — whether from age, storm damage, or years of deferred maintenance — the cost of replacement is usually the first question. The second question is usually whether replacement is actually necessary or whether repair will do the job. This guide answers both, with 2026 pricing specific to Lancaster County homes and what the installation process actually involves.

Gutter replacement in Lancaster PA costs $6 to $15 per linear foot for seamless aluminum, the most common system on Lancaster County homes, or $25 to $45 per linear foot for copper. A typical home with 150 to 200 linear feet of guttering runs $900 to $3,000 for a full aluminum replacement including downspouts and trim.

Gutter Replacement vs. Repair — When Each Makes Sense

Not every gutter problem requires a full replacement. Here is a practical breakdown:

  • Repair is the right call when you have isolated leaks at end caps or joints, a few loose hangers, one or two small holes or cracks, or a single section of sagging. These are $50 to $200 repairs that extend the life of an otherwise functioning system.
  • Replacement is the right call when gutters are pulling away from the fascia in multiple locations, when rust or holes have spread through most of the system, when your home still has sectional gutters with widespread joint failure, or when your current gutters are undersized for your roof's drainage area.
  • Fascia damage matters. In Lancaster County's humid summers, gutters that have been failing for a while often damage the fascia board behind them. We assess fascia condition at every estimate — if the wood is soft or rotted, it needs to be addressed before new gutters go on. New gutters on a failing fascia will fail themselves within a few years.

What the Gutter Replacement Process Looks Like

Seamless gutter installation in Lancaster County is a one-day job for most homes. The process starts with removing the old gutters and inspecting the fascia boards. Any fascia repairs are done before new gutters go on. New seamless aluminum gutters are fabricated on-site from a roll of aluminum stock — this is what makes them seamless. The profile is cut to exact length for each run, eliminating the joints where sectional gutters leak.

Gutters are pitched and hung to direct water toward the downspouts, with a specific drop per 10 feet of run to ensure proper drainage. Downspouts are positioned to discharge water at least four feet from the foundation. If your existing downspout locations are not optimal — discharging near a door or against the foundation — we can adjust placement during the replacement.

How do I know when my gutters need to be replaced in Lancaster PA?
Key signs include multiple sections pulling away from the fascia, visible holes or rust through the gutter body, persistent sagging, extensive joint failures on sectional gutters, or gutters more than 20 years old with widespread deterioration. Minor isolated damage can often be repaired without full replacement.
How long do seamless gutters last in Lancaster PA?
Seamless aluminum gutters in Lancaster County typically last 20 to 30 years with proper maintenance. Steel gutters last 15 to 25 years before rust becomes an issue. Copper gutters last 50 years or more but cost significantly more upfront.
What size gutters do Lancaster PA homes need?

Most Lancaster County homes use 5-inch K-style gutters. Homes with steep roof pitches or large roof planes may benefit from 6-inch gutters, which move roughly 40 percent more water. We assess the roof pitch and drainage area at the estimate to recommend the correct size.
