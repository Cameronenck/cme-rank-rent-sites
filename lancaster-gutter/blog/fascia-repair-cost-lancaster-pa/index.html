Fascia Repair Cost in Lancaster PA

Lancaster Gutter Installation Pros — Published May 23, 2026

Fascia is the horizontal board that runs along the edge of your roofline, directly behind your gutters. It is the structural backbone of your entire gutter system. When fascia deteriorates, gutters pull away, water infiltrates your soffit, and what started as a minor repair can become a costly structural problem. Homeowners across Lancaster County frequently ask us the same question: what does fascia repair cost in Lancaster PA? The short answer is $8 to $25 per linear foot for repairs, and $15 to $45 per linear foot for full replacement, depending on material and labor complexity.

What Is Fascia and Why Does It Matter?

Fascia boards are typically one-by-eight or one-by-ten lumber (or their aluminum/PVC equivalents) mounted vertically at the roof's edge. Your gutters attach directly to them with spikes or hidden hangers. Beyond supporting the gutters, fascia seals the gap between the roofing deck and the exterior wall, blocking moisture, insects, and pests from entering the attic cavity. Damaged fascia compromises every layer of protection above and below it.

Signs Your Fascia Needs Attention

  • Soft or spongy wood when pressed -- a clear indicator of rot that can spread rapidly
  • Paint bubbling or peeling along the roofline, signaling trapped moisture beneath the surface
  • Visible water staining on the board or on the soffit below it
  • Sagging or pulling gutters -- when fascia softens, gutter spikes lose their grip and the gutter tilts outward
  • Pest or insect damage -- carpenter bees and squirrels target weakened fascia wood
  • Visible cracks or splits on the face of the board, especially on older homes with original pine or spruce fascia

If you notice sagging gutters alongside fascia damage, you may be dealing with a combined problem. Our guide on downspout repair cost in Lancaster PA covers what happens when water cannot exit the system properly, which accelerates fascia deterioration from behind.

How Lancaster PA Climate Damages Fascia

Lancaster County's climate is particularly hard on fascia. Winters bring freeze-thaw cycles that force water into microscopic cracks in the wood, expanding them each time temperatures drop below freezing. Heavy spring rains then saturate any exposed or poorly sealed board. Summers add UV degradation. Over several seasons, even quality painted wood fascia can begin to rot from the back side where it contacts the rafter tails, making the damage invisible until gutters start pulling away. Aluminum and PVC options were developed largely in response to this type of climate stress.

Fascia Material Options and Costs

Wood Fascia

Traditional pine or cedar fascia remains the most common material on older Lancaster homes. It accepts paint well and integrates easily with existing trim profiles. The downside is susceptibility to rot. Repair cost typically runs $8 to $14 per linear foot; full replacement with new wood fascia ranges from $15 to $22 per linear foot installed.

Aluminum Fascia

Aluminum fascia wrapping (typically .019 to .024 gauge) is often installed over existing wood as a protective cap. It will not rot, requires no painting, and handles freeze-thaw stress well. Full aluminum fascia replacement runs $18 to $30 per linear foot installed, making it a popular upgrade on Lancaster homes getting new gutters at the same time.

PVC / Cellular PVC Fascia

PVC fascia boards are impervious to moisture, insects, and rot. They are the premium long-term option but carry a higher upfront cost: $22 to $45 per linear foot installed. For homeowners planning to stay in their home long-term, PVC often provides the best return given the reduced maintenance cost over 20-plus years.

Patch Repair vs Full Replacement

Not every damaged fascia section requires a full board replacement. Spot repairs with wood filler, epoxy consolidant, and fresh paint are viable when rot is isolated to a small area -- typically less than 12 inches -- and the surrounding wood is structurally sound. However, once rot spreads beyond a single section or the board is soft behind the gutter mount points, full board replacement is the safer and more economical long-term choice. Leaving compromised fascia in place while reinstalling gutters typically means repeating the job within two to three years.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does fascia repair cost in Lancaster PA on average?

Most Lancaster homeowners pay between $400 and $1,100 for fascia repairs covering a typical section of 50 linear feet. Spot patching on isolated rot runs $8 to $14 per linear foot, while full board replacement in wood ranges from $15 to $22 per linear foot installed. Aluminum and PVC materials cost more upfront but last significantly longer.

Can I just replace fascia without replacing my gutters?

Yes, in many cases. If the gutters are in good condition, a contractor can carefully remove them, replace or repair the fascia beneath, then rehang the same gutters. Expect an additional $2 to $4 per linear foot in labor to remove and reinstall existing gutters. If the gutters are older than 15 years, combining both projects at once often makes more financial sense.

How long does fascia replacement take?

A standard single-story home with 100 to 150 linear feet of fascia typically takes one to two days. Larger two-story homes or those with complex rooflines may require an additional day. Weather and material availability can affect scheduling, especially during Lancaster County's busy spring season when many homeowners address winter damage.

Does homeowners insurance cover fascia repair in Pennsylvania?

It depends on the cause of damage. Storm-related fascia damage -- from a fallen branch, high winds, or ice damming -- is often covered under standard homeowners policies after the deductible. Gradual rot from deferred maintenance is typically excluded. Document damage promptly with photos and contact your insurer before authorizing repairs to understand what your policy covers.
