Why Gutters Matter in Elizabethtown Specifically

Elizabethtown averages around 40 inches of rainfall per year, which is consistent with the rest of Lancaster County. But Elizabethtown also sits in a part of the county with a fair amount of mature tree canopy — the older neighborhoods in particular have large maples and oaks that shed leaves, seed pods, and debris into gutters every fall. When those gutters clog and overflow, the water doesn't just run into the yard. It saturates the soil directly against your foundation, which is how you end up with seepage in the basement and cracks in the footer over time.

I've walked countless Elizabethtown properties where homeowners were dealing with basement water problems they'd been blaming on the water table. When we cleared the gutters and redirected the downspouts, the problem stopped. Proper gutter installation — not just functional gutters, but correctly sloped, properly drained, and with downspouts extended away from the foundation — does more to protect a Lancaster County basement than almost any other single investment.

Seamless Gutters: Why We Don't Install Sectional Systems Anymore

If you've looked at DIY gutter sections at the home improvement store, those are the old sectional style — pieces that join together with brackets and sealant at every seam. Every seam is a future leak. The sealant dries, cracks, and separates. When the sealant fails, you get water pouring down the fascia board right where the gutter is supposed to hold it.

We fabricate seamless gutters on-site using a roll-forming machine in the truck. Every run is a single continuous piece cut to the exact length of your roofline. The only joints are at inside and outside corners, and at downspout connections — not strung along every 10 feet. Seamless gutters last longer, look better, and don't develop the drip points that slowly rot your fascia and siding.

What to Expect on Older Elizabethtown Properties

Elizabethtown's older homes — the ones on South Market and Bainbridge streets, the neighborhoods around East High Street — often have issues you won't find on newer construction:

  • Rotted fascia boards — years of gutter overflow soak the board the gutter hangs from. Soft, punky wood won't hold a hanger correctly. We check every fascia board before we hang anything and replace what needs it.
  • Undersized 4-inch gutters — older homes were built with 4-inch round or K-style gutters. Current best practice for Lancaster County's rainfall intensity is 5-inch K-style at minimum. We size up on every replacement job.
  • Steep roof pitches — many period homes have steeper than average pitches. More pitch means water hits the gutter faster and with more force, which means you want a properly sized, securely hung system — not something held with cheap spike-and-ferrule hangers.
  • Insufficient downspout count — older systems were often installed with one downspout per side of the house. For a 50-foot run, that's pushing the limits. We calculate downspout capacity per run and add outlets where the math says you need them.

Gutter Guards for Elizabethtown Tree Coverage

If you have mature trees near the roofline — and most homes in Elizabethtown's older neighborhoods do — gutter guards are worth the conversation. Not all guards are worth buying. Foam inserts and brush-style guards trap debris and turn into a maintenance headache. We install screen and micro-mesh options that keep large debris out while letting water through. With guards installed, most homeowners go from cleaning gutters twice a year to once every couple of years.

How do I know if my gutters in Elizabethtown need to be replaced versus repaired?

Replacement is the smarter investment when gutters are pulling away from the fascia along multiple runs, when there's visible rust or widespread cracking, or when the system is more than 20 years old and causing repeated maintenance calls.
