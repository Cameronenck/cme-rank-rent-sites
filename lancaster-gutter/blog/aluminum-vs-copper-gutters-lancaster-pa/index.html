Aluminum vs Copper Gutters in Lancaster PA — Which Is Worth It?

📅 May 11, 2026 🕒 6 min read 📍 Lancaster, PA

Every week I get at least one homeowner in Lancaster County who asks me the same question: "Should I go with aluminum or copper gutters?" It's a fair question, and the honest answer depends on your home, your budget, and how long you plan to stay put.

I've installed gutters on everything from new builds in Manheim Township to historic row homes in downtown Lancaster. Here's what I've learned after 20+ years of doing this work in our county.

The Quick Answer

Aluminum gutters are the right call for 90% of homes in Lancaster PA. They're durable, affordable, come in dozens of colors, and will protect your home for 20–30 years when installed correctly. Copper gutters are genuinely beautiful and can last 50+ years — but they cost three to four times as much and make sense only for specific situations.

Both options can work here. Our winters are hard on gutters — we get freeze-thaw cycles, ice dams, heavy spring rains, and those back-to-back storms that hit the county every few years. The material you pick needs to handle all of that. Both aluminum and copper do, just in different ways.

Let's Talk About Aluminum

Seamless aluminum is what we install on the vast majority of homes in Lancaster PA, and there's a reason for that. When we pull up to your property and fabricate the gutters right in the driveway using our machine, we're cutting them to the exact length your roofline needs. No seams means no joints — and joints are where leaks happen.

Aluminum doesn't rust. It holds paint well, so you can match your gutters to your fascia, your trim, or whatever color your wife picked for the exterior this spring. It handles our heavy rain events without trouble, and it's light enough that it won't put excessive stress on your fascia boards over time.

The most common aluminum gauge we install is .032 — which is the residential standard. I'll sometimes recommend .040 gauge for larger homes or commercial work because it handles heavy debris loads better and is more resistant to denting. A few extra dollars per foot is worth it if you've got big trees hanging over the roofline.

Local note: Lancaster County's mature tree canopy is beautiful — but it drops a lot of debris into gutters every fall. If you're in a wooded lot in Lititz, Ephrata, or anywhere in the rural townships, I almost always recommend pairing aluminum gutters with micro-mesh guards. It cuts your cleaning frequency down dramatically.

What About Copper?

Copper gutters are a different animal entirely. If you've walked through the historic districts in Lancaster City or seen some of the older homes in Strasburg or Marietta, you've probably noticed that warm greenish-brown color on the gutters. That's copper in its patina stage — and it looks stunning on the right house.

When copper is first installed, it's shiny and bright — almost like a new penny. Over the first few years, it oxidizes into darker brown tones and eventually develops that classic blue-green patina. Some homeowners love that aged, historic look. Others want to maintain the brighter appearance, which requires occasional polishing or a clear sealant.

The durability of copper is genuinely remarkable. A properly installed copper gutter system can outlast the home itself — 50 to 100 years is realistic. They handle our freeze-thaw cycles particularly well because copper is naturally malleable and doesn't crack the way some cheaper materials do under thermal stress.

The catch is cost. On a typical 1,600 square foot Lancaster County home with roughly 160 linear feet of gutter, you're looking at a rough difference of $1,600–$2,000 for aluminum vs. $4,000–$7,000+ for copper. That's not a small gap. It's real money, and most homeowners — even ones who love the look — decide aluminum makes more sense financially.

When I'd Actually Recommend Copper

I'm not going to push copper on someone who doesn't need it. But here's when it genuinely makes sense:

  • Historic homes in Lancaster City or borough areas — Copper is architecturally appropriate and can be required in some historic districts. It fits the character of the home in a way aluminum simply doesn't.
  • High-end builds and luxury renovations — If you're doing a full exterior renovation with custom woodwork, slate roofing, and stone siding, aluminum gutters will look out of place. Copper finishes the picture.
  • Long-term homeowners who want set-it-and-forget-it — If you're planning to stay in your home for 30+ years and want something you'll never have to replace again, the copper premium makes financial sense over that time horizon.
  • Homes where curb appeal directly affects value — Some high-end neighborhoods in Lancaster County where copper gutters are a visible differentiator. If your neighbors have copper and you install aluminum, it'll stand out.

The Bottom Line for Lancaster Homeowners

If you call me today about a new gutter installation, here's what I'm going to tell you: start with seamless aluminum. It will protect your home for two to three decades, it won't rust, it comes in a color that matches your exterior, and I can usually schedule the install within a week. For most Lancaster County homeowners, that's the right answer.

If you have a historic home, you're doing a premium renovation, or you simply want the best material money can buy and plan to stay in your home long-term — let's talk copper. I'll give you a straight quote and walk you through what the install involves.

Either way, call me first. I'll come out, look at your roofline, check your fascia condition, and give you a real number — not a ballpark pulled out of thin air. That's the only way to do this right.
