Gutter Installation in Lititz PA — Local Service, Honest Prices

I've been doing gutter work in Lancaster County for going on 14 years now. Rotted fascia, water in the basement, foundation erosion. I've seen all of it. If you're shopping for gutter installation in Lititz, here's what the job actually looks like from my end and what to expect when you call us.

Why Lititz Homes Need Solid Gutters

Lititz sits right in the middle of Lancaster County where we get a solid 44 inches of rain a year plus snow and ice that puts real stress on gutters and downspouts. Many of the homes in town — especially in the older neighborhoods off Broad Street and the residential blocks near Lititz Springs Park — have fascia that hasn't been touched in decades. When gutters leak or pull away from the roofline, that fascia takes the hit first.

The other thing I see constantly in Lititz: undersized gutters. A lot of these homes were built with 4-inch sectional gutters that simply can't handle the volume of water coming off the roof during a heavy summer downpour. The gutter overflows, the water runs down the siding, and eventually it finds its way into the basement. The fix is moving to 5-inch or 6-inch seamless gutters with properly sized downspouts — and making sure those downspouts actually move the water away from the foundation.

What We Install — Seamless Aluminum, Fabricated On-Site

Everything we do in Lititz uses seamless aluminum. We run a roll-forming machine right off the truck, which means we fabricate each gutter run to the exact length of your roofline — no seams along the straight sections. The only joints in the system are at inside corners, outside corners, and downspout outlets. Fewer joints means fewer leak points, which means the system lasts longer.

We use .032 gauge aluminum as our standard. That's heavier than what a lot of budget installers use. It holds its shape better, resists denting from ladders and falling branches, and doesn't sag between hangers the way thinner material does. For most Lititz homes we install 5-inch K-style gutters, though we'll go to 6-inch when the roof area is large or the pitch is steep and the water volume demands it.

Hanger spacing is 18 inches on-center — not the 24-inch spacing some contractors use to save labor. In a Lancaster County winter with ice loading, 24-inch spacing leads to sagging. We've replaced plenty of gutters installed that way by other contractors. We'd rather do it right the first time.

What the Job Looks Like

Here's a typical gutter replacement for a Lititz ranch or colonial:

  • We show up, measure every run, and inspect the fascia before anything comes down
  • If the fascia has soft spots, we flag them before we start — and give you a price to replace those sections so there are no surprises mid-job
  • Old gutters come down, we clear the area, and the roll-former goes on the truck
  • New seamless gutters are cut to length, end-capped, and hung with hidden hangers into the fascia
  • Downspouts are cut, crimped, and directed away from the foundation — minimum 5 to 6 feet out
  • We do a final walk with you, run a hose test if there's any question, and haul off all the old material

Most houses in Lititz are one-day jobs. We don't leave you with half a gutter system overnight. If something comes up that makes the job bigger than quoted — say, more rotten fascia than was visible at estimate — we call you before doing any additional work.

Gutter Guards in Lititz

Lititz has some serious tree cover, and we get a lot of calls in November from people who've been cleaning gutters twice a year for 20 years and are done with it. If that's you, gutter guards are worth the conversation.

We install micro-mesh guards that keep out leaves, pine needles, and seed pods while letting water through cleanly. They're not zero-maintenance — nothing is — but they take a twice-yearly cleaning chore down to an occasional inspection. On homes with significant tree overhang, they pay for themselves fast in ladder time alone, not to mention the water damage that comes from neglected gutters.

Check out our full gutter guard options or call and we'll walk you through whether they make sense for your specific property.
