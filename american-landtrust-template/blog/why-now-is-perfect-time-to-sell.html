Why Now Is the Perfect Time to Sell

Back to Articles

By Help Sell My Home Fast Team · May 2025

If you've been holding onto vacant land and wondering whether it's time to sell, the answer might surprise you. Several converging factors are creating a unique window of opportunity for landowners across America — from favorable market conditions to tax advantages that may not last forever.

Land Values Are at Historic Highs

According to USDA data, the average value of farmland in the United States has risen steadily over the past decade, with Texas seeing some of the most significant appreciation. Urban sprawl, commercial development, and increasing demand for rural recreational properties have all contributed to land values that may be near their peak in many markets.

While no one can predict the future with certainty, experienced land professionals will tell you the same thing: markets are cyclical. Today's strong valuations may not persist indefinitely, especially as interest rates and economic conditions evolve.

The Hidden Costs of Holding

Many landowners don't realize how much their vacant property is actually costing them each year:

  • Property taxes — even vacant land is taxed, and many counties have been increasing assessments
  • Insurance liability — if someone is injured on your property, you could be held responsible
  • Maintenance costs — brush clearing, fence repairs, and preventing illegal dumping add up
  • Opportunity cost — the money tied up in land could be invested elsewhere, earning returns

When you add these up over years or decades, the total cost of holding can be staggering — sometimes exceeding tens of thousands of dollars.

Tax Advantages of Selling Now

Current capital gains tax rates, combined with potential step-up basis provisions for inherited property, can make 2025 an especially advantageous year to sell. If you've inherited land, you may have a significantly reduced tax burden compared to selling in the future.

We always recommend consulting with a qualified tax professional, but the bottom line is this: the tax landscape for land sales is currently favorable, and legislative changes could alter that at any time.

Life Changes That Make Selling Smart

Beyond market and tax considerations, many landowners reach a point in life where selling simply makes sense:

  • Retirement planning — converting land to liquid assets can fund your retirement lifestyle
  • Estate simplification — reducing the complexity of your estate benefits your heirs
  • Debt elimination — a land sale can eliminate mortgages, medical bills, or other obligations
  • Relocation — if you've moved away from the area, managing remote property becomes burdensome
