Appliance Removal in Peoria, IL

Old appliances are heavy, awkward, and often contain materials that can't go in a standard dumpster. The refrigerator in the garage that stopped working two winters ago. The washer from the basement when you upgraded to a front-loader. The chest freezer that came with the house and has been sitting unplugged in the utility room for three years. These things don't move themselves, and most hauling services either won't touch them or charge extra to figure out disposal compliance.

Peoria Junk Removal Pros handles appliance removal throughout Peoria, East Peoria, Morton, Dunlap, Washington, and the surrounding area. We're equipped for heavy lifts, we handle refrigerant compliance for units that require it, and we dispose of everything properly — no dumping.

  • Washers and dryers (any brand, top-load and front-load)

The Appliance Removal Process

For most appliances, the process is straightforward: we disconnect (if needed), carry it out, load it, done. Gas appliances require the gas line to be shut off and capped before we arrive — we don't do gas work. If you're not sure how to cap a gas line, your gas provider or a plumber can do it quickly and inexpensively before we show up.

Refrigerators and window AC units that still contain refrigerant require compliant disposal. We coordinate that through licensed facilities. You don't need to know the details — just know we handle it correctly. Peoria County has disposal requirements for refrigerants; we're not the kind of operation that bypasses them.

Exercise equipment deserves its own note: a commercial treadmill or loaded weight bench can weigh 400–600 pounds. We bring the right equipment and crew for heavy lifts. If you've got a home gym that's turning into dead weight, call us — we've cleared them before and know how to get equipment through doorways it shouldn't fit through.

Appliance Removal When You're Replacing

If you're having a new appliance delivered by a big-box retailer, many delivery services take the old unit — but with restrictions. They often won't take items in certain conditions, items in basements, or items that require special handling. We have none of those restrictions. We can schedule to arrive the morning of your delivery, clear the old unit, and have the space ready before the new appliance arrives.

This is particularly common in older Peoria homes where the basement laundry room has a tight staircase that delivery drivers won't navigate. We do it regularly.
