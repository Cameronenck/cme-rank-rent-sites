Garage Cleanout Cost in Peoria IL — What You'll Actually Pay

Updated May 2026 • 6 min read • By Peoria Junk Removal Pros

The garage is where stuff goes to disappear. A box here, a broken appliance there, and before long you're parking on the street because there's no room for the car. Garage cleanouts are one of the most common jobs we do in Peoria, and the pricing question always comes up first: how much is this actually going to cost?

Short answer: most Peoria garage cleanouts run between $200 and $650 for a two-car space, depending on how full it is and what's in there. Here's exactly how that price gets calculated.

How Garage Cleanout Pricing Works

Junk removal is priced by volume — how much truck space your items take up. That's true for garage cleanouts too, but garages have a few unique factors that affect the final number.

  • How full the garage is. A quarter-full garage is a very different job than floor-to-ceiling stuff packed on both sides with a path down the middle. Volume is the biggest driver.
  • What's in there. Most garage contents are straightforward — boxes, old furniture, yard equipment, holiday decorations, scrap wood. Some things have disposal costs: tires, paint cans, motor oil, old appliances. We'll identify those on the walk-through.
  • Weight. Garages tend to have heavier items than a typical room — concrete blocks, engine parts, tool chests, bags of sand. Heavy loads take more effort and fuel.
  • Organization needed. If items are already sorted in piles and ready to go, that's faster. If we need to dig through decades of accumulated stuff, that adds time.
  • Appliances. Old refrigerators, freezers, washers, and dryers in the garage need special handling. There's typically a $45–$75 per-appliance charge for proper disposal, which we discuss upfront.

What Happens to the Stuff

We don't haul everything straight to the landfill. Garages almost always have items worth salvaging:

  • Tools in working condition go to Habitat for Humanity ReStore or local tool lending programs.
  • Sports equipment, outdoor furniture, and yard tools get donated to local Peoria thrift stores and nonprofits.
  • Scrap metal (old pipes, metal shelving, automotive parts) goes to a local recycling facility.
  • Cardboard and clean paper goes to the recycling center, not the landfill.
  • Electronics (old TVs, computer monitors, printers) go through e-waste recycling, not the trash.

What's genuinely past its useful life goes to the transfer station. We keep landfill loads to a minimum because it matters — and because disposal fees are one of our actual costs.

Things to Set Aside Before We Arrive

Before your cleanout appointment, it helps to pull out anything you're keeping so the crew can work without second-guessing. If you have items for donation that you've already sorted, point those out and we'll handle them separately. And if you have hazardous materials — motor oil, paint, solvents, propane tanks — let us know on the booking call. We'll walk you through how to handle those, since they need special disposal that we can't do at a standard transfer station.

For most garages, you don't need to do anything except unlock it and show us what goes. We handle the rest.

Garage Cleanout vs. Dumpster Rental for Peoria Homes

Sometimes people ask whether a dumpster rental is cheaper. It can be for very large projects where you're doing the work yourself over multiple days. But for most garage cleanouts in Peoria:

  • A 10-yard dumpster rental runs $350–$450 for 7 days in the Peoria area, plus you load it yourself.
  • Overage fees apply if you exceed the weight limit (common with garages — scrap metal and concrete add up fast).
  • You still have to haul everything out to the dumpster, which is most of the work.

For a single-day garage cleanout where you want it done and gone, our full-service approach is usually comparable in cost and saves your weekend. For a multi-day renovation with ongoing debris, a dumpster might make more sense. We'll tell you honestly which way to go when you call.
