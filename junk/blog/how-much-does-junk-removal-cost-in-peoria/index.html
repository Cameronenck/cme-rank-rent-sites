How Much Does Junk Removal Cost in Peoria IL? [2026 Pricing Guide]

Updated May 2026 • 6 min read • By Peoria Junk Removal Pros

If you've been sitting on a pile of old furniture, appliance junk, or leftover construction debris, you've probably wondered what it'll cost to make it disappear. Good news: junk removal in Peoria is pretty straightforward once you know how the pricing works. Bad news: a lot of companies online give you national averages that have nothing to do with what you'll actually pay here in the 309.

We're a Peoria-based junk removal company, and we run these jobs every day. Here's the honest breakdown.

What You'll Pay: Junk Removal Pricing in Peoria IL

Most junk removal companies — us included — price by volume. We show up, size up the load, and quote you based on how much truck space it takes up. No hourly rate, no per-item nonsense. Here's what that looks like in real dollars:

What Affects the Final Price

Volume is the main driver, but a few other things can bump your quote up or down:

  • How heavy is it? Concrete, tile, dirt, and roofing shingles are dense. They take up less visual space but cost more to haul and dump. A small pile of concrete can run close to a half-truck load in price.
  • Where is it located? Stuff sitting in the driveway or garage takes five minutes. Junk in a second-floor bedroom or tight crawlspace takes longer. Labor matters.
  • Does it need to be dismantled? A sectional sofa that won't fit through the door costs more to remove than one sitting in the front yard already.
  • Any hazardous materials? Paint cans, propane tanks, batteries — these can't go in our truck. We'll tell you upfront rather than hit you with surprise fees.
  • Timing. Same-day service sometimes carries a small rush fee, though not always. Ask when you call.

What's Included in the Price

When you book with us, the quote covers everything: loading, hauling, disposal fees, and cleanup. You don't lift a finger. We pull it out of wherever it is, carry it to the truck, and take it away. The only thing that's not included is anything you change your mind about after we've loaded — so walk through the job with your crew at the start and flag anything you're unsure about.

Estate and Full Property Cleanouts

These are a different animal. If you're dealing with a parent's house, a rental property that got trashed, or years of accumulated stuff, we quote the whole job rather than load by load. Prices vary a lot here — a clean three-bedroom house is different from a packed basement that hasn't been touched in 20 years.

For big cleanouts, call us to walk through the property first. We'll give you a flat-rate number so there are no surprises.

How to Get the Best Price on Junk Removal in Peoria

  • Group your junk. If you have stuff in three rooms, pile it all in one spot or in the garage. Less labor = lower price.
  • Be upfront about what you have. Describe the heaviest items when you call. Surprises slow the job down and can affect the final number.
  • Don't rent a dumpster for a one-time cleanout. Most dumpster rentals cost $300–500 and you still have to do the loading yourself. Junk removal often comes out cheaper once you factor in your time.
  • Ask about donation pickup. Items in good shape — furniture, working appliances, clothes — we try to drop at local nonprofits. Less dump weight can mean a slightly lower price.

Frequently Asked Questions

What factors affect junk removal cost the most?

Volume is the biggest one — the more truck space your stuff takes up, the more you pay. After that, it's weight (heavy materials like concrete cost more to dump), location of the junk (second floor vs. driveway), and whether anything needs to be broken down before loading. Special items like tires, paint, or electronics can also add to the price.

Can you do same-day junk removal in Peoria?

Yes, most of the time. If you call before noon, there's a solid chance we can get out the same day. We can't always guarantee it during our busiest weeks, but we'll always tell you straight what's available when you call. Text or call 309-861-9815 for current availability.

What items won't you take?

We can't haul hazardous waste: paint, chemicals, asbestos, propane tanks (unless empty), medical waste, or large amounts of oil. Tires are a maybe — ask when you call. Pretty much everything else is fair game: appliances, furniture, yard waste, construction debris, electronics, hot tubs, you name it.

How can I lower my junk removal bill?

A few things help: move the junk to one central spot so loading is faster, separate anything that's clearly donatable (we'll often haul donation-ready items at a reduced rate), and book during our slower midweek slots rather than weekend rush. Also — don't wait until the load doubles before calling. Smaller, more frequent pickups can actually cost less per trip than one massive cleanout.
