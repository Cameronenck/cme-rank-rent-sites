What Drives the Price Up (or Down)

The quoted price you see online is rarely what you'll pay when a truck shows up. Here's what actually moves the needle on appliance removal costs in Peoria:

Location Within the Home

Ground floor appliances with a clear path to the door are the simplest jobs. Basement hauls or second-floor removals take more crew time and physical effort. We typically charge $25 to $50 per appliance for staircase access, which reflects the real labor cost of safely carrying a 200-pound refrigerator up a narrow stairwell without damaging walls or floors.

Refrigerant Recovery Requirements

Any appliance that contains Freon — refrigerators, chest freezers, window AC units, and some dehumidifiers — requires certified refrigerant recovery before it can be legally disposed of. This adds $20 to $40 to the job but it's non-negotiable under EPA regulations. We're fully certified and handle this as part of the service so you stay compliant.

Quantity and Bundling

If you're clearing out a kitchen, laundry room, or vacant rental property with multiple appliances, bundled pricing almost always applies. Moving three or four items in a single trip is significantly more efficient than four separate pickups, and we pass that savings on to you. A full kitchen worth of appliances — fridge, stove, dishwasher, and microwave — might run $225 to $350 total rather than paying retail for each piece individually.

Appliance Size and Weight

Commercial-grade or oversized appliances like side-by-side refrigerators, industrial dryers, or large chest freezers fall outside standard pricing. These require extra crew or equipment and are quoted individually.

How We Handle Appliance Disposal in Peoria

We don't just load appliances onto a truck and dump them at the nearest landfill. Appliances contain recoverable materials — steel, copper, aluminum — that can be recycled. Refrigerants are recovered and processed through EPA-certified channels. Whatever can be donated to a local resale organization goes there first, since a working washer or dryer still has real value for someone in need.

Once we've sorted what can be recycled or donated, the remainder goes to a licensed disposal facility. You're not creating an illegal dumping problem when you hire us — everything ends up where it's supposed to go.

Appliance Removal vs. Retailer Haul-Away

If you're buying a new appliance from a big-box store, you've probably seen the "haul away old appliance for $25" add-on. That sounds cheap until you read the fine print. Retailer haul-away typically only applies to like-for-like replacements — they take the old fridge when they deliver the new one — and it often doesn't cover items with Freon issues, appliances not on the same floor as the delivery point, or multiple items. We handle all of that without the restrictions.

For standalone removal of appliances you already own — with no new delivery involved — a local junk removal company is almost always faster, more flexible, and more capable than retailer programs.
