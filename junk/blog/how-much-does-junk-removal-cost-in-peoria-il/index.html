What Actually Drives Up the Cost

Volume is the biggest factor. The more stuff you have, the more it costs. A bedroom with a few boxes costs way less than a bedroom packed floor to ceiling. We're charging based on truck space and disposal weight, so the more material, the higher the bill.

Type and weight of items matter a lot. A truckload of light cardboard boxes is different from a truckload of old office furniture and filing cabinets. Appliances like refrigerators, washers, and stoves have disposal fees built in—they contain hazardous materials and can't just go to the landfill. A fridge might add $150–$250 to your cost. Same with air conditioning units, water heaters, and old TVs.

Construction debris and yard waste cost more. If you're clearing out drywall, lumber, concrete chunks, or asphalt, that's specialized disposal. It weighs a ton—literally—and the facilities that take construction debris charge differently than general waste. Yard debris—brush, branches, grass clippings—also has dedicated disposal locations. Budget higher if you're dealing with this.

Accessibility makes a difference. A single-story ranch with a garage you can pull right up to? Easy. A third-floor apartment with a narrow stairwell? That's slower and harder on your crew. A basement with only one exit? Same deal. If junk has to be carried 50 feet through the house versus loaded directly from the door, it takes more time. We price accordingly.

Distance from Peoria affects cost too. We charge standard rates within Peoria city limits and immediate suburbs (East Peoria, Peoria Heights, Morton). If you're way out in the county—20+ minutes from our base—there's a fuel surcharge. Nothing crazy, but it adds up for rural jobs.

Hazardous materials add cost. Old paint cans, batteries, fluorescent bulbs, oil, or unknown chemicals? That's hazmat. We have to dispose of it at specialized facilities, and that costs more. If you don't know if something's hazmat, tell us and we'll ID it on-site. No surprises.

Ways to Save on Junk Removal in Peoria

Sort and separate before we come. Go through everything yourself. Put the donatable stuff in a pile, the recyclable items in another, and the junk in a third. We still haul it all, but you've cut the mental labor. When you're organized, the job moves faster and costs less—usually saves you 15–25%.

Remove hazardous items yourself. Take old paint cans, batteries, and chemicals to the Peoria hazmat disposal day yourself. It's usually free or cheap at the county, and it saves you from paying our hazmat surcharge. One or two hazmat items might only add $30–$50, but a whole pile adds up.

Donate or sell items before calling us. List free stuff on Facebook Marketplace or Craigslist—people love free. Sell furniture in decent shape on Marketplace. Goodwill will pick up large donations for free in Peoria (call them directly, they handle it). By the time we arrive, you've cut the volume and the cost.

Bundle jobs together. Cleaning out the garage, basement, AND attic? One visit, one truck, one crew. It's more efficient, and we discount bundled work by 10–15% because we're not making three separate trips. Call us with the full scope upfront.

Schedule mid-week. Tuesday through Thursday are our slowest days. If you can schedule for 9 AM to 2 PM on a weekday, we sometimes offer same-week discounts. Weekends and afternoons are busier, so we don't discount them.

What Happens to Your Junk After We Haul It?

We're not just dumping everything at the landfill—that's not how responsible companies operate in 2026. Here's where stuff goes: metal gets sorted and sold to recycling facilities. General junk that can't be recycled gets disposed of responsibly at permitted facilities. You're actually helping the environment by hiring us instead of renting a dumpster and throwing everything away yourself.
