Yard Debris Removal Cost Peoria IL — Local Pricing Guide

Updated June 2026 • 8 min read • By Peoria Junk Removal Pros

Spring storms drop trees. Landscaping projects leave brush and wood scraps. Winter cleanup reveals a backyard full of branches and mulch overflow. Then comes the question: how much is it going to cost to haul it all away?

If you're looking for yard debris removal in Peoria IL, I'll give you straight numbers. Most jobs run between $200 and $1,200 depending on how much material you've got and whether it's just branches or a major cleanout. Here's what actually drives the cost.

What Yard Debris Removal Actually Costs in Peoria

The biggest factor is volume — how much space the debris takes up. A small pile of branches might fill a quarter of a truck bed. A full yard cleanup with downed trees, brush, and construction waste can need a full load or two.

Here's what a typical Peoria job breaks down to:

Job Type Volume Typical Price
Small branch pile + leaves Quarter truck $200–$350
Typical yard cleanup Half truck $400–$600
Full yard + fallen tree removal Full truck $700–$1,000
Large property or multiple trees Two+ trucks $1,100–$1,500

The key: we quote by truckload, not by the hour. You get a flat rate after we see what needs to go. No surprises when the invoice arrives.

What We Take and What We Don't

Yes, we haul: Branches, fallen trees, tree stumps, brush piles, leaves in bulk, grass clippings, wood mulch, yard waste, wood pallets, broken fencing, and landscaping debris. If it came from your yard or a landscaping project, we take it.

We can't take: Hazardous materials like pesticides, fertilizers, paint, propane tanks, or treated lumber that might have been chemically preserved. We'll spot those during the walkthrough so there are no surprises.

Same-Day Pickup Is Usually Possible

Unlike a dumpster rental that takes 5–7 days to arrive, yard debris pickup can often happen the same day if you're in our normal service area and call before 2 PM. If we're already running a route in your neighborhood, we can swing by and clear out the pile without you having to coordinate a separate time.

If it's late in the day or we're fully booked, we'll schedule you within 48 hours — usually less. Call 309-861-9815 to check same-day availability.

What Happens to the Debris?

Most of it goes to a composting facility in the Peoria area rather than straight to the landfill. Branches and organic matter break down into compost that local landscape companies use. If there's salvageable wood, some of it gets delivered to local contractors. We divert what we can away from the dump.

Yard Debris Removal vs. Dumpster Rental

A dumpster rental for yard waste costs $200–$400 upfront, plus $400–$600 in disposal fees once it's full — so you're looking at $600–$1,000 total. The dumpster sits in your driveway for a week while you haul material into it yourself, which is sweat and time on your end.

A debris pickup costs what you see upfront, the truck shows up, and it's gone. No dumpster cluttering the driveway. No hauling on your back. Choose dumpster rental if you're doing a slow multi-week cleanup. Choose pickup if you've got piles ready to go and want it done in a day.

Preparing Your Yard for Pickup

You don't need to do much. Pile the debris where our crew can access it safely. Branches and logs stacked together, leaves in piles, brush grouped up — that's ideal. If material is scattered across the yard, we can still grab it, but consolidating saves a few minutes and can help us fit more into one trip.

For larger jobs or anything with a tree down, we'll do a walkthrough to make sure we understand the scope before we quote. That protects both of us.
