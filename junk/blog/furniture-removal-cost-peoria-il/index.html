Furniture Removal Cost in Peoria IL — Real Pricing, No Surprises

Updated May 2026 • 6 min read • By Peoria Junk Removal Pros

Getting rid of old furniture in Peoria is one of those jobs that sounds simple until you're standing in front of a sleeper sofa that weighs 300 pounds and won't fit through the doorway. Whether you're clearing a bedroom after a move, downsizing after retirement, or cleaning out a rental property between tenants, furniture removal has a specific cost structure — and knowing it upfront saves you from unpleasant surprises.

The short version: single-item furniture pickups in Peoria run about $75 to $175. Multi-item loads go by volume and typically land between $175 and $450. Here's what actually moves that number up or down.

What Drives Furniture Removal Pricing in Peoria

Every quote we give starts with the same question: how much truck space are we talking about? Junk removal is priced by volume — the fraction of the truck your items fill — not by the hour. That's how you get a flat rate before we start instead of a growing clock on the wall.

  • Item count and size. A single recliner is a quick load. A bedroom set — dresser, bed frame, headboard, two nightstands, and a mattress — fills about a quarter truck. The more you have, the more volume, the higher the price.
  • Mattresses. Mattresses get their own line because disposal costs more. Recycling or proper disposal of a mattress adds $25–$50 to the base quote. This is legitimate — landfills in the Peoria area have restrictions, and responsible disposal matters.
  • Weight. Most furniture is light-to-moderate, but solid wood dressers, antique buffets, and cast-iron beds are exceptions. Heavy items take more crew time and effort. We price for the work, not just the space.
  • Access. Ground floor with a clear path is the easiest situation. Basement furniture, second-floor bedrooms with narrow staircases, or items blocked by other stuff add time and that's reflected in the price.
  • Same-day vs. scheduled. Scheduled pickups are typically at the lower end of the range. Same-day or next-day service has a small premium but we make it work most days.

What Happens to Your Furniture After Pickup

We don't just haul everything to the landfill. Furniture in decent shape goes to local Peoria-area organizations — Habitat for Humanity ReStore on Sterling Avenue, local church donation programs, and community centers that accept usable goods. If it can find a second life, we try to make that happen.

Items that are too worn for donation get broken down and sorted for recycling where possible. Metal frames, wooden components, and foam cushioning all have different disposal streams. What's left goes to the transfer station. The point is: we do this right, not just fast.

What We Can't Take

Almost all furniture is fair game. The short list of items we can't haul: anything containing hazardous materials (furniture with pressurized components, like old refrigerators — those go through appliance recycling separately), items with verified pest infestations like active bedbugs, and anything the city landfill won't accept. We'll tell you upfront on the call if something falls into a special category. No surprises when we arrive.
