What Makes Furniture Removal Expensive?

A few factors can increase the cost. Hauling a couch from a second-floor bedroom costs more than grabbing it from the living room—those narrow hallways and tight staircases take time and sometimes require disassembly. Water damage, animal damage, or fabric deterioration doesn't change the price, but basement access or outdoor sheds on difficult terrain might add a surcharge. We're honest about that upfront.

Distance from your location to the disposal site also affects cost, but in Peoria we have nearby drop-off points, so that's usually minimal. And here's the thing most people don't realize: we do the heavy lifting so you don't have to. Your time is valuable, and trying to move a 300-pound couch yourself or asking neighbors to help isn't worth the back pain.

Why Choose Professional Furniture Removal Over DIY?

I get the urge to save money by doing it yourself. But consider what you're actually saving. Renting a truck is $30–80 per day. Gas runs another $20–40. If something gets damaged during the move, that repair bill can easily exceed what professional removal would've cost. And if you get hurt moving heavy furniture, that's medical bills and lost time that no amount of savings justified.

When you call us, we handle everything: disassembly if needed, careful navigation through your home, loading, transport, and responsible disposal. We even try to donate items in good condition to local charities rather than sending them straight to the landfill. That peace of mind is worth the cost.

Can You Remove Anything?

Pretty much. Couches, beds, dressers, tables, bookshelves, cabinets, office furniture, recliners, sectionals, sleeper sofas, futons, ottomans, bar stools, entertainment centers. The only real limitation is if something's too large to fit through the house or if the access is genuinely dangerous. We'll be honest if that's the case.
