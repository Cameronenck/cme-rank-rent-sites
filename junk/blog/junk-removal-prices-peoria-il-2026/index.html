What Pushes Your Price Up

Volume is the baseline, but these factors can bump a quote higher:

  • Heavy materials. Concrete chunks, tile, brick, dirt, and roofing shingles are priced differently because they're hauled by weight at the dump. A small pile of busted concrete can cost as much as a half-truck of furniture. Tell me upfront if you've got anything dense.
  • Tight access. Junk in the garage costs less to remove than junk on the third floor, in a crawlspace, or through a narrow hallway. Factor in stairs, distance from our truck, and tight turns.
  • Disassembly needed. A sectional that won't fit through your door has to be taken apart first. A workbench bolted to the floor takes extra time. We'll tell you if it affects the price before we start.
  • Weekend timing. Saturday mornings are our busiest window. We try to keep pricing consistent, but if we're slammed, it can affect same-day availability and occasionally pricing.
  • Specialty items. Hot tubs, safes, pianos, and pool tables take extra crew and equipment. These get quoted individually — give me a heads-up when you call.

What Brings Your Price Down

There are real ways to trim the final number:

  • Move it to the curb or driveway. If your junk is staged and ready to load when we pull up, we're in and out faster. Less labor time can mean a lower quote.
  • Consolidate your pile. Junk scattered across three rooms costs more than one organized stack in the garage. Do the internal hauling yourself and you'll save money.
  • Let us donate what we can. Furniture, working appliances, and good-condition household items can go to local nonprofits instead of the landfill. Less dump weight = lower disposal cost, and we pass some of that back.
  • Book a weekday. Tuesday through Thursday appointments usually get the base rate and a tighter arrival window. Weekday slots also mean you're not competing with weekend rush bookings.
  • Book early. Last-minute same-day calls sometimes carry a small rush fee when we have to rearrange the schedule. Calling the day before or in the morning for an afternoon slot usually avoids it.

How 2026 Prices Compare to Last Year

If you used a junk removal service in Peoria in 2024 or 2025, expect to pay roughly $15–40 more for the same load this year. The jump comes from two places: the Peoria County landfill raised tipping rates in January 2026, and diesel fuel is running higher than the historical average. Neither of those is going back down anytime soon, so I wouldn't count on prices dropping in the second half of the year.

The good news: the price increase is smaller than what happened between 2022 and 2023. Things have stabilized — we're just at a new normal floor.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why did junk removal prices go up in 2026?

Landfill tipping fees in Central Illinois climbed again this year, and fuel hasn't come back down to where it was. Those two costs get baked directly into what we charge. Most reputable companies in Peoria raised rates 5–10% over 2025. If someone's quoting you 2023 prices, they're either cutting corners on disposal or they'll hit you with add-ons at the end.

Is junk removal cheaper on weekdays in Peoria?

Yes, usually. Weekends fill up fast — especially Saturday mornings — so we sometimes add a small weekend rate. Booking a Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday slot almost always gets you the base price. It also means a faster arrival window since our schedule isn't as packed.

Do you charge extra for heavy items like concrete or tile?

Heavy materials — concrete, tile, brick, dirt, roofing shingles — are priced by weight, not volume. A wheelbarrow of broken concrete is dense enough to push you into a higher price bracket even though it looks small. Let us know upfront if your load includes any heavy materials and we'll quote accordingly so there are no surprises.

Can I get a price estimate over the phone without anyone coming out?

For most standard jobs — appliance pickup, furniture removal, garage cleanouts up to half a truck — yes, absolutely. Describe what you have, where it's located, and roughly how much, and we'll give you a range on the spot. For large estate cleanouts or anything that sounds complicated, we'll do a free in-person look before quoting.

What's the cheapest way to get junk removed in Peoria?

Move your items to the driveway or curb before we arrive — less labor means a lower final price. Group everything in one pile instead of scattered across multiple rooms. Separate anything donation-worthy; items we can drop at a local nonprofit often get a slight discount since they don't go to the landfill. And book early in the week to avoid weekend premiums.
