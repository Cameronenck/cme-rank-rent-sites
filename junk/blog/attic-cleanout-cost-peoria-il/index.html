Here's the thing most people don't realize: attic cleanouts aren't just about hauling junk out. You're paying for several services bundled together. Let me break it down based on what my crew actually does on every job.

What's Included in an Attic Cleanout?

Labor & Removal

Most of our attic cleanouts run 4-8 hours depending on size and what's up there. We're not just throwing things down from the attic—we're carefully removing everything, sorting salvageable items, bagging debris, and loading it all into our truck. That's skilled labor, and in Peoria, you're looking at $45-$65 per hour per crew member. A typical crew is 2-3 people, so basic labor is $360-$1,560 depending on job length.

Disposal Costs

Landfill tipping fees and hauling add up. Old insulation, asbestos (if present—which is a whole different cost), drywall dust, rodent droppings, and general debris all have disposal costs. This typically runs $200-$800 depending on tonnage and what contamination is present. If we find asbestos (common in older Peoria homes), you're adding $500-$2,000 for certified removal.

Equipment & Access

If your attic access is limited—which it usually is in Peoria homes—we bring in drop cloths, tarps, dust barriers, and sometimes a small dumpster to contain debris while we work. This protects your home and speeds up the job. Budget $150-$300 for access setup.

Critter Cleanup & Sanitation

Probably half the attics I walk into have evidence of rodents or bats. We handle cleanup, disposal of fecal matter, and sometimes recommend pest treatment. This adds $200-$600 to the bill but saves you serious health hazards.

Cost Factors That Drive the Price Up

  • Asbestos or lead paint: +$500-$2,000 (requires certified disposal)
  • Water damage or mold: +$300-$1,500 (containment required)
  • Fiberglass insulation replacement: +$800-$2,500 (if you want new insulation installed)
  • Structural repairs discovered: +$500+ (roof leaks, rot in joists, etc.)
  • Biohazard cleanup (rodent/bat droppings): +$200-$600
  • Difficult access or tight spaces: +$200-$500 (hand-removal vs. bulk removal)

Can You Save Money on an Attic Cleanout?

Yes, but do it smartly. Here's what works and what doesn't:

What works: Remove smaller items yourself before we arrive. Separate anything hazardous. Get multiple quotes so you know the market rate. Schedule during our slower seasons (winter) for potential discounts.

What doesn't work: Trying to DIY a full attic cleanout solo—you'll underestimate time and cost quickly. Using unlicensed services (you're liable for illegal dumping). Assuming all removals cost the same (they don't—structural hazards add significant cost).

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does an attic cleanout take?
Typically 4-8 hours depending on size and debris volume. Small, straightforward attics: 4-5 hours. Large or complex attics with hazards: 8+ hours. We quote the timeline after an in-person inspection.
Do you handle asbestos or lead paint?
We identify hazardous materials and connect you with certified abatement specialists. We don't remove asbestos directly, but we coordinate the full process. This adds $500-$2,000 but protects your health.
What about items that are salvageable?
We sort during removal. Anything valuable or reusable we can donate, recycle, or keep for you. This sometimes offsets a small portion of the removal cost through tax deductions.
Do I need to be home during the cleanout?
No, but we recommend an inspection visit first so you can walk through what's going. The actual cleanout can happen while you're not there. We'll secure everything and leave your home locked.
Will you come back if we find more stuff later?
Absolutely. If you discover additional items after the initial cleanout, we can do a second pass. Most jobs are one-and-done, but follow-ups are straightforward to schedule.
Are you licensed and insured?
Yes. We're fully licensed, insured, and bonded in Peoria. We protect your home and our crew with comprehensive coverage. Ask for certificates—any reputable service should provide them.
