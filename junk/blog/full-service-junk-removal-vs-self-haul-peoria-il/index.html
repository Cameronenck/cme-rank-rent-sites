Full Service Junk Removal vs Self Haul in Peoria IL — Which One Actually Saves You Money?

Updated May 2026 • 6 min read • By Peoria Junk Removal Pros

I get this question a lot: "How hard can it be to just load up my truck and take it to the dump myself?" Honestly, for certain jobs, it's not hard at all and it makes perfect sense. But for others, the DIY route quietly costs you more than hiring a crew would have — in money, in time, and occasionally in a strained back or a friend who stopped answering your calls after the sofa incident.

I've been doing junk removal in Peoria IL for years. I've seen both sides of this. Here's the honest breakdown of when self-haul works, when it doesn't, and what each option actually costs when you add everything up.

What Full Service Junk Removal Actually Includes

When you hire a full service crew, you're paying for labor, a truck, and disposal — all bundled together. You point at the stuff, we load it, we haul it, we take it to the transfer station or donation center or recycling facility, and that's it. You don't touch anything, you don't make any trips, and you don't deal with dump fees separately.

Full service makes the most sense when:

  • You're dealing with heavy items — Refrigerators, sectional sofas, mattresses, treadmills, hot tubs. These aren't one-person jobs, and they're not pickup-truck jobs. Trying to muscle a fridge down stairs alone is how people end up at OSF.
  • You have a lot of stuff — Anything over a pickup truck's worth gets expensive fast when you're doing it yourself. Multiple dump runs add up in gas and dump fees, and you're spending half your Saturday on I-74.
  • You have mixed loads — If you've got regular junk plus appliances plus electronics plus a tire or two, you can't legally dump all of that in one place. A licensed crew handles mixed loads with the right certifications and disposal channels.
  • You're on a deadline — Estate closings, rental turnovers, contractor timelines. When the house has to be empty by Friday, DIY is too risky. Crews work on your schedule, not around the dump's hours.

When Self-Haul Makes Sense in Peoria

I'm not going to pretend you always need to hire us. If you've got a small, manageable amount of junk, a truck you can use, and a few hours free, doing it yourself can absolutely pencil out.

Self-haul works when:

  • It's genuinely one pickup load or less — A few bags of garage clutter, some old boxes, a couple of chairs. If it fits in one trip and you can load it yourself without risking injury, haul it.
  • Everything is already staged near the curb or garage door — If you've done the work to organize and move things to a staging area, the remaining effort shrinks. The loading and driving part is the easy part.
  • Nothing is hazardous or appliance-grade — Regular household junk, furniture without refrigerants, yard waste — the Peoria County transfer station on Sterling Avenue handles all of that. Budget about $50–$80 for a standard truck load.
  • You actually have the time — Saturday morning dump run, you've got the truck, nobody is waiting on you. This is the scenario where DIY makes sense. If you're stealing hours from work or pushing into family time, the math changes.

The Items That Wreck Self-Haul Plans

There are specific items that almost always push people from "I'll do it myself" to "okay, I need a crew." Here's what catches people off guard most often in Peoria:

  • Refrigerators and freezers — The transfer station won't take them unless the refrigerant is already removed. Certified refrigerant recovery is required by federal law. We handle this every week — you can't just dump a fridge.
  • Old TVs and monitors — CRT televisions and older monitors are e-waste. Most landfills won't accept them. The Peoria County recycling programs have limited hours and charge fees. We route these to proper e-waste channels.
  • Mattresses — Some transfer stations accept them, some don't depending on the day. Mattresses are bulky and awkward to transport. Getting one down a staircase alone is miserable. This is the item that makes more people call us than almost anything else.
  • Construction debris — If you've got drywall, concrete, or mixed renovation debris, self-haul gets complicated fast. Heavy material adds significant weight charges at the dump, and sorting requirements vary.

The Honest Answer

If you have a truck, the strength, the time, and a straightforward single-load situation — do it yourself and pocket the savings. I'd rather be straight with you than talk you into calling us when you don't need to.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it cheaper to haul junk yourself in Peoria IL?

On paper, yes. In practice, it depends heavily on how much you have and whether you factor in your own time, dump fees, vehicle wear, and the physical labor involved. For a single truckload or less, self-haul can pencil out if you already have a truck and a few hours free. Once you're looking at more than a pickup load — or items that need two people to move — full service usually wins on total cost when you account for everything.

What does it cost to dump a load yourself at the Peoria transfer station?

Peoria County charges by weight at their transfer station on Sterling Avenue. Rates run roughly $50–$80 for a standard pickup load, more for heavier debris like concrete or drywall. You also need to haul it yourself during operating hours, which are limited on weekends. Add in gas, your time, and any help you need loading, and the savings shrink fast.

What items can't I haul to the Peoria dump myself?

The Peoria County transfer station doesn't accept hazardous waste, tires, electronics, or appliances with refrigerants — fridges, freezers, AC units, and dehumidifiers. Those require special certified disposal. A licensed junk removal crew handles refrigerant recovery and proper e-waste disposal as part of the job, so you don't have to track down separate drop-off locations.

How long does a full service junk removal job take in Peoria?

Most residential jobs — a room, a garage, a basement — run 45 minutes to two hours depending on how much there is and how accessible the space is. An estate cleanout on a full house might be a half day or a full day. Either way, you're not lifting a finger. The crew loads, hauls, and disposes of everything while you do something else.

Is full service junk removal worth it for a small load in Peoria?

For a single piece of furniture or one appliance, self-haul might be worth the effort if you have a truck and can do it yourself safely. But if the item is heavy — a refrigerator, a sectional sofa, a treadmill — or you don't have a truck, paying $80–$150 for a crew to come get it is almost always the right call. Pulled backs and rental truck fees add up fast.
