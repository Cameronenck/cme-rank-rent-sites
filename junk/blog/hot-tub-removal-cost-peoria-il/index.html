Hot Tub Removal Cost Peoria IL – 2026 Pricing Guide

📅 May 19, 2026 📍 Peoria, IL ⏱ 5 min read

Getting rid of a hot tub is a completely different ballgame from tossing a couch or a few boxes of old furniture. Hot tubs weigh anywhere from 300 to over 800 pounds empty — and they're not exactly designed to roll out of your backyard on wheels. Factor in disconnecting the plumbing and electrical, potentially cutting apart the shell to fit through a gate, and you've got a job that requires the right equipment and experience. This guide breaks down exactly what hot tub removal costs in Peoria, IL in 2026 and what's included in the service.

How Much Does Hot Tub Removal Cost in Peoria IL?

Pricing depends primarily on the size and weight of the hot tub, how accessible it is, and whether the deck around it needs to be partially demolished to get it out. Here's a realistic look at what Peoria homeowners typically pay:

Hot Tub Type Approx. Weight Typical Cost
Small / 2-person hot tub Under 300 lbs $200 – $350
Standard / 4-6 person hot tub 300 – 600 lbs $300 – $500
Large spa / 7+ person 600+ lbs $450 – $700
Deck demolition (if required) — +$150 – $300

These prices include draining, disconnecting plumbing and electrical, breaking down the shell if necessary, loading, and full haul-away. There are no dump fees tacked on at the end — what you're quoted is what you pay. Factors that can push the price toward the higher end include tight access (narrow side gates, fenced yards), units that are still full of water, or situations where the hot tub has been in place for years and is partially embedded in a deck structure.

What's Included in Hot Tub Removal?

A full-service hot tub removal from Peoria Junk Removal Pros covers every step of the process — you don't need to do any prep:

  • Draining: We drain all remaining water from the tub before attempting to move it. A full standard hot tub can hold 400–500 gallons, so this step is non-negotiable before any lift.
  • Plumbing disconnection: We disconnect and cap off any plumbing lines running to the unit. If you have a permanent gas or electric hookup, we coordinate the safe disconnect (or advise when a licensed electrician is needed for 240V hardwired units).
  • On-site cutting: Many hot tubs won't fit through a standard 36-inch gate intact. When that's the case, we use reciprocating saws to section the shell into manageable pieces on-site. This is standard practice and is included in the removal cost — not a surprise add-on.
  • Loading and haul-away: All pieces, the cabinet panels, the cover, and any associated equipment are loaded into our truck and hauled to a licensed disposal or recycling facility in the greater Peoria area.

The whole process typically takes 1–2 hours for a standard hot tub with reasonable access. Larger units or complex deck situations may run 2–3 hours.

Hot Tub Removal vs. DIY — Why Most Homeowners Hire Out

Some homeowners look at a hot tub removal and think: "How hard can it be?" The answer is: surprisingly hard and genuinely dangerous if you're not prepared. Here's why most Peoria residents who've tried to DIY this job end up calling a pro:

  • The weight is deceptive. Even a "small" 2-person hot tub weighs 250–350 lbs empty. A standard 6-person unit can hit 600+ lbs. Moving that across a yard — especially through a gate or over landscaping — without proper equipment and crew almost always results in property damage or injury.
  • Electrical is a real hazard. Many hot tubs are hardwired to a 240V GFCI disconnect. Cutting or pulling on electrical connections without knowing what you're doing puts you at serious risk. Even if the breaker is off, mistakes happen.
  • Cutting up the shell is messy and requires tools. Fiberglass and acrylic hot tub shells are thick. Cutting them generates sharp debris and requires the right blade selection and protective gear. Getting it wrong turns a manageable job into a half-day frustration.
  • Disposal isn't free. You still have to get it to a facility that will accept it. Not all waste sites take hot tubs, and renting a trailer plus dump fees can easily run $100–$200 before you've done any actual labor.

The math usually works out close to even on costs — but a pro crew finishes the job in a couple of hours without risk to you, your yard, or your back. For most Peoria homeowners, that's worth it.
