Estate Cleanout Cost in Peoria IL — What You'll Actually Pay

Updated May 2026 • 7 min read • By Peoria Junk Removal Pros

Estate cleanouts are one of the most emotionally and logistically difficult jobs a family deals with. You're already handling grief, probate paperwork, and a tight real estate timeline — and now you need to figure out what it's going to cost to clear out a house full of decades worth of furniture, clothing, appliances, and everything in between. I'll give you straight numbers from someone who does these jobs in Peoria every week.

The short answer: most estate cleanouts in Peoria IL run between $350 and $2,800, depending on how big the home is and how much is inside it. I'll break down exactly what drives that number up or down so you can budget before you ever call anyone.

What Drives Estate Cleanout Pricing in Peoria

Every estate cleanout quote starts with volume — how much cubic footage of material are we loading? That's what determines how many truck loads we need, and truck loads are the biggest cost driver. Here's what else factors in:

  • Property size. A 900 sq ft ranch home on Knoxville typically takes about half a day. A 2,400 sq ft two-story on the North Side with a full basement and two-car garage can easily fill two full trucks and take a full crew all day.
  • How packed it is. Some estates are partially cleared by family before we arrive — that brings the cost down. Some are untouched for years, every closet full, every room stacked. The latter costs more because volume equals more truck runs to the transfer station.
  • Heavy or special items. Piano removal, gun safes, and large riding mowers take extra crew and sometimes special equipment. These typically add $100–$300 to the base quote depending on what's involved.
  • Access. Ground-floor loading is straightforward. Narrow stairways, low ceilings, or items that have to come out through a second-floor window add time and complexity. We price for this during the walkthrough, not as a surprise at the end.
  • How much sorting is needed. If the family has already separated keep-pile from haul-pile, we're faster and cheaper. If everything is mixed together and needs sorting, that adds time. We can sort if needed — just know that it's reflected in the price.

How Our Estate Cleanout Process Works

I get calls from families who've had bad experiences with other junk haulers during estate situations. Usually it's one of two problems: the price changed dramatically once the truck was half full, or the crew showed up and disappeared when things got complicated. Here's how we handle it:

Walkthrough first. Before we quote anything, we walk the property with you — or with whoever is managing the estate if family isn't local. We look at every room, the basement, the garage, the attic if there's one. That walk is what our flat-rate quote is based on.

Flat rate, in writing. After the walkthrough, you get a firm number. That's the price. If we underestimated and it takes longer, that's on us. If you've already had family come through and there's less than we thought, we adjust down. We don't penalize you for something being easier than expected.

We donate what we can. Furniture, clothing, kitchenware, working appliances — anything in good shape goes to local Peoria nonprofits instead of the landfill. This doesn't change your price, but it's the right thing to do and it keeps usable goods in the community.

Full cleanup. When we leave, the house should be broom-clean. We're not leaving trash bags in corners, debris in the driveway, or anything behind that wasn't there when we arrived. Real estate agents and attorneys appreciate this because it means the property is ready for the next step without another contractor call.

What We Take — and What We Can't

We take nearly everything in an estate: furniture of all kinds, mattresses, appliances, clothing, books, boxes, tools, garden equipment, construction materials left behind, TVs, electronics, and general household junk. The short list of items we can't haul includes hazardous materials — things like:

  • Paint cans with liquid paint remaining
  • Propane tanks
  • Chemicals, solvents, and pesticides
  • Certain older electronics with hazardous components (we'll tell you which ones)
  • Medical sharps or biohazardous waste

If any of these show up during the walkthrough, we'll flag them and point you to the right local disposal option. In Peoria County, the household hazardous waste program handles most of that — it's free and runs regularly. We make sure you know before we start, so nothing slows us down on the job day.

Comparing Estate Cleanout Options in Peoria

Some families consider doing it themselves with a rented dumpster. That works fine if you have the labor and the time, but on a typical estate cleanout timeline — where you've got a probate deadline or a real estate sale pending — renting a dumpster and filling it yourself over several weekends often isn't realistic. A full-service crew gets it done in one day, which means your real estate agent can get in faster and the sale can close sooner. That's usually worth more than whatever you'd save on labor.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does an estate cleanout cost in Peoria IL?

Most estate cleanouts in Peoria IL run between $350 and $2,800 depending on the size of the home and how full it is. A small home with modest contents might be $400–$700. A 4-bedroom house packed floor to ceiling can push past $2,000. We price by volume after a walkthrough — not by the hour — so you always know the number before we start.

Do you charge by the hour or by the load for estate cleanouts?

We charge by the load — meaning by how much space your items take up in the truck. This protects you from runaway hourly billing. After we walk the property, we give you a flat rate. That's the price you pay, no matter how long the job actually takes us.

Can you do an estate cleanout in one day?

Usually yes. Most single-family homes in Peoria can be cleared in a full day with a two-person crew. Very large or heavily packed properties may need two days. We'll tell you upfront after the walkthrough what the realistic timeline looks like.

Will you take absolutely everything, or are there items you won't haul?

We take almost everything: furniture, appliances, clothing, boxes, tools, yard equipment, mattresses, TVs, and construction debris. The only items we can't take are hazardous materials — things like paint, chemicals, propane tanks, and certain electronics that require special disposal. We'll flag those during the walkthrough so there are no surprises.

Do you donate items from estate cleanouts?

Yes, whenever possible. Furniture, clothing, kitchenware, and working appliances that are in decent shape go to local Peoria-area nonprofits instead of the landfill. This doesn't change your price, but it's the right thing to do — and it keeps usable stuff in the community.
