The real talk: If your mattress is on the second floor or basement, you're paying more because it takes longer and requires more labor. Basement mattress removal often costs 20–30% more than first-floor pickups.

Why Can't You Just Put It Out on Trash Day?

Here's the thing about Peoria curbside pickup: they won't take mattresses. City of Peoria waste management has a specific list of items they collect, and mattresses aren't on it. Part of it is logistics — a truck can't compress a mattress the way it does regular trash. Part of it is that mattresses end up at landfills that specifically accept bulk items, not standard waste streams.

If you try to leave a mattress out, it'll sit there until someone (usually the city or your landlord) complains, and then you're responsible for hauling it legally.

Your Actual Options for Getting Rid of a Mattress

Option 1: Professional Junk Removal (Most Common)

Call a junk removal crew like us, they show up, take the mattress away, dispose of it properly. Cost: $75–$200 depending on mattress size and location. Time: Usually same-day or next-day availability in Peoria. This is what most people do, and for good reason — it's simple and legal.

Option 2: Donation or Resale

If your mattress is still in decent condition, charities like Habitat for Humanity ReStores sometimes accept donations. You'll need to deliver it yourself in most cases. Cost: $0, but you do the work. Note: Many charities won't accept used mattresses due to hygiene concerns, so call ahead.

Option 3: Bulk Waste Drop-Off

Peoria has a bulk waste facility where you can drop off large items, including mattresses. You'll need transportation and to be able to handle it yourself. Cost: Usually $25–$40 for drop-off. Time: You control the timing, but you're doing the heavy lifting.

Option 4: Sanitation Truck Bulk Pickup

Some areas of Peoria offer quarterly bulk pickup — you schedule it in advance and leave the mattress at the curb. Cost: Often included in your garbage bill or a small fee ($10–$25). Time: You have to wait for the scheduled date.

What Happens to Your Mattress After We Take It?

A lot of people wonder. When we haul a mattress from your home, it goes to a licensed recycling or waste facility in the greater Peoria area. Mattresses are actually recyclable — the metal springs and frame can be salvaged, foam can be repurposed, and wood components get processed. It doesn't all end up in a landfill, though some material does.

When Should You Actually Remove Your Mattress?

Most mattresses last 7–10 years. Signs it's time: sagging, visible stains you can't remove, lumps, or just not sleeping well on it anymore. If you're replacing it with a new one, have the old one removed the same day if possible — one less thing to figure out.
