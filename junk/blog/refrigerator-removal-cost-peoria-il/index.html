Refrigerator Removal Cost in Peoria IL

Updated June 13, 2026 • 8 min read

When you need to get rid of an old refrigerator, the question most Peoria homeowners ask first is simple: how much is this going to cost?

I've been hauling junk in Peoria for over a decade, and refrigerator removal is one of the most common jobs I handle. Whether you're upgrading to a new fridge, clearing out a garage, or dealing with a unit that finally quit, you need a straightforward answer on what it'll cost and what to expect.

Here's what you need to know based on what we actually charge and what the removal process really looks like.

What's Included in the Price

When you call us to remove a refrigerator, here's what you're actually paying for:

Full Pickup and Haul

We come to your home, disconnect the refrigerator (if plugged in), and load it onto our truck. We handle all the heavy lifting—old fridges can weigh 200+ pounds, and getting them out of tight kitchens, down basements stairs, or through doorways takes experience. We've got the equipment and the know-how to do it without damaging your walls or flooring.

R-134a Refrigerant Recovery

Illinois law requires proper recovery and disposal of R-134a refrigerant before a refrigerator enters a landfill. This protects the ozone layer and the environment. The cost is built into our price—we don't charge you extra. We handle it, you don't have to think about it.

Responsible Disposal

Old refrigerators can't just go to the dump. We take them to a certified recycling facility where the metal, plastic, and glass are recovered, and the hazardous materials are handled safely. You get the environmental benefit without the hassle.

Same-Day Scheduling When Available

In most cases, if you call us before noon, we can pick up your fridge the same day. We don't make you wait around. If you need a specific time window, we work with you to fit your schedule.

Why Refrigerator Removal Costs What It Does

A lot of people are surprised by the cost. "It's just a fridge," they think. Here's why it's not that simple.

Weight and Logistics

A typical refrigerator weighs 150-300 pounds. Getting it out of a kitchen, into a truck, and to a facility requires proper equipment, not just brute force. You risk injury, property damage, or both if you try to DIY this.

Hazardous Material Handling

The refrigerant inside is not something you handle yourself. It's a regulated material. Improper disposal can result in fines for the property owner. We're licensed to handle it properly.

Fuel and Facility Costs

Our truck, our driver, and the recycling facility all cost money. When you hire us, you're paying for the full chain of responsible disposal, not just the pickup.

How to Save Money on Refrigerator Removal

Check for Donations First

If your refrigerator still works, Salvation Army, Goodwill, and local charities will often pick it up for free. We can coordinate that pickup if you'd like. You get a tax deduction, and the fridge gets a second life. Win-win.

Bundle It With Other Junk

If you're removing multiple items—a fridge, an old couch, some appliances—the per-item cost goes down. Call us with everything you need gone, and we'll give you a volume discount.

Clear the Path

The easier the access to your fridge, the faster we work and the cheaper it is for you. If it's in a basement with a narrow stairwell, that adds labor. Moving boxes out of the way or clearing a garage beforehand helps keep costs down.

Schedule During Off-Peak Hours

Weekday mornings are usually our slowest time. If you're flexible on timing, booking a Tuesday or Wednesday morning instead of a Saturday might get you a better rate.

DIY vs. Professional Removal: The Real Math

Some people consider renting a truck and doing it themselves. On the surface, it looks cheaper. Until you realize:

  • A one-way truck rental is $30-50—only saves you $20-50 vs. our cost
  • The recycling facility won't take a fridge without proper documentation of refrigerant recovery, which you can't do on your own
  • You could hurt yourself moving a 250-pound box for no savings
  • Landfills won't accept refrigerators at all—there's no cheap dump option

The real cost of DIY is liability, waste of time, and risk of injury. Our removal cost is actually the better deal.

FAQ

How much does refrigerator removal cost in Peoria IL?

Standard refrigerator removal costs $50-100 for typical upright models. Larger units like side-by-side or French door fridges run $100-150. If access is difficult (basement location, narrow stairs), add $30-75. The price includes pickup, haul-away, proper R-134a refrigerant recovery, and recycling.

Do you charge extra for R-134a refrigerant removal?

No. Refrigerant removal is included in our standard price. Illinois law requires it for environmental protection, and we factor that cost into what we quote you. No surprise fees.

Will you remove a refrigerator from my basement?

Yes, we remove fridges from basements, garages, and any location. If the space is tight or stairs are narrow, there may be an additional labor charge. Call with details about your location, and we'll give you an exact price.

Can I donate my working refrigerator instead?

Absolutely. If your fridge still works, we can coordinate a pickup with Salvation Army or Goodwill. You get a tax deduction, and the fridge gets reused. We handle the whole thing—just call us.
