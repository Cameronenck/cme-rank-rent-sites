Basement Junk Removal Cost in Peoria IL — What It Actually Costs

Published 2026-06-04

Basements are junk magnets. Over the years, they become catch-all storage for things we don't know what to do with — broken appliances, old furniture, boxes of stuff we'll "maybe use someday," seasonal decorations gone bad.

When you finally decide to clean it out, the first question is usually: How much is this going to cost?

That's a fair question. And the honest answer is: it depends on how full your basement is.

The Real Cost Range for Basement Junk Removal

Here's what we typically see in Peoria:

  • Small cleanout (quarter truck): $300–$500. Light clutter, manageable volume, quick access.
  • Medium cleanout (half truck): $600–$1,000. More significant accumulation, may need multiple loads of smaller items.
  • Large cleanout (full truck): $1,200–$1,800. Basement is heavily loaded — furniture, appliances, boxes, construction debris.
  • Full-basement excavation: $2,000+. You're basically clearing everything; basement is packed wall-to-wall, or there are access challenges.

The number one cost driver is volume. The more stuff you have, the larger the truck we need, and the higher your price. It's that simple.

What Affects Basement Junk Removal Pricing

Volume is the main factor, but a few other things influence the final number:

1. How Accessible Is Everything?

A basement with a wide exterior bulkhead door is a gift. Items roll straight out, job takes 45 minutes. A basement where everything has to come up a narrow indoor staircase? That's slower, more labor-intensive, and sometimes costs more. Tight hallways, low ceilings, cramped spaces — they all add time.

If your basement is a maze, mention it when you call. We'll factor that into the estimate.

2. Is There Anything Hazardous?

Most basement items are fine: old furniture, broken appliances, water-damaged boxes, tools, sports equipment. We take all of it.

But if you have:

  • Paint cans (with liquid still in them)
  • Motor oil or transmission fluid
  • Propane tanks
  • Pesticides or chemical containers
  • Asbestos pipe insulation

...we can't take those. You'll need a hazmat disposal service. It's not common, but it happens — and it's important to know upfront.

3. Are Items Pre-Sorted or Salvageable?

If you've pulled out furniture that's actually in decent shape, or you have metal items, old electronics, or books you want to donate — let us know. We can drop those at the right place for free or a small fee, which means less volume going to the dump, which means a lower bill for you.

4. Do You Need Multiple Trips?

Occasionally, a basement is so packed that one truck load isn't enough. If that's the case, we'll be upfront about it, and you pay for the additional trip. Most of the time, one trip covers it.

Real Example Breakdowns

Scenario 1: The Finished Basement Clean-Out

A finished basement with a finished room that was used as a guest room and storage. Over 10 years, it collected old furniture, a futon frame, boxes of books, some water-damaged items from a minor leak. Access is good — interior stairs, but wide and clear.

Cost: $650

Why? Half truck, straightforward access, nothing hazardous. One crew, 90 minutes on-site.

Scenario 2: The Unfinished Basement Excavation

An unfinished basement that's been a catchall for 20 years. Broken washer and dryer, old shop equipment, stacks of lumber, decades of boxes, broken shelving units, paint cans (empty), an old water heater. Exterior bulkhead, but a little tight. Basement floor has some standing water in one corner.

Cost: $1,400

Why? Full truck needed. Access is slightly tight (costs more time). Larger items like the washer and dryer don't add cost — they're just volume. We work carefully around the water. One crew, about 3 hours on-site.

Scenario 3: The Hoarder Situation

A basement that's been used as storage for items from two households. Furniture piled high, boxes stacked to the ceiling, narrow walkways, items blocking stairs. Access is difficult. Needs two trips.

Cost: $2,100 (two full loads)

Why? Volume requires two truck trips. Labor is higher due to access challenges. Takes one crew about 4–5 hours total, with a second trip.

How to Lower Your Basement Junk Removal Cost

You have more control over price than you think:

Cost-Saving Tips

  • Pre-sort items into "trash" and "donate/sell" piles — reduces volume
  • Pull out anything you want to keep BEFORE we arrive — saves us from loading/unloading mistakes
  • Clear a walking path to items — speeds up the job
  • Move lighter items closer to the exit if possible — less travel distance per load
  • Schedule on a weekday if you can — we often have more availability and can move faster
  • Be honest about what's in there — no surprises means no delays

Why Basement Junk Removal Is Worth the Investment

Yes, a full basement clean-out can cost $1,000 or more. But consider what you're getting:

  • Time: A full basement would take YOU weeks or months of trips to the dump. We do it in a day.

  • Disposal: We handle sorting, recycling, and dumping. You just watch.
  • Reclaimed space: A clean basement is usable space — storage, workshop, recreation room. That's real value.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the average cost to remove junk from a basement in Peoria?
There's no one-size-fits-all number because basements vary wildly. A small finished basement with light clutter might run $300-$600. A fully loaded unfinished basement filled to the rafters could easily hit $1,200-$2,500 or more. What matters is volume — if it fills the truck, you pay for the truck. We'll give you an accurate estimate once we see what you're dealing with.
Do I pay more for basement removal because it's harder to access?
Yes, sometimes. A basement with an exterior bulkhead door and a wide staircase moves faster than one where we're hauling items up a narrow inside stairwell. Concrete floors that allow truck access from a storm door are a time-saver too. The labor factor is real — cramped access can add 20-40% to the job time, and labor is a cost. But it doesn't mean we won't do it. Just be upfront about access when you call for an estimate.
Are there items in basements that cost extra to remove?
A few things. Hazmat like paint cans, oil tanks, or chemical containers we can't take at all — you'll need a hazmat disposal service for that. Heavy items like treadmills, safes, or old furnaces aren't a problem cost-wise; they're usually included in the volume estimate. But if you have asbestos pipe insulation or suspect mold, we'll need to know upfront. Those situations may require specialists instead of us.
Can I save money on basement junk removal?
Yes. Pre-sort anything salvageable — old furniture in decent condition, metal items, electronics — and let us know. We can often donate or recycle those for free, which lowers your volume and your bill. Also, clearing a walking path to the items helps us move faster. The more accessible your stuff is, the faster we work, and faster work = lower cost.
Do you remove junk from other basements in Peoria?
Yes — residential basements are one of our most common jobs. We handle single-family basements, apartment basements, rental properties, you name it. Whether you're cleaning out after a move, downsizing, dealing with a flooded basement, or just finally tackling that storage nightmare, we've done it before and we'll get it done fast.
