Basement Cleanout Cost in Peoria IL: 2026 Pricing Guide

A basement cleanout in Peoria can be a one-afternoon job or a multi-day project depending on how much has accumulated over the years. The pricing follows volume — what we're hauling, how much of it there is, and whether anything is particularly heavy or awkward to move. Here's what to expect and what drives the cost.

Basement Cleanout Cost in Peoria IL

Most residential basement cleanouts in the Peoria area run:

  • Light cleanout (small amount, easy access): $150–$300
  • Medium cleanout (partial basement, mixed items): $300–$550
  • Full basement cleanout (completely cleared): $450–$850
  • Hoarder-level or compacted storage: $700–$1,400+

These prices include labor, hauling, and disposal. We quote based on estimated truck space after seeing the basement — no per-item billing, no surprise fees after the fact.

What Goes Into the Price

Volume is the biggest factor. Junk removal is priced on truck capacity — a quarter load, half load, three-quarter load, or full truckload. A few boxes and an old couch is different from a basement packed floor-to-ceiling with furniture, boxes, equipment, and decades of storage.

Heavy items cost more to move. Appliances, exercise equipment, safes, concrete debris, and water heaters require more labor per cubic foot than furniture or boxes. We factor that in upfront.

Staircase access affects the job. Basements in Peoria range from wide walk-out access to narrow steep staircases with a 90-degree turn at the bottom. Tight access takes longer per item and affects the price. We walk the stairs before quoting.

Moisture and mold issues. Basements with water damage or active mold require more protective handling and may require hazmat disposal for some materials. We flag this during the walkthrough.

What We Take From Basements

We haul almost everything from basements:

  • Old furniture — couches, dressers, bed frames, shelving
  • Appliances — washers, dryers, water heaters, dehumidifiers
  • Exercise equipment — treadmills, ellipticals, weight sets
  • Boxes of clothes, books, household goods
  • Construction debris and leftover materials
  • Old electronics, TVs, computers
  • Holiday decorations, seasonal storage
  • Water-damaged items (with appropriate handling)

We cannot haul hazardous materials — paint, chemicals, propane, asbestos, or medical waste. If your basement has any of these, let us know on the call so we can plan around them. The rest goes.

Estate Cleanouts vs. General Basement Cleanouts

Estate cleanouts in Peoria often start in the basement — that's where people store things they meant to deal with later. If you're handling a loved one's estate and the basement is full, we work alongside family members to keep items you want to save and haul the rest. We move methodically, not in a rush. If you need us to stage items for donation pick-up separately from the general load, we can do that too.

General basement cleanouts for active homeowners tend to be more straightforward — move out, clear out, done. For a house going on the market or a renovation project, timing matters, and we accommodate tight schedules.

Saving Money on Your Basement Cleanout

If you want to reduce the cost, sort before we arrive. Separate items you want to keep from items that go. Having the path to the basement clear and the items ready to move reduces our labor time and your cost. We'll tell you exactly how the volume translates to pricing once we see it.
