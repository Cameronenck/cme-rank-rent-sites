Basement Cleanout Cost in Peoria IL — 2026 Pricing Guide

Updated May 31, 2026 • 6 min read • By Peoria Junk Removal Pros

I have been doing basement cleanouts across Peoria for years, and the same thing comes up on almost every job: people have no idea how much stuff has piled up down there until they are standing at the bottom of the stairs looking at it. Holiday decorations from 2007. A treadmill that became a clothes rack. Three broken dehumidifiers. A full set of encyclopedias. A chest freezer that stopped working during the Obama administration.

Basement cleanouts are the most physically demanding jobs we do, and they take longer than people expect. Tight stairways, low ceilings, heavy appliances, and years of accumulated stuff all add to the work. A typical Peoria basement cleanout runs $200 to $900 depending on how full it is, what is in it, and how complicated the stairway situation is. Here is a full breakdown of what drives that number.

What Affects Basement Cleanout Cost in Peoria

We price basement cleanouts by the volume of junk we haul away. More precisely: how much of our truck your basement fills. But a few factors push the price up or down beyond raw volume:

  • Volume of material: The biggest driver. A quarter-truck load costs less than a full truck. Most residential basements fall in the half to full truck range.
  • Appliances and heavy items: Old washers, dryers, chest freezers, water heaters, and cast iron tubs require extra labor to move safely. We factor that into the quote.
  • Stairway access: Narrow stairs, low ceilings, or 90-degree turns all slow the job down. Basement-level bilco door access speeds it up significantly.
  • Number of loads: Most basements fit in one truck. Extremely heavy or packed basements may need a second run, which adds to the total.
  • Item type: General junk hauls faster than a basement full of concrete blocks, cast iron pipes, or stacked lumber.

What We Haul Out of Peoria Basements

Basements hold a little bit of everything. Here is what we deal with on most jobs:

  • Old furniture — sofas, recliners, dressers, box springs, futons
  • Appliances — washers, dryers, chest freezers, old refrigerators, dehumidifiers, water heaters
  • Exercise equipment — treadmills, ellipticals, weight benches, stationary bikes
  • Boxes and bins — old paperwork, holiday decorations, kids toys, clothes
  • Electronics — old TVs, printers, stereo systems, computers
  • Tools and hardware — broken power tools, shelving units, scrap metal, old paint cans (dried)
  • Construction material — leftover drywall, lumber scraps, tile, carpet remnants
  • Miscellaneous — sports gear, holiday decorations, old books, luggage, lamps

What we cannot take: liquid paint, propane tanks, chemicals, asbestos materials, or any recognized hazardous waste. If you have those items, the City of Peoria runs periodic hazardous waste collection events. Everything else in a typical basement is fair game.

Basement Cleanout vs. Dumpster Rental in Peoria

Renting a dumpster and clearing the basement yourself saves money on the service side but costs you the labor. A 10-yard dumpster in the Peoria area runs roughly $350–$450 for a week. You still have to get every item up the stairs, outside, and into the dumpster yourself — including that cast iron tub or the chest freezer.

For most homeowners tackling a basement full of heavy, bulky items, our full-service removal is cheaper than the hours it takes to do it alone, and easier on your back. If you are dealing with a light cleanout and have help, a dumpster might pencil out. For anything involving appliances or furniture, hiring us almost always wins.
