What's Included in Professional Tree Planting

A properly installed tree isn't just dropped in a hole. Professional planting in Lancaster County includes site evaluation, proper hole sizing, soil amendment where needed, and post-installation care instructions that give the tree the best chance of thriving in Lancaster's specific soil and climate conditions.

Site and Soil Evaluation

Lancaster County soils vary considerably — from well-drained limestone-based soils in some areas to heavier clay in others. Clay soil drains slowly, which can be fatal for young trees if the root ball sits in standing water. We evaluate drainage conditions at the planting site and recommend soil amendments or drainage improvements before anything goes in the ground.

Proper Hole Sizing

One of the most common planting mistakes is digging a hole that's too deep and too narrow. The correct approach is to dig a hole two to three times the width of the root ball but no deeper than the root ball height. Planting too deep kills more trees than almost any other single factor. We measure every time.

Staking and Mulching

Newly planted trees in Lancaster need staking for 6 to 12 months in most cases — long enough to establish roots but not so long that the stake creates a dependency. Mulching the base (3 to 4 inches, pulled back from the trunk) retains moisture and moderates soil temperature. These steps are standard practice on every installation we do.

Best Trees for Lancaster, PA Properties

Lancaster sits in USDA Hardiness Zone 6b — winters are cold but not extreme, and the growing season is long enough for a wide variety of species. Some of the best-performing trees for Lancaster County residential properties include:

  • Red Maple (Acer rubrum) — Fast-growing, excellent fall color, tolerates wet and dry soils
  • Tulip Poplar (Liriodendron tulipifera) — Pennsylvania's state tree, very fast growth, striking flowers
  • Eastern Redbud (Cercis canadensis) — Small ornamental with stunning spring bloom
  • Serviceberry (Amelanchier) — Multi-season interest, berries attract birds
  • Emerald Green Arborvitae — Privacy screening along property lines, low maintenance
  • White Oak (Quercus alba) — Long-lived shade tree, exceptional wildlife value

Spring vs. Fall Planting in Lancaster, PA

Both spring and fall are good planting seasons in Lancaster County. Fall planting (September through November) gives roots time to establish before summer heat stress — trees planted in fall often outperform spring plantings by their second summer. Spring planting works well too, especially for bare-root and container stock. Summer planting is possible but requires more diligent watering through the establishment period.

Frequently Asked Questions

Professional tree planting in Lancaster typically costs $200 to $2,000 per tree depending on species, size, and site conditions. Most residential jobs run $350 to $900 per tree including the tree, planting, staking, and mulching.

What's the best time to plant trees in Lancaster, PA?

Both spring (March–May) and fall (September–November) are excellent planting seasons in Lancaster County. Fall planting is often preferred because roots establish during the cooler months before summer heat arrives. Summer planting is possible but requires more consistent watering.

Do you supply the trees or does the homeowner?

We can source trees through our nursery contacts, which ensures you get healthy, appropriately-sized stock suited to Lancaster County conditions. Homeowners can also supply their own trees. If you supply the tree, we just charge for labor and materials.

How long does a newly planted tree take to establish in Lancaster, PA?

Most trees planted professionally in Lancaster will be well-established within 2 to 3 years. The first year is the most critical — consistent watering through dry spells and keeping mulch in place makes a significant difference in long-term success.

Can you plant trees along a property line for privacy in Lancaster?

Absolutely — privacy screening is one of our most common planting requests in Lancaster County. Emerald Green Arborvitae, Leyland Cypress, and Green Giant Arborvitae are all popular choices for fast-growing year-round screens.
