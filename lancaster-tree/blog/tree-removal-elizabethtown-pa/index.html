What We Typically See in Elizabethtown

Elizabethtown has a lot going on when it comes to tree canopy. The older blocks near the borough center have some large, long-established trees — silver maples, Norway maples, and a good number of oaks — that are now well past their prime growth years. That means more structural issues: co-dominant stems that have grown apart at bad angles, hollowing in the lower trunk, root systems that have lifted sidewalk sections or crept under foundations.

The neighborhoods around Elizabethtown College see a particular mix of ornamental plantings from the past few decades alongside some serious-sized native trees. We've taken down some impressively large specimens near the college corridor — jobs that required careful rigging because of adjacent buildings, parking areas, and foot traffic patterns.

We also see a lot of emerald ash borer damage throughout this part of Lancaster County. If you have ash trees on your property — they were popular plantings through the 80s and 90s — and they haven't been assessed recently, it's worth a look. A declining ash goes from looking rough to being genuinely dangerous faster than most tree species. The wood dries out, the bark peels, and the structural integrity drops fast. By the time an untreated ash looks obviously dead, it's already brittle enough to be a real hazard.

What Tree Removal Costs in Elizabethtown PA

People ask me this all the time and the honest answer is: it depends on the specific tree. But here's a real range to work from. Tree removal in Elizabethtown typically runs $350 to $1,900 for residential work. Here's what moves that number:

  • Size: This is the biggest factor. A 25-foot ornamental or small street tree is a few hours of work. A 60-foot silver maple or ash with a 28-inch base at the trunk is a full day with a full crew.
  • Access and surroundings: Open lawn and equipment access keeps costs down. Tight fence lines, proximity to structures, overhead utility lines, or neighbor property concerns all add time — and time is what drives the price.
  • Dead vs. live wood: Dead trees can actually cost more to remove than live ones. Dead wood is brittle and unpredictable. You lose some of the directional control you'd have with a live limb. That means more careful work, which means more time.
  • Stump: We price stump grinding separately, but bundling it with the removal is always the smarter move financially. We're already there with the right equipment. A return trip costs more.

We give written estimates before anything starts. You'll know the number before any chainsaw touches your property.
