Best Tree Removal Services Near Me in Lancaster PA

Published June 22, 2026 | Local Lancaster Tree Removal

When you need tree removal near you in Lancaster PA, you want a crew you can trust. Not all tree services are created equal. Some are uninsured. Some cut corners. Some disappear when the stump's left behind.

I've been removing trees in Lancaster County for over a decade. In this guide, I'll walk you through how to find the best local service, what separates pros from amateurs, and how to get a fair price without getting ripped off.

What Makes a Tree Removal Service "Local"?

Local matters. A lot. It means they know your soil—clay-heavy Lancaster County dirt is different from sandy soil in other parts of Pennsylvania. They know the weather patterns, the tree diseases common to our region, and the local codes and permit requirements.

More importantly, local means they're invested in their reputation. They live here. Their families live here. A local crew won't disappear after the job is done if something goes wrong. They're not chasing the next job three hours away—they're building a business in your community.

When you call a local service, you're not talking to a call center 300 miles away. You're talking to someone who knows the area and can give you a real estimate over the phone before an arborist even shows up at your property.

How to Find Reliable Tree Removal Near You

Start with Google. Search "tree removal near me Lancaster PA" and look at the top results. Check their Google Business Profile reviews—that's the most honest feedback you'll find. Real customers, real work, real photos.

Then check Facebook reviews. People are often more willing to leave detailed feedback on Facebook. Read the negative reviews too. How did the company respond? Did they fix the problem or make excuses?

Ask neighbors. This is underrated. One conversation with someone who's had their trees removed will tell you more than 50 online reviews. If a crew did good work in your neighborhood, you'll hear about it.

Call at least three companies. Get free estimates from each. A good local service will come out and give you a price with no obligation. They'll explain why the price is what it is—the size of the tree, how close it is to your house, whether they need to remove the stump, what they'll do with the debris.

What Separates Pros From Fly-By-Night Operations

Ask for proof of insurance. Not just that they have it—ask to see the certificate. It should show both liability insurance and workers' compensation. If they balk at showing you proof, walk away. A certified arborist crew should have this ready to hand over.

Check if they're licensed. In Pennsylvania, any company doing tree work on a property that affects neighbors—or anywhere near a power line—should be licensed and bonded. Call the Pennsylvania Department of Agriculture to verify.

Ask if they're ISA certified. ISA stands for International Society of Arboriculture. An ISA-certified arborist has studied tree biology, safety standards, and best practices. It's not required by law, but it's the gold standard. If a company is ISA-certified, they're serious about the work.

What about the equipment? Professional crews use climbing equipment, hazard ropes, bucket trucks, and chippers. If someone shows up with a chainsaw and a pickup truck, you've got a problem. Tree removal near a house requires skill and equipment. Don't cheap out on this.

Red Flags to Watch For

Avoid companies that can't explain their pricing. If you ask "Why is this $1,200?" and they just say "That's what it costs," that's a red flag. Good companies break it down: removal $800, stump grinding $300, cleanup $100.

Avoid quotes that are way lower than the others. I see it all the time. One crew quotes $1,500 and another quotes $600 for the same job. The cheap quote almost never includes debris removal, stump grinding, or site cleanup. By the time they're done, you're paying the full price anyway—and the work is rushed.

Avoid companies that want full payment upfront. Reputable services ask for partial payment before work starts and the balance when the job is complete. If someone demands all the money on day one, that's a problem.

Watch out for door-to-door sales pitches. "I was in the neighborhood and noticed your tree..." They're looking for quick cash jobs, not quality work. Use services you've researched, not ones that found you.

What to Expect When You Call for Tree Removal

When you call us or another local service, here's what should happen:

First call (5–10 minutes): A real person answers. You describe the tree and the situation. They ask clarifying questions: How tall? How close to the house? Do you want the stump removed? Based on your answers, they'll give you a ballpark estimate and schedule a free on-site evaluation.

Site visit: An arborist visits your property, measures the tree, assesses the difficulty, talks through options, and gives you a written estimate. This usually takes 15–30 minutes.

Work day: Your crew arrives with equipment, covers the driveway to prevent damage, sets up safety zones, and removes the tree methodically. For most residential trees, this takes 2–6 hours. They clean up everything before they leave.

After the job: You call to confirm everything looks good. A professional crew is happy to make minor adjustments or do follow-up work if needed.

Lancaster County Tree Removal Pricing (Real Numbers)

You want to know what you'll actually pay. Here's what I see in Lancaster County right now:

Small trees (25 feet or less, clear area): $150–$400. These are the easiest. No climbing required, no risk of hitting the house. A crew can knock these out quickly.

Medium trees (25–50 feet, moderate difficulty): $400–$900. Most residential trees fall here. Climbing is required, risk assessment is needed, cleanup takes time.

Large or hazard trees (50+ feet, near structures, heavy risk): $900–$2,000+. These need careful planning, professional equipment, and experienced climbers. Stump grinding can add $200–$500.

Stump removal (grinding only, no tree removal): $150–$400 depending on size.

Emergency removal (24/7, storm damage): Usually 20–50% higher than standard pricing due to immediate availability and coordination needs.

These prices assume standard Lancaster County soil and access. Rock, wet ground, or restricted access can increase costs.

How to Get the Best Price

If you have multiple trees, bundle them. A crew charging $500 per tree might do your second tree for $350 because they're already there with their equipment set up. Same day, multiple jobs = discount.

Schedule in the off-season. Winter and early spring are cheaper than summer and fall. The crew's less booked, and you save money. If your removal isn't urgent, wait for November through February.

Be flexible on cleanup options. Full debris removal (everything hauled away) is the premium option. Ask if they can chip some and leave mulch in a pile instead. That saves them labor and saves you money.

Use a local company. You avoid call center markups and regional overhead. A local Lancaster company doesn't have to build in big profit margins to cover corporate headquarters staff.
