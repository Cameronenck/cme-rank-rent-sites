How Much Does Tree Removal Cost in Lancaster PA? 2026 Pricing Guide

By Lancaster Tree Removal Pros  ·  June 19, 2026  ·  8 min read

If you've got a tree that needs to come down, you probably want to know two things: Is it really necessary? and How much is this going to cost? This guide answers the second question with real pricing based on what we see in Lancaster County. No estimates padded with extra fees, no hidden charges — just what you'll actually pay.

What Affects Your Final Cost

Tree Height and Diameter

This is the biggest driver. A tree that's 40 feet tall and 18 inches in diameter takes roughly twice as long as a 30-foot tree with a 12-inch trunk. We measure height and diameter on every estimate, and both directly affect labor time and equipment needs.

Tree Condition

A healthy, living tree is straightforward. A dead tree or one with major rot changes the picture. Dead wood is brittle and unpredictable — sections can snap unexpectedly during rigging. A diseased or rotting trunk might not hold a line the way we need it to. That complexity adds cost because the crew has to move slower and be more careful. We're not padding the estimate; we're accounting for actual risk.

Location Relative to Structures and Power Lines

A tree 50 feet from the house is easier than one hanging over the roof. A tree nowhere near power lines is easier than one within 50 feet of a line. The closer a tree is to something we need to protect, the more precisely we need to rig each piece down, which takes more time and sometimes requires a crane. If power lines are involved, we often need to notify the utility company first. That time adds to the cost.

Ground Access and Space

A tree in an open yard with level ground and clear access is efficient. A tree in a tight side yard with fencing, underground utilities, or no room for a chipper truck to pull in adds labor and complexity. We might have to hand-carry debris, chip on a neighbor's property, or make multiple passes. Limited access can add 20–40% to the cost depending on how tight the space is.

Stump Removal

If you want just the tree gone and the stump left, that's the base price. If you want the stump ground, add $75–$200 depending on stump diameter and how deep you want us to grind it. A 12-inch stump is around $100; a 24-inch stump is closer to $200. We can usually do it the same day, which saves a second trip.

Debris Removal and Chipping

Hauling away logs, branches, and chips is included in our price — you're not paying extra to have the debris removed. We either chip it on site (if there's room) or haul it away (if there isn't). If you want to keep some logs for firewood, we'll work around that on site at no extra charge.

Why Prices Vary Between Companies

If you get quotes ranging from $300 to $1,200 for the same tree, the difference usually comes down to:

  • Insurance and licensing. A fully licensed and insured operation costs more than a crew working cash jobs with no insurance. We're licensed and insured — if something goes wrong, you're protected.
  • Experience. An experienced crew reads a tree quickly and knows how to rig it efficiently. A less experienced crew might take twice as long or misjudge the risk.
  • Equipment. Rigging a difficult tree safely requires proper equipment and knowledge. Lowering it without that adds risk.
  • Crew quality and safety practices. A professional operation follows ANSI safety standards. Day-labor operations often cut corners on safety, which is a liability for you as much as for them.
  • Cleanup standards. Some crews pull the stump and leave a mess. We clean as we go and chip or haul everything. That takes more labor and costs more, but your yard doesn't look like a disaster when we leave.
  • Overhead. A company with an office, insured trucks, and steady employees costs more to run than a solo operator. That cost gets passed to you.

The cheapest estimate isn't always the best deal. A low estimate sometimes means the crew underestimated the complexity, or they plan to cut corners on safety or cleanup. We price fairly based on the actual work required and the expertise we bring.

Ways to Reduce Costs

  • Skip the stump grind. If you leave the stump, removal is cheaper. You can always grind it later. This saves $75–$200.
  • Handle your own smaller debris. If you have a chipper or don't mind stacking branches, let us know and we can adjust the scope and price.
  • Clear ground space beforehand. If you can remove fencing, move vehicles, or clear obstacles before we arrive, we work faster. That saves time and money.
  • Get multiple trees done at once. If you have several trees that need removing, one crew visit is more efficient than multiple trips. The per-tree cost often drops when we're doing several.
  • Book during slower season. Winter and early spring are busier for emergency storm work, but mid-summer is usually calmer. Flexible scheduling can sometimes help.

Is It Worth the Cost?

If the tree is dead, diseased, hazardous, or blocking views and sunlight, yes — the cost is worth it. Removing a tree that's about to fail on its own is far cheaper than paying to fix a roof or replace a vehicle after it comes down in a storm. Prevention is cheaper than disaster recovery.

If you're on the fence about whether the tree needs to come out or just needs trimming, get a free estimate and we'll give you our honest take. Sometimes trimming is the right call and saves money. Sometimes removal is the real solution.
