Emergency Tree Removal vs Standard Removal in Lancaster PA — What's the Real Difference?

By Lancaster Tree Removal Pros  ·  May 12, 2026  ·  8 min read

I've been doing tree work in Lancaster County for going on 16 years now. In that time, I've taken calls at 2 AM after nor'easters, responded to downed oaks across driveways during July thunderstorms, and talked dozens of homeowners down from legitimate panic when a half-ton limb came through their roof. I've also talked just as many people out of calling it an emergency when it wasn't one.

The distinction between emergency tree removal and standard removal matters — and not just because of price. It's about response time, crew mobilization, safety risk, and yes, what your insurance company will cover. Here's how I break it down for folks calling us from Manheim, Elizabethtown, or right here in Lancaster city.

What Makes a Tree Removal an Emergency?

A true tree emergency is simple: there's an active, immediate threat to people or property. That's it. If you're looking at a tree and asking "is this an emergency?"— run through this list.

You've got an emergency if:

  • A tree or large limb has already fallen on your house, garage, fence, or car
  • A tree has cracked and is leaning hard toward a structure, partially hanging
  • Root plate lift is visible — meaning the root ball is pulling out of the ground after a storm
  • A limb is blocking a road, driveway, or power line
  • There's an active safety hazard that can't wait 24–48 hours

You probably don't have an emergency if:

  • A tree looks sick or dead but is still standing normally
  • A dead tree is leaning but not over any structures
  • You lost a limb in a storm but the tree itself is still intact and standing clear
  • You want a tree down before you list your house next month

That second category — the "it can wait" category — is standard removal territory. We schedule it, we plan it, and we get it done right without the overtime clock running.

The Real Cost Difference

I'll be straight with you: emergency removal costs more. Usually 25–50% more than the same job done as a standard scheduled removal. The reasons are legitimate — we're pulling a crew off another job, mobilizing equipment in the dark or rain, and often dealing with a tree that's already partially failed and in a dangerous position. That adds time, skill, and risk.

What About Insurance?

This is the question I hear on almost every emergency call. The short answer: it depends on your policy and what the tree actually hit.

Standard homeowner's policies in Pennsylvania will generally cover tree removal if a fallen tree damaged a covered structure — your house, attached garage, fence, or sometimes a detached structure. If a tree falls in your backyard and doesn't hit anything, most policies consider that your cleanup bill, not theirs.

My advice: before you authorize any work on a potential insurance claim, photograph everything. Every angle. The fallen tree, the damage, the position before anyone touches it. Then call your insurance company. Some folks skip that step in the chaos of a storm and regret it when the adjuster shows up two days later and can't document the cause of loss.

We work with homeowners navigating claims all the time and will give you clear documentation of what we removed and why. But we don't file your claim — that's your call to make.

Response Time: What to Actually Expect

During normal conditions, we can typically get someone on-site within 2–4 hours for active emergencies anywhere in Lancaster County — Lititz, Ephrata, Manheim, Mount Joy, Quarryville, you name it. We run 24/7 during storm season precisely because that's when people need us.

After a major storm event — and we get some bad ones in June and July out here — response times stretch. When we've got 40 calls queued up and crews working around the clock, active threats to occupied structures jump to the front of the line. A tree leaning against a vacant garage may wait until morning. I'd rather be honest with you upfront than promise a time I can't keep.

Standard removal is different. Most jobs in Lancaster get scheduled within 5–10 business days. Spring and early fall are our busiest windows, so if you want flexibility on timing, calling before you absolutely need it is always the right move. It's also the way to get the best price.

How Standard Removal Is Done (and Why It's Better When You Have Time)

When a job isn't an emergency, we can do it better. That's just the reality. A standard scheduled removal means I can send my best crew for the specific job type, bring the right equipment, and take the time to protect landscaping, siding, and anything else near the tree. We can plan drop zones, set up rigging on taller trees, and do clean, controlled sectional work rather than reactive response.

Standard jobs also give you time to make decisions — stump grinding, firewood splitting, chip drop, haul-away. With emergency work, those decisions get made fast under pressure. With scheduled work, you can think it through.

If your tree is a hazard but hasn't failed yet — dead wood up top, a heavy lean toward the fence line, visible decay at the trunk base — the smart move is to call us now and schedule removal before it becomes a 2 AM emergency. You'll pay less, get a cleaner job, and avoid the insurance headache.
