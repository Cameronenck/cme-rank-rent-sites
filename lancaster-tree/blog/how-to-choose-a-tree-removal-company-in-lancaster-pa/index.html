How to Choose a Tree Removal Company in Lancaster PA

By Lancaster Tree Removal Pros  ·  June 20, 2026  ·  7 min read

Picking the right tree removal company can mean the difference between a smooth, professional job and an expensive mess. I've done hundreds of removals in Lancaster County, and I see the same mistakes over and over — homeowners picking the cheapest quote, skipping insurance checks, or trusting a contractor based solely on a Facebook profile. This guide walks you through what actually matters.

Why It Matters Who You Hire

Tree removal is one of the most dangerous jobs in existence. A 60-foot tree can weigh 20 tons. If something goes wrong — a miscalculation with rigging, an uninsured worker getting hurt, equipment hitting your house — you're not just looking at a bad day, you're looking at five-figure liability. The difference between a bargain crew and a professional company isn't about the stump being ground slightly differently. It's about whether you're covered if things go wrong.

Step 1: Verify Insurance

This is non-negotiable. Before anyone touches your tree, ask for proof of two things:

  • General Liability Insurance (minimum $1 million per occurrence) — covers damage to your property if something goes wrong
  • Workers' Compensation — covers medical and disability costs if a crew member gets hurt on your property

Ask for a certificate of insurance naming you as an additional insured. If they won't provide it, don't hire them. Period. I've seen homeowners sued when uninsured contractors got hurt on their property. It's not worth saving $500.

Step 2: Check Their Credentials and Track Record

Look for these markers of professionalism:

  • ISA Certified Arborist — means formal training and ongoing education. Not required for all jobs, but it's a good sign they know what they're doing
  • TCIA membership (Tree Care Industry Association) — means they follow industry standards
  • Positive reviews with specific details — avoid companies with only generic 5-star ratings. Real reviews mention the crew's cleanup, professionalism, and how issues were handled
  • Verifiable local work — ask for references and drive by past jobs if possible. Legitimate companies are happy to provide this

Check Google, Facebook, and the Better Business Bureau. Recent reviews matter more than old ones. If you see repeated complaints about surprise charges or damage, move on.

Step 3: Get Multiple Quotes (Free, On-Site)

Never hire based on a phone estimate. Get at least three in-person quotes. Here's why: tree removal pricing varies wildly based on what the crew actually sees — angles of approach, root systems, nearby structures, access for equipment. A phone estimate is a guess.

Red flag: Anyone who charges for a quote or gives a price without seeing the tree isn't worth your money.

When comparing quotes, don't just look at the total price. Ask what's included: Does it cover stump grinding? Debris removal? Cleanup? A $600 quote that includes everything is better than a $500 quote that nickel-and-dimes you with add-ons.

Step 4: Watch for These Red Flags

Walk away from any company that:

  • Won't provide proof of insurance without resistance
  • Demands a large upfront payment (we typically ask for half before work, half after)
  • Pressures you to decide immediately
  • Has no online reviews or any way to verify their work
  • Shows up in unmarked trucks or looks disorganized on your property
  • Can't explain the scope of the work in clear terms
  • Won't discuss cleanup or stump grinding separately

Step 5: Ask About Permits

Tree removal permits vary by township in Lancaster County. Some places require them, others don't. A professional company will know your local rules and handle permits if needed — or clearly tell you whether they're required. Some include permit costs in their quote, others charge separately. Get this in writing before work starts.

Step 6: Get Everything in Writing

The quote should be clear and detailed:

  • Exact work to be done (tree size, stump grinding yes/no, debris removal)
  • Total price with no surprises
  • Timeline for the work
  • What happens if bad weather delays the job
  • Payment terms (deposit required, final payment due when)

A handshake agreement is worthless when something goes wrong. Professional companies put it in writing.

The Bottom Line

Hiring a tree removal company isn't about finding the cheapest option. It's about finding someone who knows what they're doing, is properly insured, has done good work in your area, and communicates clearly. The cost difference between a $500 quote and a $700 quote is a reasonable insurance premium for avoiding disaster.
