How to Choose a Tree Removal Company in Lancaster PA

Published May 2026 • 6 min read • By Lancaster Tree Removal Pros

I've been removing trees in Lancaster County for 15 years. After major storms, I watch homeowners make the same expensive mistakes: hiring the wrong company, finding out too late there's no insurance, or getting a quote that doubles before the job is done. Here's how to avoid all of that.

Insurance First — Non-Negotiable

Tree removal is one of the most dangerous jobs in the trades. Falling branches, rope failures, chainsaw accidents, equipment tipping on uneven ground — the risk is real and the consequences can be severe. Before you hire any tree company in Lancaster, ask for a certificate of insurance showing:

  • General liability: $1 million minimum per occurrence. This covers property damage — if a crew drops a limb on your roof or a truck rolls into your fence.
  • Workers' compensation: This covers injured workers. If a climber falls on your property and there's no workers' comp, you may be on the hook for their medical bills. This is not a hypothetical — it happens.

Don't take their word for it. Ask for the certificate and verify it's current. Any legitimate company will provide this without hesitation.

Look for Certified Arborists

The ISA (International Society of Arboriculture) certifies arborists who have demonstrated knowledge of tree biology, proper pruning techniques, risk assessment, and safety practices. Not every tree removal job requires a certified arborist — cutting down a dead tree in an open yard is straightforward. But for trees near structures, trees with disease or unusual root issues, or any situation where you want professional judgment about whether the tree can be saved, ISA certification matters.

Ask if any of their team members hold ISA credentials. Check the ISA's online directory to verify. See our full tree services for how we handle each situation.

Get Written Quotes — Not Just Numbers Over the Phone

A tree removal quote should be in writing and should specify:

  • Exactly which tree(s) are being removed
  • Whether stump grinding is included (to what depth)
  • Who removes the wood, chips, and debris — does it leave with the crew?
  • Whether the quote is fixed or subject to change
  • Payment terms

Get three written quotes from different companies. This is the fastest way to identify outliers — both suspiciously low bids (which will surprise you later) and unjustifiably high ones.

Watch Out for Storm Chasers

After major storms, Lancaster sees an influx of out-of-town crews knocking on doors offering "emergency rates." Some are legitimate. Many are not. Warning signs: no local address, out-of-state plates, asking for full cash payment upfront, no written paperwork. Hire local. A company with a Lancaster County address and verifiable references has a reputation to protect. An out-of-town crew has an easy exit.

Ask Specifically About Cleanup

Tree removal creates a lot of debris — branches, chips, log rounds, leaves. Confirm before work starts: what leaves the property with the crew? Does the price include hauling all debris, or just cutting the tree? Some companies leave the wood rounds for you and only haul the brush — which can mean a significant pile of logs you now need to deal with.

If you want the wood for firewood, that's usually fine — just say so upfront. If you want everything gone, confirm that's included in the quote.

Frequently Asked Questions

What insurance should a tree removal company carry?

At minimum: general liability (at least $1 million per occurrence) and workers' compensation. Tree removal is one of the most dangerous trades — if a crew member is hurt on your property and there's no workers' comp, you could be held liable. Always ask for certificates of insurance before anyone climbs.

What is an ISA Certified Arborist and do I need one?

ISA (International Society of Arboriculture) Certified Arborists have passed a rigorous exam and maintain continuing education. Not every tree removal job requires one, but if the tree is near your house, near power lines, or you need advice on whether it can be saved, having an ISA arborist involved is valuable insurance.

How do I get a fair tree removal quote in Lancaster PA?

Get at least three written quotes. Make sure each quote specifies: whether stump removal is included, who hauls the wood chips and debris, and whether the price is fixed or can change. Quotes that are suspiciously low often exclude stump grinding or debris removal.

What are the red flags when hiring a tree company?

Door-knockers who show up right after a storm (storm chasers). No written quote — only a verbal number. No proof of insurance. Demanding full payment upfront. Not wearing PPE on site. Any of these should send you looking elsewhere.

Is stump grinding included in tree removal quotes?

Not always — and this is one of the most common quote mismatches. Ask specifically whether stump grinding to ground level is included. Some companies quote the tree removal separately from stump work, which can add $150–400 to your final bill.
