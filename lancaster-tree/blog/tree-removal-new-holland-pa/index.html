Tree Removal Pricing in New Holland PA

Tree removal costs vary based on size, location, and difficulty. In New Holland, you're typically looking at these ranges:

  • Small trees (20–30 feet, clear fall zone): $200–$450
  • Medium trees (40–60 feet, typical property): $400–$850
  • Large mature oaks, maples, hickories (60–80 feet, complex removal): $800–$1,700+

New Holland's clay soil and deep root systems can add complexity to removal jobs. Trees growing in clay tend to have deeper, thicker roots, which means removal requires proper rigging and sometimes longer cleanup time. If the tree is near a structure, power line, or in a confined space, expect the higher end of the range. Always get a free on-site estimate before committing.

Why Tree Removal is Urgent in Eastern Lancaster County

Emerald ash borer has swept through the entire ash tree population in Lancaster County, including New Holland and all the surrounding townships. If you have a dead or dying ash tree on your property, don't put it off. Dead ash dries out and becomes unpredictable within one or two seasons — the bark starts to slough off, branches snap without warning even in calm weather, and the removal job gets exponentially harder and more expensive the longer you wait.

The same applies to any dead or declining hardwood — oak, maple, hickory, or walnut. These trees can be 70–90 feet tall with massive crown spreads. Once they're dead, they become hazards that pose real risk to your structure, fences, and neighbors' property. Get them assessed now, not after a storm or a near-miss.

Tree Removal Near Structures and Power Lines

New Holland has plenty of older homes and barns where large trees grow inches from the roof edge or reach toward power lines. We handle that work every week. When the fall zone is tight, we use rigging and sectioning to bring the tree down piece by piece rather than all at once. This requires skill and experience, but it keeps your property and structures safe.

If you have a tree within 20 feet of your house, barn, or power line, don't attempt removal yourself and be cautious of low-cost contractors who guarantee to "drop it straight" without a full walk of the property. Stump Grinding and What Comes Next

After the tree comes down, you have options. You can leave the stump (not recommended), have us grind it out, or we can fell the tree and leave the cleanup for later. Stump grinding typically costs $150–$300 depending on diameter and runs time, and it opens up the space for new landscaping, a replacement tree, or simply a cleaner yard. Most New Holland properties choose grinding; the investment is relatively small and eliminates a hazard (especially if children or pets are around).
