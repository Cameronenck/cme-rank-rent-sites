Tree Removal in Manheim PA — Local Crew, Honest Prices

Published May 2026 • 5 min read • By Lancaster Tree Removal Pros

I've been doing tree work in Lancaster County for over 15 years now, and Manheim is one of those towns that keeps us busy year-round. If you need tree removal in Manheim PA, here's what you should know before you call anyone.

Tree Problems We See Most in Manheim

The borough has a good mix of older residential streets and newer development on the edges of town, and the tree issues tend to reflect that. On the older blocks, we deal with a lot of silver maples and Norway maples that were planted back in the 60s and 70s and are now hitting the end of their lifespan. These trees get big fast, which is why people planted them — but the wood is weak, and by the time they're 50+ years old, you're dealing with hollow trunks, root heave on sidewalks, and branches that come down in every significant storm.

We also see plenty of emerald ash borer damage across Manheim Township and the borough. If you've got ash trees on your property that haven't been treated or assessed, there's a real chance they're already declining. A dead ash doesn't just look bad — it becomes structurally unpredictable. The bark sloughs off, the wood dries out fast, and what looks like a standing tree can come apart in sections without much warning when the wind picks up.

And then there's the storm damage calls. We're out here after every significant weather event — the nor'easters in winter, the pop-up thunderstorms in July and August. Manheim sits in a part of Lancaster County that gets its share of straight-line wind events. When a tree comes down on a structure or blocks a driveway, people need help fast, not in two weeks.

What Tree Removal Costs in Manheim PA

I'll give you real numbers. Tree removal in Manheim generally runs $350 to $1,800 for residential work, and here's what moves that price up or down:

  • Tree size: Height and trunk diameter are the two biggest factors. A 20-foot ornamental pear is a couple hours of work. A 65-foot silver maple with a 30-inch base is a full day, minimum.
  • Access: Can we get equipment into the yard, or are we climbing and hand-rigging the whole job? Open access drops the price. Tight fence lines and overhead utility proximity push it up.
  • Condition of the tree: Surprisingly, dead trees sometimes cost more than live ones. Dead wood is brittle and unpredictable. We can't control how it breaks the way we can direct a live limb. That takes more time and more care.
  • Stump grinding: We quote this separately, but bundling it with the removal is always cheaper than scheduling a second trip. Ask up front.

We provide written estimates before any work starts. No surprises when the invoice lands.

How the Job Actually Goes

When we come out to quote in Manheim, we walk the property with you. We look at the tree from multiple angles — root zone, canopy structure, direction of lean, what's in the fall zone. Sometimes a tree looks worse from the street than it actually is. Sometimes it's the opposite. Either way, we'll tell you what we see and give you an honest recommendation.

On the day of the job, the crew shows up with the right equipment for that specific job. We section the tree down in a controlled way, manage where the material lands, and clean up completely when we're done. Brush and chips go with us. Firewood rounds we'll stack neatly if you want them, or haul them away if you don't.

We're fully insured — general liability and workers' comp — and we'll produce the certificates before anyone sets foot on your property. This isn't optional. If a crew shows up and can't hand you insurance docs when you ask, send them home. An uninsured injury on your property can become your problem legally.
