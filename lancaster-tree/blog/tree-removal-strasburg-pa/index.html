Common Tree Species in Strasburg

Strasburg and the surrounding area has a mix of mature deciduous trees — oaks, maples, elms, and a significant number of ash trees that have been impacted by emerald ash borer. Dead or dying ash trees are a priority removal: they become brittle quickly and unpredictably. If you have ash trees on your property in Strasburg, have them assessed — dead ash can fail without warning and the window to remove them safely before they become more hazardous is shorter than most homeowners realize.

Silver maples are another common issue in the area. They're fast-growing, which made them popular as shade trees, but they're structurally weak and frequently develop split unions that become a significant drop risk. Any silver maple over 40 feet with a V-shaped union should be assessed.

When to Remove vs. Trim in Strasburg

Trimming makes sense when the tree is structurally sound and the issue is clearance or overgrowth. Removal is the right call when:

  • More than 50% of the crown is dead or diseased
  • There's significant decay at the base or in major structural limbs
  • The tree has already started dropping large limbs
  • It's a hazardous location (over a structure or high-use area) with structural compromise
  • It's a dead ash tree anywhere near structures or high-traffic areas

We give you a straight assessment. If a trim will address the issue, we'll say so. If the tree needs to come down, we'll tell you why rather than just pointing at the equipment.
