Tree Trimming Cost in Lancaster PA: 2026 Pricing Guide

Published 2026-05-27 · Lancaster Tree Removal Pros

Home › Blog › Tree Trimming Cost in Lancaster PA: 2026 Pricing Guide

Tree trimming is one of those services where the price range is genuinely wide, and for good reason. A single mature oak in a Lancaster backyard with clearance on all sides is a different job from the same tree overhanging a roof with a deck underneath and a fence on two sides. Here's what's actually driving the cost so you know what to expect when you call for a quote.

Tree Trimming Cost Ranges in Lancaster PA

For most residential tree trimming in Lancaster County, expect to pay:

  • Small trees (under 25 ft): $150–$400 per tree
  • Medium trees (25–50 ft): $300–$700 per tree
  • Large trees (50–80 ft): $600–$1,200 per tree
  • Very large trees (80 ft+): $1,000–$2,000+

These are per-tree numbers for a standard trim. If you have multiple trees, most contractors will quote a job rate rather than per-tree — and you can usually save money by bundling several trees into one visit.

What Affects the Price

Tree size is the primary factor. Larger trees require more time, more crew, and more equipment. A 20-foot ornamental pear is straightforward. An 80-foot white oak over a Lancaster County farmhouse is a full-day job for an experienced crew.

Access and obstacles matter significantly. Trees growing close to structures — houses, garages, fences, power lines — require slower, more precise work and often additional rigging to lower branches safely. Open access with room to drop limbs is faster and cheaper.

Scope of work affects price more than homeowners often realize. A "cleanup trim" to remove dead wood and crossing branches is different from a structural crown reduction, which requires a certified arborist making judgment calls about tree structure and long-term health. Both are called "trimming" — but the complexity and time are different.

Debris removal. Most quotes in Lancaster include cleanup and hauling the debris. But confirm this upfront. Some contractors quote trimming only and charge separately for brush disposal.

When to Trim vs. When to Remove

This is a judgment call that affects cost significantly. Trimming a declining or structurally compromised tree can extend its life or it can be money spent on a tree that's eventually coming down anyway. Signs that a tree might be beyond trimming:

  • More than 50% of the crown is dead or dying
  • Significant trunk damage or decay at the base
  • Repeated heavy limb failure without apparent cause
  • Major structural cracks in the main union of a large tree

A crew that will tell you honestly whether a tree is worth trimming or should come down is more valuable than one that just takes your money to trim a tree that's going to fail in two years anyway.

Trimming for Power Line Clearance in Lancaster

If your trees are growing into PPL or Met-Ed lines, the utility company handles utility-side clearance — that's not your job and not ours. But the property-side of the tree, including branches growing toward the house or garage, is your responsibility.

Lancaster County has a lot of mature tree canopy, especially in older neighborhoods in the city and in rural properties with established woodlots. Regular trimming cycles — every 3–5 years for most species — keep trees healthy, reduce storm damage risk, and prevent the kind of reactive emergency removal that costs significantly more.

Lancaster County Trees: What We See Most

Oak, maple, and ash are the most common large trees in Lancaster County properties. Ash is a specific concern — emerald ash borer has affected ash trees throughout PA, and many Lancaster area ashes are in decline or already dead. If you have an ash tree, have it assessed before investing in trimming work.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to trim a large oak tree in Lancaster PA?
A large oak (50–80 ft) in Lancaster County typically runs $600–$1,200 to trim, depending on access and scope. Trees overhanging structures or with limited access on multiple sides will be toward the higher end. We give accurate quotes after seeing the tree — size estimates from the street aren't reliable for oaks.
Do I need a permit to trim trees in Lancaster PA?
Most residential tree trimming on private property in Lancaster County doesn't require a permit. Some municipalities and Lancaster City have specific ordinances for street trees or larger removal jobs — we'll flag anything that requires attention when we assess your property.
How often should I trim trees on my Lancaster County property?
Most healthy mature trees benefit from trimming every 3–5 years. Young trees may need more frequent formative pruning. Fruit trees, if you have them, typically need annual trimming. Fast-growing species like silver maple or Bradford pear may need attention more often.
What's the difference between tree trimming and tree pruning?
The terms are often used interchangeably, but technically trimming usually refers to removing overgrown or wayward branches for clearance or aesthetics, while pruning involves more deliberate removal for tree health — targeting dead wood, crossing branches, or structural improvements. We do both and will recommend the right approach for your specific trees.
Can you trim trees near power lines in Lancaster?
We trim trees on the property side. Work directly on utility lines is handled by the utility company (PPL or Met-Ed) under their right-of-way programs. If your tree has branches growing toward your home or garage alongside power line proximity, that's what we address.
