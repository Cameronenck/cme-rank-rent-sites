Professional Arborist Services in Lancaster PA

By Lancaster Tree Removal Pros  ·  June 16, 2026  ·  7 min read

Most people think a tree is either fine or it needs to come down. But that's not how trees work. Between healthy and hazardous, there's a whole middle ground — trees that are struggling, diseased, poorly structured, or at risk. That's where a professional arborist comes in. I've been certified through the ISA for 12 years now, and I've seen the difference proper arborist care makes. It saves trees, saves money, and keeps people safe.

What Does a Professional Arborist Actually Do?

An arborist isn't just someone with a chainsaw who calls himself a tree guy. A certified arborist has formal education in tree biology, pruning science, structural assessment, and safety protocols. The ISA (International Society of Arboriculture) certification requires 3 years of experience, documented work hours, and passing a rigorous exam that covers everything from plant biology to hazard assessment to risk management.

In plain English: when you call a certified arborist, you're getting someone who knows the deep science of how trees work and what they need to stay healthy.

Here's what professional arborist services actually cover:

Tree Health Diagnosis & Treatment

A tree that looks sick might have multiple issues — disease, nutrient deficiency, pest damage, root compaction, soil issues. A professional arborist can diagnose the actual problem instead of guessing. If it's treatable, we recommend treatment: targeted pruning, cabling, bracing, soil aeration, pest management. A lot of trees that "need removal" can actually be saved.

Structural Pruning

Bad pruning is worse than no pruning. I've seen plenty of damage from people (or "tree services") who don't understand tree structure. They top trees, leave stubs, prune too much, or remove the wrong branches. Structural pruning isn't about making a tree look symmetrical — it's about improving branch architecture, removing codominant leaders, opening the canopy for health, and preventing structural failure down the line.

Risk Assessment & Hazard Mitigation

A tree doesn't have to be dead to be dangerous. Dead branches over a walkway, a tree with included bark that's splitting apart, a root that's damaging your foundation — these are hazards. An arborist can identify them, assess the actual risk level, and recommend mitigation. Sometimes that's removal. Sometimes it's cable bracing or targeted pruning.

Preservation & Heritage Trees

If you've got an old oak, a historic tree, or something you just don't want to lose, an arborist will fight to keep it. We manage competing roots, work around constraints, and make decisions that preserve the tree long-term instead of just getting through this season.

How to Tell Your Tree Needs Professional Arborist Care

Not every tree issue needs an arborist. But here are the signs that one should at least look at it:

  • Dead branches: Large dead limbs don't just look bad — they're falling hazards.
  • Visible disease: Cankers, oozing sap, fungal growth, unusual leaf discoloration.
  • Structural problems: Multiple trunks rubbing together, severe lean, codominant leaders competing.
  • Poor crown structure: Dense, heavy foliage concentrated on one side; long spindly growth.
  • Large cavities or hollow sections: Tree might still be salvageable, but it needs assessment.
  • Recent storm damage: Torn branches, bark stripping, uprooted roots.
  • Insect or pest damage: Boring insects, scale, defoliation, tent caterpillars.
  • Trees near structures or utilities: Any tree close to your house, power lines, or a neighbor's property benefits from professional management.

Arborist vs. Tree Service: What's the Difference?

A tree service cuts trees down. An arborist manages them. Both matter. Some companies do both. But the mindset is different. A tree service thinks "is this tree removable?" An arborist thinks "should this tree come down, or can we preserve it?"

For routine removals, a general tree service is fine. For diagnosis, preservation, or complex situations, you want someone who specializes in tree health and structure. That's where professional arborist training shows up.

Why Professional Arborist Care Saves Money Long-Term

Hiring an arborist costs more upfront than just removing a tree. But early intervention prevents larger, more expensive problems later. A tree with early-stage disease caught and treated might live another 20 years. Structural work now prevents failure and liability later. Proper pruning prevents hazardous growth patterns.

Think of it like health care. Regular checkups and preventive treatment beat emergency surgery. Same with trees.
