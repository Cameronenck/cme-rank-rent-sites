Dead Ash Trees and Emerald Ash Borer in Denver

Emerald ash borer has been working through Lancaster County for years, and rural properties in Denver and Earl Township have plenty of ash — standing in pastures, lining old fence rows, in woodlots. When ash dies from EAB, it deteriorates fast. A dead ash standing in an open field seems less urgent than one next to a house, but dead wood is unpredictable. Brittle branches drop in wind. The tree can lean as the root system weakens. On a property where you move equipment or livestock, a dead ash becomes a real hazard.

The longer a dead ash stands, the harder it gets to take down safely and the more expensive removal becomes. When the wood starts to crumble and the tree's structural integrity is compromised, it takes longer to section and rig down safely. Remove ash trees sooner rather than later — both for cost and for liability. See our full guide on dead tree removal in Lancaster PA for more on EAB management and removal.

Utility Line Clearances and Overhead Infrastructure

Rural properties in Denver often have PECO power lines running through or near tree stands, service lines to barns or equipment shelters, and telephone infrastructure that needs clearance. I handle tree removal near utility lines regularly — it requires notification to PECO and to local utilities, it requires precision cutting technique to avoid contact, and it requires knowledge of safe working distances.

Never attempt to remove a tree yourself if there are utility lines involved. The risk is serious — electrocution, equipment damage, and significant liability. Call 717-297-1693 and we'll do it right. We work with the utilities, maintain proper clearances, and get the tree down safely.
