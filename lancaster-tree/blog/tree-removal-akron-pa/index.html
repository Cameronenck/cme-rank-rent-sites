Why Akron Homeowners Need Tree Removal

Akron sits in the heart of Lancaster County's mixed forest zone. We've got thick stands of oak, maple, ash, hickory, and pine—beautiful trees, but they don't stay young forever. And they don't always stay where you want them.

The most common reasons I get called to Akron properties:

  • Dead ash trees from emerald ash borer. Once an ash dies, it's dead for good. The wood hardens and becomes brittle within 12-18 months. I've pulled down hundreds of ash trees that were a safety liability.
  • Trees too close to structures. A mature oak or maple with branches over your roof or gutters. Roots under a foundation or septic system. These need professional removal before they cause damage.
  • Hazard trees near power lines. PECO lines run through most Akron properties. A tree leaning toward a line or with heavy branches overhead needs careful removal.
  • Storm damage. A downed tree, split trunk, or dangerous widow-makers (broken branches hanging overhead) after a hard rain or ice storm.
  • Lot clearing for equipment access. Farm equipment needs clear lines on rural property. We clear brush and remove trees to open up pastures or work areas.

Every situation is different, but they all have one thing in common: attempting removal yourself is dangerous and often illegal if power lines are involved. That's where licensed removal comes in.

Dead Ash Trees: The Emerald Ash Borer Crisis in Lancaster County

If you have an ash tree on your Akron property and it's showing no leaves in June, it's almost certainly dead from emerald ash borer. This invasive beetle has wiped out ash trees from coast to coast. In Lancaster County, it started showing up around 2008 and now it's everywhere.

Here's what happens: the beetle lays eggs under the bark. Larvae tunnel through the sapwood, cutting off water and nutrients. Within one to two seasons, the whole tree dies. Once it's dead, you have a problem.

A dead ash standing in your yard:

  • Becomes brittle—branches drop without warning
  • Becomes a liability—if a branch hits your car or a guest, you could be liable
  • Blocks sunlight and drops wood chips into gutters as it decays
  • Becomes harder to remove the longer it stands—the wood deteriorates and becomes unpredictable

I remove dead ash trees of all sizes throughout Akron. Don't wait for one to fail on your house. Call 717-297-1693 and I'll remove it safely while it's still manageable.

How I Remove Trees Safely Near Power Lines

A lot of Akron properties have PECO Energy lines running across them—either to the house, barn, or property line. Removing a tree near these lines takes special care.

Here's my process:

  1. Assess the clearance. I look at the tree height, branch spread, and the exact location of the power line. I determine if the tree can fall safely away from the line or if it's too close.
  2. Call the utility if needed. If the tree is directly under or very close to a live line, I call PECO and ask them to either de-energize or verify that my planned cutting is safe. It's free and it's the law.
  3. Use directional felling. For trees near lines, I use ropes, pulleys, and sometimes a climber to drop the tree at a specific angle—never toward the line. It takes longer, but it's the only safe way.
  4. Never cut into a live power line. If a tree touches an energized line, the line is energized. Touching it means electrocution. I maintain clearance and verify the cut path before I make any cut.

A homeowner who tries this alone is risking their life. Trees near power lines are a job for licensed professionals. That's non-negotiable.

Stump Grinding: Why You Need It

After I remove a tree, you're left with the stump. A lot of people leave it, thinking they'll deal with it later. Big mistake.

A stump left in your yard:

  • Becomes a tripping hazard
  • Resprouts—the root system is still alive, and new shoots grow from the cut surface
  • Becomes an eyesore that doesn't age well
  • Ties up space you might want to replant or use

Stump grinding removes the stump and most of the root system below grade. The area can be replanted, sodded, or left as mulch. It costs $100–$300 per stump on most Akron properties and saves you from dealing with regrowth and tree shoots for years.

I include stump grinding in almost every removal estimate. It's the right way to finish the job.
