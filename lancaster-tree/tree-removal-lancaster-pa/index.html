Professional Tree Removal in Lancaster, PA

When a tree has to come down, the difference between a clean job and a nightmare comes down to equipment, experience, and crew discipline. Lancaster Tree Pros handles tree removal across Lancaster County — from 20-foot ornamental trees in tight backyards to 80-foot oak canopies that have been standing since the Revolutionary War farms this county was built on.

We work in every situation: trees close to structures, trees over utility lines, trees on slopes, post-storm trees that are already half-down. Our crews are insured, the equipment is right-sized for each job, and we clean up everything before we leave.

What Tree Removal Includes

  • Full evaluation of the tree and surrounding hazards before work begins
  • Sectional felling or directional felling depending on space and access
  • Limb-by-limb lowering for trees close to structures, fences, or utility lines
  • Complete debris removal — wood rounds, brush, leaves, and bark
  • Rake and blow of the work area before we leave
  • Stump cut to ground level (stump grinding quoted separately if you want it flush)

The Removal Process: What to Expect

We start with an on-site estimate — no charge, no pressure. The estimator walks the property, looks at the tree's structure, checks for lean, evaluates root condition, and identifies any access constraints. You get a written quote before any work is scheduled.

On job day, the crew arrives with a chipper truck, a bucket truck for tall trees, and hand climbers for tight spots. We protect nearby structures with roping systems when needed. Large trees come down in sections — the top is rigged and lowered, then we work down the trunk. For trees in open areas with enough fall zone, directional felling is faster and less expensive.

Cleanup is always included. Wood can be left in rounds if you want firewood — just let us know upfront. Otherwise it all goes with us.

When You Need Tree Removal vs. Trimming

Not every tree problem requires removal. A tree that's leaning toward a structure, showing significant deadwood in the canopy, has root damage from construction, or has been struck by lightning typically needs to come down. A tree that's healthy but overgrown or touching the house often just needs structural pruning.

We'll tell you honestly which one applies. We do both, so we have no incentive to upsell you on removal when trimming is the right answer.

Common reasons Lancaster homeowners call us for full removal: storm damage that's compromised the root system, trees in decline from emerald ash borer, dead trees creating liability, and trees too close to the house causing foundation or gutter problems over time. Contractors doing additions or pool installations in Lititz, Manheim, and Ephrata also call us regularly for lot clearing ahead of construction.
