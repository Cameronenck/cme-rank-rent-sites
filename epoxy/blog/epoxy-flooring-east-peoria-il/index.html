Basement Floors in East Peoria

A lot of the ranch homes in East Peoria have full unfinished basements that are either bare concrete or painted with that old battleship-gray latex that was popular in the 80s. We coat a lot of these — either as full basement conversions or utility areas and workshops. The main consideration with basements in this area is moisture. East Peoria has clay-heavy soil, and basements here can have seasonal moisture migration that'll push right through a coating if you don't address it first.

We do a moisture test before every basement job. If we're seeing elevated readings, we talk through the right approach — either a moisture-tolerant epoxy primer or addressing the source before we coat. We'll tell you straight if a floor isn't a good candidate for epoxy right now. That's not a sales pitch — it's just how we operate.

East Peoria basement tip: If you've had any water intrusion in the past — even a slow seep along the wall — get that sealed before calling us for epoxy. A waterproofed slab that stays dry will outlast any coating on a damp floor by 10+ years.

What to Expect on Install Day

Here's how a typical East Peoria residential garage job runs:

  1. Morning arrival, 7–8 AM. We show up with our grinder, shop vac, and materials. You don't need to do anything except have the garage empty.
  2. Concrete prep. We diamond-grind the entire surface, clean up the dust, fill any cracks or divots. This takes 1–2 hours depending on the condition of the slab.
  3. Base coat application. Epoxy goes down and we broadcast the chip (if applicable). This cures for a few hours.
  4. Topcoat. Polyaspartic topcoat gets rolled on in the afternoon. This is what makes the floor scratch-resistant, chemical-resistant, and UV-stable.
  5. Walkthrough and cure instructions. We do a final walkthrough with you before we leave. 24-hour wait for foot traffic, 72 hours minimum before vehicles.

Most jobs are done in a single day. We don't leave a half-finished floor overnight. If something comes up and we can't finish — bad weather, unforeseen slab issue — we tell you immediately and reschedule, no hassle.

What's the difference between epoxy and the floor paint sold at hardware stores?

Hardware store floor paint is typically a one-part latex product with a small amount of epoxy added for marketing. It looks fine for a season or two but peels badly under tire heat, oil, and salt. A real epoxy system is a two-part chemically cured product — and when we add a polyaspartic topcoat, it can handle hot tires, harsh chemicals, and Midwest winters without peeling. There's no comparison in longevity.

Most residential garage floors in East Peoria take one day for installation, with a full cure in 24–72 hours before foot traffic and three to seven days before parking vehicles. Concrete prep — grinding and profiling the slab — is the most time-intensive part of the job and the most important. We do not skip it.
