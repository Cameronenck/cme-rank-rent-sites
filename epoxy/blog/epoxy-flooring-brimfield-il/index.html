The Installation Process

Here's what happens from the day I pick up the phone to the day you drive on it:

  1. Phone estimate: We talk through your space, your timeline, and your budget. No charge.
  2. On-site inspection: I walk the floor, test for moisture, check for cracks or damage. I bring photos and notes home.
  3. Detailed quote: Based on square footage, condition, and the system you choose, you get a fixed price in writing.
  4. Prep work (1–2 days before): Diamond grinding removes old paint, sealer, and coating. We clean and repair cracks. The concrete has to be bare and solid.
  5. Moisture testing: Brimfield gets moisture. We measure to make sure your slab is ready for epoxy (under 3% is ideal).
  6. Primer application: Seals the concrete and ensures the epoxy bonds perfectly.
  7. Epoxy application: Basecoat and topcoat, with your choice of color and finish (solid, flake, or metallic).
  8. Curing: No foot traffic for 72 hours. Standard epoxy cures in 5–7 days before you can park on it. Polyaspartic topcoats cure in 24 hours if speed is critical.

Epoxy vs. Other Coatings

I get asked this a lot. Here's the honest comparison:

  • Epoxy vs. Paint: Paint sits on top and peels. Epoxy bonds to concrete and lasts years longer.
  • Epoxy vs. Polished Concrete: Polished is slippery and can't resist stains. Epoxy is grippy and stain-proof.
  • Epoxy vs. Polyurea: Both are strong, but polyurea cures faster (24 hours vs. 5–7 days). Polyurea costs more. Epoxy is the value pick for most Brimfield homeowners.
  • Epoxy vs. Polyaspartic Topcoat: Polyaspartic is epoxy with a fast-setting topcoat. Costs more, cures faster. If you need the floor in 24 hours, we can do it.

Brimfield's Concrete Challenges

I've sealed enough Brimfield floors to know the local variables:

  • Clay soil: It holds moisture. Your concrete sits on clay, and spring rain moves through it. That's why we test for moisture before any coating goes down.
  • Seasonal foundation shifts: Illinois freeze-thaw cycles work your foundation. Older concrete cracks. We prep and seal to prevent water intrusion under the epoxy.
  • Older concrete: Many Brimfield properties have 30+ year old slabs. They need more prep — grinding, patching, crack repair — but they take epoxy beautifully once ready.
  • Agricultural residue: Equipment tracking soil and fertilizer into garages is normal. Epoxy wipes clean. Sealed concrete doesn't stain.

Maintenance and Longevity

Do this, and your Brimfield epoxy floor will look good for 5–10 years:

  • Sweep weekly: Keep grit and dirt off the surface.
  • Mop monthly: Warm water and a mild cleaner. Dry it afterward.
  • Address spills immediately: Oil and fuel don't damage epoxy, but they look bad. Wipe them up.
  • Avoid dragging metal: Forklifts and equipment with sharp edges can scratch. Use plywood or mats for heavy equipment.
  • Avoid rapid temperature changes: Don't hose down a hot garage floor. Let it cool naturally.
