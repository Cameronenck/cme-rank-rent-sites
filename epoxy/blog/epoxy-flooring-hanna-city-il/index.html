The Installation Process in Hanna City

Here's what happens from start to finish:

1. Free On-Site Estimate

I come out to Hanna City, look at your floor, talk through what you want, and answer questions. I'll check moisture levels with a calcium chloride test, look for cracks or damage, and discuss color and finish options. You get a detailed quote before any work starts.

2. Concrete Prep

Prep is everything. I diamond-grind the entire floor to remove old paint, sealer, oils, or contamination. I fill cracks with self-leveling epoxy, repair any spalls or damage, and clean away all dust. The concrete surface has to be clean and open for the epoxy to bond properly. This usually takes 6–8 hours depending on floor size and condition.

3. Moisture Testing

Before coating, I run moisture tests. Central Illinois humidity and groundwater mean some slabs have high moisture vapor transmission rates. If moisture is present, I use a moisture-blocking epoxy primer to prevent the topcoat from failing later.

4. Epoxy Application

I roll on the epoxy coat in thin, even layers using professional-grade equipment. If you've chosen a flake system, the flakes go down while the first coat is wet, and we seal them with a topcoat. If you want metallic epoxy, I mix the pigments carefully and apply in a way that lets the color flow and move. The whole installation usually takes one full day for a standard garage.

5. Cure and Walkthrough

Epoxy sets overnight but needs time to fully harden. We schedule light foot traffic only for the first 72 hours. After 5–7 days, the floor can handle vehicle parking and normal use. I come back to do a final walkthrough and show you how to care for your new floor.

Comparing Epoxy to Other Coatings

You'll see other options advertised. Here's how they stack up:

  • Epoxy vs. Polyurethane: Polyurethane is thinner and cheaper upfront, but epoxy is harder and lasts longer. Epoxy handles chemical spills better. Both are good; epoxy is the premium choice.
  • Epoxy vs. Polished Concrete: Polished concrete looks smooth and clean but offers no chemical protection. It stains easily. Epoxy over polished concrete is the best of both worlds — shiny and protected.
  • Epoxy vs. Paint: Garage paint is a budget option, but it peels within 2–3 years with traffic and moisture. Epoxy is an investment that lasts 5–10 years or more.
  • Epoxy vs. Polyaspartic: Polyaspartic is a newer coating that cures faster (you can drive on it in 24 hours). It's more expensive but great if you need your floor ready quickly. We offer both.

Maintenance and Longevity

Your epoxy floor will last 5–10 years with basic care:

  • Sweep regularly: Remove dirt and grit so you're not grinding them into the surface.
  • Damp mop: Clean with a damp mop and mild soap and water. No harsh chemicals needed.
  • Address spills fast: Oil, fuel, and antifreeze won't stain or damage epoxy, but wipe them up anyway so they don't get tracked around.
  • Avoid dragging: Don't drag metal objects or heavy equipment across the floor. Use dollies or sliders when moving heavy items.
  • No open flames: Epoxy is flammable during application, but once cured it's fire-resistant. Still, don't use open flames or extreme heat directly on the surface.

If you take care of it, your epoxy floor will stay looking sharp for years. After 8–10 years, you might choose to apply a topcoat refresh, but the base system should hold strong.
