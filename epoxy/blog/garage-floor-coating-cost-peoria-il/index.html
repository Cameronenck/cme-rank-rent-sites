What Drives the Cost Up or Down

1. Concrete Condition

This is the biggest variable I see. A clean, well-cured slab with no previous coatings and no major damage is the best-case scenario. But most Peoria garages have been through 20-plus winters — I'm talking about moisture intrusion, old latex paint, oil stains from leaky vehicles, and cracks from frost heave. Any of those require extra prep work, and that costs money.

  • Diamond grinding: $0.50–$1.50/sq ft added cost
  • Crack repair: $50–$200 per crack depending on depth and length
  • Oil stain treatment: $75–$150 per stain
  • Stripping old paint or sealer: $150–$400

When I quote a job, I inspect the slab first. That's not a sales tactic — it's the only way to give you an accurate number.

3. Coating System and Layers

A single-layer roll-on epoxy from the hardware store is not the same thing as a professional multi-coat system. What I install has at least three layers: a penetrating primer that bonds to the concrete at a molecular level, a pigmented base coat, and a commercial-grade clear topcoat. Some systems add a fourth decorative layer with vinyl flake broadcast for texture and slip resistance.

More layers cost more money, but they also mean a coating that lasts 7–15 years instead of 1–2.

4. Turnaround Time

Standard epoxy takes 24 hours to cure before you can walk on it and 72 hours before you park a vehicle on it. Polyurea/polyaspartic systems cure in 4–6 hours — same-day use in most cases. If you need your garage back quickly, expect to pay a premium for the faster system.

Epoxy vs. Polyurea: Which Is Right for Your Peoria Garage?

I've written about this in more detail in our epoxy vs. polyurea comparison post, but here's the short version:

  • Epoxy is the better value for most residential garages. Solid, proven, looks great, and handles daily use without complaint.
  • Polyurea handles Peoria's freeze-thaw cycles better, won't yellow in UV light (important for garages with skylights or sunlit doors), and gives you same-day use. Worth the extra investment if longevity is the priority.

I'll tell you honestly which one makes sense for your situation when I come out — I'm not pushing one over the other for margin reasons.

DIY vs. Hiring a Pro: The Real Math

I understand the appeal of the $80 DIY epoxy kit. Here's what most people find out after they try it: the prep work alone — acid etching, patching, degreasing — takes a full day if you do it right. The kit paints on fine. Then winter comes, the concrete breathes, and by spring the coating is peeling in sheets.

Professional installation uses moisture-tolerant primers, mechanical surface preparation (diamond grinding), and commercial-grade coatings with UV-stable topcoats. The difference isn't just cosmetic — it's the difference between a floor that lasts 10 years and one that lasts 18 months. For most Peoria homeowners, the math works out in favor of doing it once and doing it right.

What to Expect on Install Day

If you've never watched a professional epoxy installation, here's how it goes. We show up, move any vehicles and items off the slab, then diamond-grind the entire surface. That opens the concrete's pores and gives the coating a mechanical bond — it's not optional. Then we fill any cracks or divots, apply the primer, let it tack, broadcast the base coat (and flake, if that's the look you want), and roll the topcoat. The whole process takes 4–8 hours depending on the system and garage size. We do a walkthrough before we leave and go over care instructions.

That's it. No mess, no lingering smells after 24 hours, no follow-up visits needed — just a floor that looks like a showroom when we're done.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does garage floor coating cost in Peoria IL?

Most Peoria homeowners pay $1,200–$4,500 for a professionally coated 2-car garage (400–500 sq ft). The price depends on coating type, concrete condition, and any surface prep needed. Epoxy runs $3–$7/sq ft installed; polyurea/polyaspartic runs $5–$10/sq ft.

What's the difference between epoxy and polyurea garage floor coatings?

Epoxy is a two-part resin that bonds hard to concrete — durable, affordable, great for most Peoria garages. Polyurea (or polyaspartic) cures faster, handles Peoria's freeze-thaw cycles better, and has higher UV resistance. Polyurea costs more upfront but lasts longer in harsh Illinois winters.

Does concrete condition affect the price?

Yes — significantly. If your garage slab has cracks, spalling, previous paint, or oil stains, prep work adds $150–$600 to the project. Diamond grinding alone can run $0.50–$1.50/sq ft. I inspect every slab before quoting so there are no surprises.

How long does a garage floor coating last in Illinois?

A professionally installed epoxy coating lasts 5–10 years with basic maintenance. Polyurea coatings typically last 10–20 years. Illinois winters are tough on coatings — that's why we use systems specifically rated for freeze-thaw cycling and road salt resistance.
