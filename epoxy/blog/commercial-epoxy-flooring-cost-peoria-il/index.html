Commercial Epoxy Flooring Cost in Peoria IL

Published May 23, 2026  |  Peoria Epoxy Flooring Pros

If you run a business in Peoria and your concrete floor is taking a beating, commercial epoxy is one of the smartest investments you can make. But how much does it actually cost? The answer depends on the space type, the condition of your existing slab, and the coating system you choose. This guide breaks it all down so you can budget accurately before you call anyone.

Commercial Epoxy Flooring Cost Per Square Foot in Peoria IL

Most commercial epoxy flooring projects in Peoria range from $3 to $12 per square foot installed. That wide range exists because a basic single-coat system for a warehouse is a completely different animal than a decorative, slip-resistant floor for a restaurant kitchen. These figures assume the slab is in fair to good condition. If there are significant cracks, active moisture, or old coatings that need grinding off, surface prep costs can add $0.50 to $2.00 per square foot to the total.

What Drives the Price Up or Down

1. Coating System Thickness

Thicker coatings cost more but last longer and handle heavier traffic. A single-coat water-based epoxy at 3 mils is inexpensive but not suitable for forklift environments. A 100% solids two-coat system at 20+ mils is the standard for serious commercial use and is worth every dollar in a high-traffic facility.

2. Surface Preparation

Shot blasting or diamond grinding is non-negotiable for proper adhesion. Most reputable contractors in Peoria include this in their quote, but always confirm. Skipping prep is how you end up with peeling epoxy within two years.

3. Square Footage

Larger projects benefit from economies of scale. A 15,000 sq ft warehouse floor will have a lower per-square-foot cost than a 1,200 sq ft retail office. If you are comparing quotes, always look at total cost and system specs together, not just the per-square-foot number.

4. Specialty Requirements

Restaurant kitchens need slip-resistant finishes and coved bases to meet health code. Medical and pharmaceutical spaces may require ESD (electrostatic discharge) or antimicrobial coatings. These add cost but are mandatory in certain applications.

Is Commercial Epoxy Worth It vs. Other Flooring?

Compared to VCT tile, polished concrete, or industrial carpet, epoxy wins on total cost of ownership. VCT needs stripping and waxing multiple times a year. Polished concrete, while durable, lacks the chemical resistance of epoxy. Commercial epoxy requires almost no maintenance beyond daily sweeping and occasional damp mopping. With a 10 to 20 year lifespan in most commercial applications, the numbers favor epoxy even if the upfront cost is higher than alternatives.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does commercial epoxy flooring cost per square foot in Peoria IL?
Commercial epoxy flooring in Peoria IL typically costs between $3 and $12 per square foot installed, depending on the application type, surface condition, and coating system chosen. Warehouses and light industrial spaces fall on the lower end; medical, restaurant, and specialty environments run higher due to system requirements.
Is commercial epoxy flooring worth the investment for Peoria businesses?
Yes. Commercial epoxy floors last 10 to 20 years with minimal maintenance, making them far more cost-effective than tile, VCT, or bare concrete alternatives over the long term. The durability, chemical resistance, and ease of cleaning make epoxy a favorite for high-traffic Peoria businesses.
How long does it take to install commercial epoxy flooring?
Most commercial installations in Peoria take one to three days depending on square footage and coating system. Multi-coat systems with moisture barriers may take an additional day to cure between coats. We schedule jobs to minimize downtime and can work nights or weekends for businesses that cannot close during normal hours.
Can epoxy flooring handle heavy forklifts and pallet jacks?
Yes. Heavy-duty industrial epoxy systems are rated for forklift traffic and point loads. We use 100% solids epoxy with a broadcast aggregate top coat for warehouses and manufacturing floors that need maximum durability. This system resists abrasion, impact, and chemical spills far better than standard paint or single-coat products.
