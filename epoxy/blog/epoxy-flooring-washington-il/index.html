The Epoxy Process for Washington, IL Floors

A proper epoxy installation isn't something you slap on over a weekend with a roller from the hardware store. The prep work is where everything either succeeds or fails. Here's what a professional installation looks like:

Surface Preparation

We start by diamond grinding or shot blasting the concrete. This opens the pores of the slab, removes any previous coatings or sealers, and creates the mechanical profile the epoxy needs to bond properly. Washington's older homes often have 30- to 50-year-old concrete slabs with surface contamination — oil stains from decades of parking, efflorescence from moisture, or residue from prior coatings. We deal with all of it before a drop of epoxy goes down.

Crack and Joint Repair

Control joints and hairline cracks in the slab get filled with a flexible filler or rigid epoxy compound depending on whether the crack is active or dormant. We flag any structural concerns before we start — if a crack is growing from a soil settlement issue, epoxy won't fix that and we'll tell you so.

Primer Coat

A penetrating primer bonds to the prepared slab and gives the base coat something solid to grip. In Washington's climate, we pay particular attention to moisture vapor transmission — slabs that push moisture upward from below need a moisture-mitigating primer to prevent delamination later.

Base and Broadcast Coats

The epoxy base coat goes down in one pass while wet. For flake finishes — our most popular option in Washington — decorative chips are broadcast into the wet base to full rejection, then scraped back and sealed. For solid colors, we skip the chips and move straight to topcoat.

Topcoat / Sealer

A clear polyurea or polyaspartic topcoat seals everything in. This is what gives the floor its finished gloss or satin appearance and protects the epoxy layer from UV yellowing, chemical splash, and abrasion. Most floors are ready for foot traffic within 24 hours.

How long does epoxy flooring last in Washington, IL's climate?

A professionally installed epoxy system with a quality topcoat typically lasts 10 to 20 years in central Illinois conditions. The key is proper surface prep and a UV-resistant polyurea or polyaspartic topcoat. Cheap DIY kits from hardware stores rarely last more than a few years.

How long before I can park on epoxy flooring?

With a polyurea or polyaspartic topcoat, you can park on the floor within 24 hours in most cases. Cure time varies by temperature and humidity — cooler weather slows the process slightly. We'll give you specific instructions before we leave the job.
