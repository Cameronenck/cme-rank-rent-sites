What Makes Decorative Epoxy Different?

Standard epoxy flooring is solid color — typically gray, tan, or clear. It's clean, professional, and durable. But decorative epoxy goes further. It takes your concrete foundation and turns it into a design statement.

Here's the difference:

  • Standard epoxy: Single color base coat with clear topcoat. Professional, timeless, industrial look.
  • Decorative epoxy: Engineered patterns — flakes, metallics, terrazzo effects, or custom colors — embedded into the finish itself. The design is part of the structure, not on top of it.

From an installation perspective, both are applied the same way. What changes is what gets applied before the topcoat. With decorative epoxy, we're adding design elements at specific stages, which is why it's genuinely skilled work. A bad application looks like someone threw confetti on a floor. A good one looks intentional, cohesive, and professional.

The Main Decorative Epoxy Styles (And Which Works Best in Peoria)

1. Flake Epoxy

This is the most popular choice we install in Peoria, and for good reason. Think of it like you're embedding colored chips into the epoxy. The flakes come in multiple sizes and colors — we typically use 20% and 40% coverage, which affects how much texture and visual interest you get.

Here's why Peoria customers love flake: it hides imperfections (dust, minor marks, footprints) while still looking intentional. If you have a garage, basement, or showroom, flake gives you that "professionally installed" appearance without being too trendy. We've seen flake floors age beautifully — they look as good at year 5 as they did on day 1.

Cost: 20-30% more than standard epoxy. So if standard runs $800-1200 for a 300 sq ft garage, decorative flake runs $1000-1500.

2. Metallic Epoxy

This is the Instagram-worthy option. Metallic pigments are mixed into the epoxy, and as the floor cures, they create natural swirling patterns — no two floors look identical. We've done silver, copper, gold, and bronze effects. Each one catches light differently and creates depth that flat colors simply can't match.

Fair warning: metallic is stunning but requires more precision in application. If the epoxy isn't mixed consistently or applied at the right speed, you lose the effect. We use a specific roller technique to ensure the pigments distribute evenly. Done right, it's showroom-quality. Done carelessly, it's a waste of money.

Cost: 40-60% more than standard. For a 300 sq ft garage, expect $1200-1800.

3. Terrazzo Epoxy

Think of this as decorative flake's more sophisticated cousin. Terrazzo uses different colored flakes at varying sizes, sometimes mixed with glass or stone chips, to create a rich, textured appearance. It's what you'd see in high-end commercial spaces, but we're now installing it in residential garages and basements across Peoria because the price has come down and the quality has improved.

Terrazzo works especially well if you want a floor that doesn't shout "epoxy" — it just looks like a premium material that happens to be incredibly durable.

Cost: 50-70% more than standard epoxy. Plan on $1300-2000 for a typical residential space.

4. Custom Two-Tone or Gradient

We can also create custom two-color bases or gradient effects — imagine a floor that starts dark gray near the walls and gradually shifts to light gray in the center. This requires careful planning and application, but the result is a floor that genuinely looks custom.

Cost: Similar to metallic, 40-60% premium.

The Installation Process for Decorative Epoxy

The foundation is identical to standard epoxy: proper surface prep, moisture testing, grinding, etching. But once we get to the application stage, here's where decorative epoxy diverges:

Day 1-2: Prep (same as standard) — Grind, clean, etch, seal cracks. This is 60% of the success, and we don't cut corners.

Day 3: Primer + First Epoxy Coat — We apply a moisture barrier, then the base epoxy.

Day 3-4: Design Application — This is where decorative epoxy happens. For flake, we broadcast the chips onto the wet epoxy and let them set. For metallic, we pour and spread the pigmented epoxy at a precise rate. For terrazzo, we're layering different sized aggregates. Timing is critical — apply too early and the flakes sink into the base coat; too late and they don't bond. This is purely experience.

Day 4-5: Topcoat — We seal everything with a clear protective topcoat. This is what locks the design in place and provides UV protection so your metallic doesn't fade or your colors don't shift over time.

Total timeline: 5-7 days including cure time. Your floor will be unusable for at least 72 hours after application.

How to Choose Your Decorative Epoxy Design

Here's what I tell customers who are overwhelmed by the options:

Step 1: Consider the space's purpose. A showroom or retail floor benefits from high-impact metallic. A garage or workshop where durability matters more than impression? Flake is perfect. A basement that'll become a game room or gym? Go with what makes you happy — any decorative option will hold up.

Step 2: Think about maintenance. All decorative epoxy is equally easy to clean, but lighter colors show dust more readily. If you have an active family or business, consider flake with darker chips or metallic with a darker base.

Step 3: Match your existing space. Pull out your wall colors, cabinet finishes, and furniture. A blue-gray metallic epoxy might be stunning in a white modern garage but clash with warm-toned basement walls. Our design consultants will help you see samples in the actual lighting of your space, not just in a showroom.

Step 4: Plan for the future. Epoxy lasts 10-15+ years with normal maintenance. That's a long time to look at the same design. Choose something you'll love, not something that's trendy this year.

Decorative Epoxy Maintenance and Longevity

One of the best parts about decorative epoxy? It's not high-maintenance. Once installed, here's all you need to do:

  • Regular sweeping: Keep dust and debris off the surface. A broom or soft-bristle push sweep is fine.
  • Weekly wet cleaning: Mop with pH-neutral cleaner diluted in water. Avoid acidic cleaners that can dull the finish over time.
  • Quarterly deep clean: Use a mild degreaser if needed for stubborn marks. A pressure washer on low setting is fine but not necessary.
  • Spill cleanup: Wipe up oils, chemicals, or harsh liquids immediately. Epoxy is resistant but not immune.

Most decorative epoxy floors we installed 8-10 years ago are still pristine. The flakes haven't shifted, the metallics haven't faded, the colors are still vivid. That's what a proper topcoat and correct application gets you.

FAQ — Decorative Epoxy Questions Answered

How much does decorative concrete epoxy cost in Peoria? ›

Decorative epoxy typically costs 20-70% more than standard epoxy, depending on the style. For a 300 sq ft garage, expect $1000-2000 total (labor + materials). Flake is the most affordable decorative option; terrazzo and high-end metallic are the premium choices. We provide detailed estimates with photos of similar completed projects so you know exactly what you're investing in.

Will decorative epoxy fade or discolor over time? ›

No, not with proper installation and a quality topcoat. We use UV-resistant epoxy systems specifically because Peoria gets direct sunlight in many garages and basements. The flakes, metallics, and colors are locked into the epoxy structure, not sitting on top. That said, extremely heavy sunlight (south-facing windows all day) may cause minimal color shift over 12+ years. It's rare and subtle, but worth knowing.

Can I customize the design, or do I choose from pre-set options? ›

You can do both. We have standard color palettes and flake combinations that are proven and work well, but we also do custom designs. If you want a specific color combination or a unique pattern, we'll work with you to make it happen. The lead time increases slightly for custom orders, but the result is a floor that's truly one-of-a-kind.

How long until I can use the floor after installation? ›

Full cure takes 7 days, but you can walk on the floor after 72 hours with light footsteps. Heavy use (cars in the garage, equipment rolling) should wait the full 7 days. We'll give you a detailed care schedule when we finish the installation so you know exactly when you can resume normal use.

What if I don't like the design after installation? ›

This is why we do in-person design consultations. We'll show you samples in your actual space before you commit. We've never had a customer regret their choice once they've seen the finished product because they've already approved the design. But if something unexpected happens, epoxy can be stripped and reapplied — it's not inexpensive, but it's possible.

Is decorative epoxy slippery, or is it safe to walk on? ›

Epoxy with flakes or terrazzo has natural texture and is not slippery. Metallic and smooth finishes can be slightly slick when wet, but we can add an anti-slip topcoat if you're in a high-moisture environment or need extra traction. Just let us know during the consultation.
