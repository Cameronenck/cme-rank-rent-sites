What Epoxy Flooring Involves

Epoxy is a resin coating applied to a prepared concrete surface. When done right, it bonds chemically to the concrete and creates a surface that's harder, easier to clean, and far more resistant to stains and damage than bare concrete. In Morton, where winters can be rough on garage floors from road salt and freeze-thaw cycles, it's a practical upgrade, not just a cosmetic one.

A proper installation involves three main steps: surface prep (usually mechanical grinding to open the concrete), applying primer or base coat, and then the top coat with optional flake or metallic overlay. Skipping prep is the most common mistake — and it's why DIY kits and budget contractors fail within a year or two.

Typical Cost for Epoxy Flooring in Morton IL

Garage floor epoxy in Morton typically runs $3–$7 per square foot for a standard two-car garage (around 400–500 sq ft). That puts most jobs in the $1,200–$3,500 range depending on the system.

  • Single-color epoxy: $3–$5/sq ft — clean, durable, most cost-effective
  • Flake broadcast system: $4–$6/sq ft — adds texture, hides minor imperfections, more slip-resistant
  • Polyurea or polyaspartic topcoat: $5–$8/sq ft — faster cure, better UV stability, premium durability
  • Metallic epoxy: $6–$10/sq ft — decorative, swirled effect, popular in showrooms and upscale garages

Basement floors, commercial spaces, and areas with significant existing damage will vary. The only way to get an accurate number is an on-site look at the actual condition of your concrete.

What Drives the Price Up or Down

Concrete condition is the biggest variable. Heavily cracked, pitted, or previously painted concrete takes more prep time. Floors with oil contamination need additional cleaning steps before the epoxy will bond properly.

Size matters in two directions — larger jobs have lower per-square-foot cost, but very large commercial applications may require multiple crews and days, which affects scheduling and total price.

System choice affects both cost and longevity. A standard epoxy over polyurea top coat is a solid, durable system for most Morton homeowners. Polyaspartic-only systems cure faster (important if you can't have the garage out of service for long) but cost more.

Morton-Specific Considerations

Morton and the surrounding Tazewell County area has a mix of older homes with aged concrete slabs and newer construction. On older slabs — anything poured before the mid-1990s — we typically check for moisture vapor transmission before applying epoxy. Moisture coming up through the slab is a primary cause of delamination. A moisture barrier coat adds to the cost but protects the investment.

Road salt from Illinois winters is also a serious concern for garage floors in Morton. Bare concrete absorbs salt brine through the winter and the damage accumulates over years. A properly installed epoxy system seals the surface and protects against that cycle of damage.

How to Evaluate Contractors

Ask any contractor you're considering two questions: What surface prep method do you use? And what's the cure time before I can park on it?

A good contractor uses mechanical diamond grinding — not acid etching — for proper surface prep. And a properly installed epoxy system needs at least 72 hours before vehicle traffic, ideally longer in cold weather. If a contractor says you can park on it the same day or next morning, that's a red flag.

How long does epoxy flooring last on a Morton garage floor?
A properly installed epoxy system with a quality topcoat should last 10–15 years on a residential garage floor with normal use. Floors that see heavy vehicle traffic or chemical spills may need re-coating sooner. The key is proper surface prep and the right system for your specific use.
Can you apply epoxy over an existing painted floor in Morton?
It depends on the existing coating. We'd need to test adhesion and see what's on the floor. In many cases, existing coatings need to be ground off first. Applying epoxy over a failed or poorly-adhered coating is one of the most common causes of early delamination.
How long before I can use my garage after epoxy installation?
We recommend 72 hours before light foot traffic and 5–7 days before driving vehicles on the floor. In cooler Illinois temperatures (below 50°F), cure time extends. We'll give you specific timing based on the weather and the system we're using on your floor.
What's the difference between epoxy and polyurea for a Morton garage?
Both work well. Standard epoxy is more affordable and gives you great results. Polyurea (or polyaspartic) cures faster — in some cases a same-day job — and has better UV resistance, so it won't yellow over time. For most Morton homeowners, a good epoxy base with a polyurea topcoat is the best balance of cost and performance.
