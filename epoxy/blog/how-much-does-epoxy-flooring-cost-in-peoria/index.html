How Much Does Epoxy Flooring Cost in Peoria IL? [2026 Guide]

Updated May 2026 • 6 min read • By Peoria Epoxy Flooring Experts

Epoxy floors have gotten popular fast around Peoria — you see them in garages, basements, showrooms, and commercial shops all over the area. And with that popularity comes a lot of pricing confusion. One company quotes $800. Another quotes $3,500. Same 480-square-foot garage. So what's actually going on?

We're a Peoria-based epoxy flooring company, and we've coated hundreds of floors across central Illinois. Here's the straight breakdown on what you'll pay and why.

Epoxy Flooring Cost: The Short Answer

Professional epoxy flooring in Peoria runs $2 to $12 per square foot installed, depending on the product type, the condition of your floor, and the complexity of the job. That wide range is real — here's what each tier actually means:

Epoxy Type Description Installed Price (per sqft)
DIY Kit Big-box store product, homeowner-applied $50–$150 total (materials only)
Water-Based Epoxy Professional entry-level, good for light-use garages $2 – $4/sqft
100% Solids Epoxy Thicker, more durable — standard for most garage jobs $4 – $7/sqft
Metallic / Decorative High-end aesthetic finish with flake or metallic pigment $6 – $12/sqft
Typical 2-Car Garage ~480 sqft, 100% solids with flake broadcast $900 – $2,500

For most Peoria homeowners doing a two-car garage with a quality 100% solids product and chip flakes, you're looking at $900–$2,500 all in. That's the most common job we do.

What Drives the Price Up (or Down)

Square footage is just the starting point. These factors move your number:

  • Floor condition. A clean, flat, dry slab costs the least to prep. Cracks, spalling, old paint, moisture issues, or previous coatings that need grinding — all of that adds prep time and cost. We won't skip prep to save you money. Skipped prep is why epoxy jobs fail.
  • Coating thickness. Water-based epoxy goes on thin and cures fast. 100% solids is thicker and more durable. Polyaspartic topcoats add UV resistance and faster cure times. Each layer adds cost, and each layer serves a purpose.
  • Decorative options. Plain solid color is cheaper. Vinyl chip/flake broadcast looks great and adds grip. Metallic epoxy with swirl effects takes more time and skill. You're paying for the appearance and the labor.
  • Number of coats. A proper 3-coat system (primer, base, topcoat) will outlast a 2-coat job by years. Some budget companies quote you on 2 coats. Ask before you sign.
  • Access and shape. A square garage with nothing in it is the easiest job. Oddly shaped spaces, columns, drains, or floors packed with stuff take more time.

DIY vs. Professional Epoxy: The Real Comparison

Big-box store kits run $50–$150 and look appealing. The problem is most of them are water-based with low solids content — they're thin, they chip at the edges, and they don't bond well to concrete that hasn't been diamond-ground. Most fail within two or three years.

Professional-grade products need professional prep. If you want a floor that lasts 10–20 years and handles Illinois winters, hot tire pickup, and actual use, it's worth having it done right. A $1,200 professional job that holds up for 15 years beats a $150 DIY job you redo every two years.

Basement and Commercial Epoxy Pricing

Basement floors run similarly to garages — $3–$8/sqft depending on the system and prep needed. Moisture testing is a bigger factor in basements, and some slabs need a moisture mitigation coating added before anything else goes down.

Commercial jobs are priced by the project. A 1,000-square-foot shop floor is different from a 10,000-square-foot warehouse with heavy forklift traffic. We'll walk through any commercial space and give you a flat quote.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does epoxy flooring last?

A properly installed professional epoxy system in a garage or basement typically lasts 10–20 years with normal use. The lifespan depends heavily on prep quality, coating thickness, and how hard the floor gets used. Hot tire pickup, chemicals, and heavy impacts are the main things that wear it down faster. We use 100% solids systems with polyaspartic topcoats specifically because they hold up to real-world abuse better than cheaper water-based products.

Can you epoxy over an existing floor coating?

It depends on what's down there. If the existing coating is well-bonded, we can sometimes scuff and overcoat. If it's peeling, bubbling, or if there's moisture under it — it needs to come off first. Skipping that step is the number-one reason epoxy jobs fail. We'll check the existing floor before we quote you and tell you exactly what's needed.

What's the best epoxy system for a garage floor in Peoria?

For a typical Peoria homeowner garage, we'd recommend a 100% solids epoxy base coat with a vinyl chip broadcast and a polyaspartic topcoat. It's slip-resistant, handles Illinois temperature swings well, cleans up easily, and looks sharp for years. The polyaspartic topcoat is key — it's UV-stable so it won't yellow from sunlight and cures fast enough that you can park on it within 24 hours.

How do you maintain an epoxy floor?

Epoxy is about as easy to maintain as flooring gets. Regular sweeping and mopping with a pH-neutral cleaner is all it takes. Avoid harsh chemicals like bleach or ammonia. If you notice any chips or worn spots along the edges, address them early before moisture gets underneath. Most epoxy floors in garages just need a quick sweep and hose-down to look good for years.
