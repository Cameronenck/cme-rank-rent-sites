Metallic Epoxy Flooring Cost in Peoria IL — What You'll Pay in 2026

Updated May 2026 • 7 min read • By Peoria Epoxy Flooring Pros

Metallic epoxy floors are a different category from standard garage floor coatings. They're the ones that look like liquid metal, marble, or ocean waves — the kind you see in high-end showrooms, restaurants, and residential living spaces where the floor is meant to be the statement. They also cost more than standard epoxy, and for good reason. Here's what you're actually paying for in Peoria.

The typical range for metallic epoxy flooring in Peoria IL is $7 to $14 per square foot installed, depending on the complexity of the design, the condition of the existing concrete, and the size of the project. That puts a 400 sq ft basement at $2,800–$5,600 installed. I'll break down exactly what pushes you toward the low or high end of that range.

What Drives Metallic Epoxy Cost in Peoria

Metallic epoxy is not a simple pour-and-roll job. The visual effect comes from manipulating metallic pigments within the wet epoxy using air, tools, and technique. Every floor is unique — there's no template. The skill and time involved is reflected in the price.

  • Concrete prep. This is the most underappreciated cost driver. The concrete must be mechanically ground or shot-blasted to create the right surface profile for adhesion. Cracks, control joints, and prior coatings need to be addressed. Prep can easily add $1–$3 per sq ft on top of the coating itself. Shortcuts here cause failures — peeling, bubbles, delamination. We don't cut this corner.
  • Single metallic vs. multi-pigment. A single metallic color (like pewter or bronze) is simpler to execute than a 3-color ocean wave pattern with depth effects. Complex designs require more product, more time, and more skill — and that pushes the number up.
  • Topcoat selection. Most metallic epoxy systems are finished with a polyurethane or polyaspartic topcoat. Higher-quality topcoats (UV-stable, commercial-grade) cost more but dramatically extend the floor's life and maintain its clarity over years. We spec the right topcoat for the intended use — residential garage vs. retail showroom vs. residential living space.
  • Project size. Smaller rooms cost more per square foot because setup, masking, and mobilization time are fixed. A 200 sq ft mudroom costs more per sq ft than a 1,000 sq ft open basement.
  • Existing concrete condition. Cracked, heaved, or moisture-damaged concrete requires more prep and potentially some repair work before any coating goes down. We assess this during the free in-person estimate.

Metallic Epoxy vs. Standard Epoxy — Is It Worth the Premium?

Standard solid-color epoxy for a two-car garage in Peoria runs $1,800–$3,500 installed. Metallic for the same space runs $3,200–$6,500. That's a real premium. Here's when it makes sense:

If the garage is a workshop or just a place to park cars, standard epoxy does everything you need. Metallic doesn't perform better mechanically — it just looks dramatically more impressive. If the space is a showroom, a living area extension, a home gym that doubles as a gathering space, or anywhere appearance matters, the metallic finish pays for itself in the visual impact and property value perception it creates.

We've installed metallic floors in Peoria homes where the basement became the social center of the house — home bars, entertainment spaces, workout rooms. In those spaces, the floor is the design element that ties everything together. In a utility garage, it's overkill. Knowing the difference is part of what we bring to the free estimate conversation.

How Long Do Metallic Epoxy Floors Last?

A properly installed metallic epoxy floor with a quality topcoat will last 15–25 years in a residential setting. Commercial settings with heavy foot traffic can expect 8–15 years before any visible wear on the topcoat. The epoxy itself doesn't degrade — surface wear happens at the topcoat level. When the topcoat shows wear, a topcoat reapplication (much less expensive than a full reinstall) refreshes the floor completely.

The #1 cause of early failure is improper concrete prep — not the product, not the design. If a contractor doesn't diamond grind and profile the concrete before coating, it will eventually peel. We always include mechanical prep in our process, and it's reflected in the quote.

How much does metallic epoxy flooring cost per square foot in Peoria IL?

How long does metallic epoxy flooring last?

Can metallic epoxy be installed in a garage?
