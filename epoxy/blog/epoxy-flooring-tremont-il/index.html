Our Process in Tremont

Free Estimate: We come out, assess the concrete, check for moisture, cracks, or contamination, and answer all your questions. No obligation, no hard sell.

Moisture Testing: This is non-negotiable in Tremont. Concrete that looks dry can still have moisture vapor transmission from groundwater. We test. If it's elevated, we use a vapor-barrier epoxy primer to prevent bubbling later.

Prep Work: Diamond grinding removes old sealers, paint, and the top layer of concrete. We fill cracks with polyurea filler, degrease any oily spots, and vacuum everything twice. This step is what separates a floor that lasts a decade from one that peels in a year.

Coating Application: Two or three coats, depending on your system, with proper cure time between coats. Most jobs are done in one day. Polyaspartic systems (premium option) cure faster and are ready for traffic in 24 hours instead of a week.

Inspection & Follow-up: We walk you through cleanup, maintenance, and how to care for your new floor. We stand behind our work with a warranty.

Epoxy vs. Other Concrete Coatings

You've probably heard of polyurea, concrete sealer, and paint. Here's the honest breakdown:

Epoxy: Best wear resistance, best looks, mid-range cost, cures in 5–7 days. Our go-to recommendation for almost every job.

Polyaspartic (Polyurea): Fastest cure time (24 hours), excellent UV resistance, premium cost. Ideal if you can't leave your garage unused for a week.

Concrete Sealer: Cheapest option, limited protection, won't hide stains, wears faster. Fine for storage areas, not ideal for garages or high-traffic spaces.

Paint: Looks cheap after a season, peels easily, offers no real protection. We don't recommend it.
