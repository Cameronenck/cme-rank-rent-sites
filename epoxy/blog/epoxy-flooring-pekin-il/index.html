Why Pekin Homeowners Choose Epoxy Flooring

Pekin sits in Tazewell County just across the Illinois River from Peoria, and the local climate is identical — hot summers, cold winters with serious freeze-thaw cycles, and late-spring humidity that can push moisture vapor right through a concrete slab. That combination is hard on bare concrete and on cheap box-store floor coatings that skip the preparation step.

A properly installed epoxy system does several things at once:

  • Seals the slab. A chemically bonded epoxy coating stops oil, water, and road salt from penetrating the concrete. This is the core function for garage floors — especially in Pekin where vehicles bring in road salt all winter.
  • Adds compressive strength. Epoxy coatings rated for 10,000+ PSI handle vehicle traffic, shop equipment, and dropped tools without chipping or flaking under normal use.
  • Looks finished. A decorative flake system or metallic epoxy turns an ordinary concrete floor into something that looks like it belongs in a showroom. It also dramatically brightens the space by reflecting light.
  • Simplifies cleaning. A smooth, sealed surface cleans up with a mop and mild detergent. Oil stains, dirt, and grit don't bond the way they do on raw concrete.

What the Installation Process Looks Like

The surface preparation step is where most cheap epoxy jobs fail, and it is the step we take the most seriously. We diamond-grind every slab to open the concrete pores and create a mechanical profile that the epoxy bonds to. A floor that was not ground properly will delaminate within a couple of years — you have probably seen the blistering, peeling results on older coatings in garages around Pekin and Peoria.

After grinding, we fill any cracks or spalls, let repairs cure, and test moisture vapor emission rates. Central Illinois slabs often run higher than the national average on moisture vapor — Tazewell County soil holds water, and slabs built on it can push measurable moisture vapor through the concrete. We use moisture-tolerant primer systems designed for these conditions rather than applying a standard epoxy and hoping for the best.

After the prep phase, installation is typically a two-day process: base coat on day one, decorative broadcast (if applicable) and topcoat on day two. The floor is ready for light foot traffic in 24 hours and full vehicle traffic in 72 hours.
