Rim Joist Insulation Cost in Peoria IL — What It Costs and Why It Matters

Updated May 2026 • 6 min read • By Peoria Spray Foam Pros

Most Peoria homeowners know their attic needs insulation. Fewer think about the rim joist — and that's a significant missed opportunity. The rim joist is consistently one of the worst air-leakage points in Midwest homes, and in Peoria's climate with cold winters and humid summers, sealing it properly can make a real difference in comfort and energy bills.

What Is the Rim Joist?

The rim joist (also called the band joist or sill plate area) is the perimeter framing at the top of your foundation wall — where the first-floor framing meets the foundation. In most homes built before 2000 in the Peoria area, it's a stack of 2x10 boards sitting directly on the concrete foundation wall, covered only by the exterior siding.

This area is problematic for a few reasons:

  • It's typically not insulated at all in older homes.
  • The wood is in direct contact with concrete, which is cold and can be damp.
  • Every floor joist creates a gap that allows cold air to infiltrate the floor above.
  • It runs the entire perimeter of the house — in a 1,200 sq ft ranch, that's 140+ linear feet of potential air leakage.

How Much Does Rim Joist Insulation Cost in Peoria?

The cost depends on the method and the size of your basement's perimeter:

MethodTypical CostPerformance
Fiberglass batts (cut and cobble)$400 – $900Moderate (air gaps remain)
Rigid foam board (cut and cobble)$500 – $1,100Good (better air seal if caulked)
Closed-cell spray foam$800 – $2,500Excellent (complete seal + moisture barrier)

Closed-cell spray foam is the most expensive option, but it's also the most effective. Here's why:

  • Complete air seal. Spray foam expands to fill every gap and crack, including the junction between the sill plate and foundation. No air moves through foam.
  • Moisture barrier. Closed-cell foam is a vapor retarder, which matters a lot in Peoria basements where moisture vapor can migrate through concrete.
  • No settling. Unlike fiberglass batts that can fall out or compress over time, spray foam stays put permanently.
  • R-value per inch. Closed-cell delivers R-6 to R-7 per inch, letting us achieve R-15 or better in the limited space of a rim joist cavity.

Energy Savings from Rim Joist Insulation

In Peoria, rim joist insulation is one of the better-performing energy upgrades for older homes. Here's what homeowners typically see:

  • Cold floor reduction. First-floor rooms directly above an uninsulated rim joist often feel cold in winter. Sealing the rim joist directly addresses this.
  • Heating cost savings. Typically 8–15% reduction in heating costs for homes with uninsulated rim joists. In a Peoria home spending $2,400/year on natural gas heating, that's $200–$360/year saved.
  • Payback period. Most rim joist spray foam jobs in Peoria pay back in 4–8 years through energy savings alone, not counting increased comfort.
  • Federal tax credit. Insulation improvements may qualify for the 25C energy efficiency tax credit — up to $1,200. Check with your tax preparer.

What to Expect from a Rim Joist Spray Foam Job

We work from inside the basement. You don't need to vacate the home. The process takes half a day to a full day depending on the perimeter size and access. We spray 2–3 inches of closed-cell foam directly onto the rim joist framing, covering the wood and creating a continuous thermal and air barrier around the entire perimeter.

Before we spray, we do a visual inspection looking for any signs of moisture intrusion, rot, or pest activity. If we find anything that needs to be addressed first, we'll tell you. Spray foam over a moisture problem can make things worse — we don't take shortcuts on that assessment.

After the job, we clean up and you're done. No drying time, no waiting — the foam cures in minutes. You'll notice the difference in floor warmth and draft reduction immediately in cold weather.
