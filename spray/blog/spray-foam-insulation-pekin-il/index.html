Why Pekin Homes Need Spray Foam

Pekin sits in Tazewell County just south of Peoria across the Illinois River. The housing stock here is older on average than a lot of Illinois communities — a lot of homes were built in the 1930s through 1960s when insulation standards were minimal or nonexistent. Even homes from the 1970s and 1980s typically have R-11 fiberglass batts in the walls and a few inches of blown insulation in the attic, which falls well short of modern efficiency standards.

The result is predictable: high heating and cooling bills, cold floors in winter, rooms that never quite reach the thermostat temperature, and humidity problems in the crawl space or basement. Spray foam insulation addresses all of these issues because it seals air leaks and insulates in the same application. Fiberglass and cellulose add R-value but do almost nothing to stop air movement, which is where most energy actually escapes.

Best Spray Foam Applications for Pekin IL Homes

Not every location in a home needs spray foam. Here's where the investment makes the most sense for a typical Pekin property:

  • Rim joists. The rim joist is the wood framing that runs around the perimeter of your foundation. It's almost always uninsulated or poorly insulated in older Pekin homes, and it's one of the biggest cold-air entry points in the whole building. Two inches of closed-cell spray foam seals it completely. Most homeowners notice the difference in the first winter — the floor above the basement stops feeling cold.
  • Crawl spaces. A lot of Pekin homes have vented crawl spaces, which is now understood to be the wrong approach for Illinois's climate. Vents let cold, moist air in every winter and humid air in every summer. Converting to a conditioned crawl space with spray foam on the walls eliminates the problem at the source and typically pays back in energy savings within a few years.
  • Attic air sealing. Even if you plan to add blown insulation on top, air sealing the attic floor first with spray foam makes a dramatic difference. Bypasses around can lights, plumbing penetrations, and top plates account for a significant fraction of heat loss in most homes. Sealing them costs a fraction of a full attic spray job and delivers strong returns.
  • Basement walls. For Pekin homes with poured concrete or block basements, spray foam on the interior walls creates a conditioned basement that's comfortable year-round and eliminates the cold-wall condensation problem that leads to mold and efflorescence.
  • Cathedral ceilings and knee walls. Cape Cod-style homes and older two-stories with finished attic space need insulation applied directly to the roof deck or knee wall framing. Closed-cell spray foam is usually the only option that achieves adequate R-value in the limited depth available.
  • New construction. Builders in the Pekin area increasingly use open-cell spray foam in wall cavities for code compliance and comfort. It creates a continuous air barrier that standard batts cannot match.

Open-Cell vs. Closed-Cell for Pekin IL

Both foam types work in this climate. Closed-cell spray foam has an R-value of about R-6.5 per inch and functions as a Class II vapor retarder, making it the right choice for any application below grade or anywhere moisture management is critical — rim joists, crawl spaces, basement walls, and rooflines. It also adds structural rigidity to walls and roof decks.

Open-cell spray foam has an R-value around R-3.7 per inch and is vapor-permeable, which makes it the better choice for attics and interior wall cavities where the assembly needs to be able to dry if moisture gets in. It's also significantly less expensive per square foot than closed-cell, which matters for large-volume applications like full attic fills.

The short version: closed-cell for moisture-critical applications, open-cell for air sealing where drying potential matters. Most Pekin homes benefit from both, just in different locations. What to Expect During Installation

Spray foam installation typically takes one to four hours for a focused application like rim joists or crawl space walls, and a full day for larger attic or basement projects. The foam is mixed and sprayed on-site — it expands to 30 to 100 times its liquid volume depending on the product, then cures fully within a few hours.

You'll need to vacate the space being sprayed during installation and for about 24 hours after, which is standard for all spray foam applications. There's no mess left behind, no cleanup required on your end. We trim excess foam and leave the space cleaner than we found it.

We pull permits when required by the project and by Tazewell County code. If a contractor tells you permits aren't necessary for a project that requires them, walk away.
