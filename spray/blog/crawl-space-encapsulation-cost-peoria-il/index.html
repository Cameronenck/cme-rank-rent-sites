Crawl Space Encapsulation Cost in Peoria IL — What You'll Actually Pay

Updated May 2026 • 8 min read • By Peoria Spray Foam Pros

Crawl space encapsulation is one of those home improvements that pays for itself over time in energy savings, moisture prevention, and air quality — but it has a wide price range that makes people nervous. I'm going to give you the honest numbers for Peoria IL homes, explain what drives the cost in each direction, and help you figure out what your crawl space actually needs before you call anyone.

For most Peoria homes, crawl space encapsulation runs $3,000 to $8,000. Large crawl spaces, severe moisture problems, or encapsulation combined with spray foam insulation on the walls and rim joists can push that number higher — up to $12,000 for large, complex jobs. Here's what's actually going into that price.

What Crawl Space Encapsulation Actually Includes

True encapsulation is more than just throwing a plastic sheet on the ground. A proper system typically includes:

  • Heavy-duty vapor barrier. At minimum 12-mil polyethylene, usually 20-mil for long-term durability. This goes on the ground and up the walls, sealed at seams and to the foundation walls with proper tape and adhesive. Cheap barriers fail at the seams — that's where moisture gets back in.
  • Rim joist insulation. The rim joists (where your floor framing meets the foundation wall) are the biggest air and moisture infiltration point in most Peoria homes. Spray foam is the right material here — it air-seals and insulates simultaneously. Two inches of closed-cell spray foam on the rim joists makes a significant difference in both energy use and moisture control.
  • Foundation wall insulation. In a sealed crawl space system, insulating the foundation walls is more effective than insulating the floor above. This keeps the crawl space within the conditioned envelope of the house.
  • Drainage and moisture management. Some crawl spaces have active water intrusion — not just humidity. If water is getting in through the walls or up through the floor, a vapor barrier alone won't fix it. Interior drainage channels and a sump pump may be needed.
  • Dehumidifier. In a sealed crawl space, a dedicated crawl space dehumidifier maintains the right humidity level. This isn't optional if you want the system to work long-term in Illinois's climate.

Why Illinois Homes Need Crawl Space Encapsulation

Peoria's climate is harsh on crawl spaces. Summers are hot and humid — relative humidity regularly hits 80–90% in July and August. That moisture migrates up from the ground and through vented crawl space walls directly into your subfloor and framing. Over years, that means wood rot, mold growth, and pest problems. In winter, a cold, drafty crawl space is one of the biggest sources of heat loss in older Peoria homes.

Traditional vented crawl spaces — the kind with those little foundation vents — were designed with an outdated theory that ventilation would dry out the crawl space. Decades of building science research have reversed that conclusion. In climates like Peoria's, those vents bring in humid outside air in summer and cold air in winter. Sealing and encapsulating is now the recognized best practice.

We've done hundreds of crawl spaces across Peoria, East Peoria, Morton, Washington, and the surrounding area. The most common story we hear: "The house felt cold no matter how much we ran the heat." After encapsulation and rim joist spray foam, that changes. The floor stops feeling cold. The HVAC doesn't cycle as often. Humidity in the living space drops. It's a whole-house improvement even though you're working in a space you'll never see again.

Signs Your Peoria Crawl Space Needs Attention

You don't need to get under the house to spot the warning signs:

  • Cold floors in rooms above the crawl space, even with the heat running
  • Musty or earthy smell coming from floor vents or lower levels
  • High humidity inside the house that your HVAC struggles to control
  • Visible condensation on pipes or ductwork in the crawl space
  • Wood floors cupping or buckling (a sign of moisture from below)
  • Increased pest activity — rodents and insects love humid, dark crawl spaces

How much does crawl space encapsulation cost in Peoria IL?

Is crawl space encapsulation worth it in Illinois?
Yes. In Peoria's climate, encapsulation pays back in energy savings, reduced moisture damage, better air quality, and pest prevention. Most homeowners see HVAC costs drop 10–20% after full encapsulation and rim joist sealing. The payback period is typically 5–8 years.
What is the difference between a vapor barrier and full encapsulation?
A vapor barrier is just the ground cover — a plastic sheet that blocks ground moisture. Full encapsulation includes vapor barrier, sealed foundation walls, rim joist insulation, and usually a dehumidifier. It creates a fully conditioned crawl space rather than just a partially protected one. The difference in performance is significant.
Do I need a dehumidifier after crawl space encapsulation?
In a sealed crawl space in Illinois's climate, yes. Without a dehumidifier, even a properly encapsulated crawl space can accumulate moisture from air infiltration over time. A dedicated crawl space dehumidifier (rated for the cubic footage) maintains the right humidity level and protects the investment.
