Why Open-Cell vs. Closed-Cell Changes Your Per-Square-Foot Price

This is the biggest price driver after location and access.

Open-cell foam is lighter, easier to apply, and reaches R-3.5 to R-3.6 per inch. If you need R-49 in your attic (which Peoria should have), that's roughly 14 inches of open-cell. The material is less dense and cheaper per gallon, which is why per-square-foot rates are lower. It's also more forgiving to apply — less risk of overspray or waste.

Closed-cell foam is denser and packs R-6 to R-6.5 per inch. To hit R-49, you only need about 8 inches. The material costs more, application takes longer (it's thicker and harder to work with), and you need more expertise. That's why per-square-foot rates are 40–80% higher. But you're also getting superior air barrier performance and moisture resistance.

For a 1,200 sq ft attic in Peoria:

  • Open-cell at $2.25/sq ft = $2,700 total
  • Closed-cell at $3.50/sq ft = $4,200 total

That's real money. Choose the right foam type for your situation, not just the cheapest quote.

What Affects Your Per-Square-Foot Cost (Beyond Foam Type)

Roof pitch is huge. A flat or low-slope roof (4/12 pitch) gets one price. A medium roof (6/12–8/12) gets a slightly higher rate — maybe 5–10% more. A steep roof (10/12 or higher)? That's 15–25% more per square foot because crew safety, equipment setup, and application time all increase. We're working at height, tethered in, moving slower. It costs more.

Old insulation removal. If your attic has blown-in fiberglass or cellulose that needs to come out first, that's an extra $0.30–$0.80 per square foot. If it's in good shape and you want it left alone, zero cost. If it's wet, moldy, or severely compressed, removal is non-negotiable for a solid job.

Access difficulty. A standard residential attic with a full-size hatch? Easy. A 2-foot crawl space or a cramped second-story attic? We're working slower, moving equipment one piece at a time, and safety protocols tighten. That can add 10–20% to labor.

Square footage efficiency. A 2,000 sq ft project is more efficient per square foot than a 500 sq ft project. The setup, equipment placement, and crew overhead are the same, but distributed across more square feet. Small jobs have higher per-square-foot rates. Large jobs have better rates.

Electrical rework. If the attic has outlets, light fixtures, or ceiling wires that need to be relocated out of the spray zone, that's separate work. Usually $50–$200 depending on what's involved.

What's NOT Included in Per-Square-Foot Pricing (Usually)

When I quote a price, I'm clear about what's in and what's not:

  • Permits: Usually $75–$200 depending on your city in Peoria
  • Asbestos surveys: If your home was built before 1980 and has existing blown-in insulation, we may recommend a survey ($300–$500). Old insulation can contain asbestos.
  • Electrical upgrades: If outlets need to be moved or new circuits added, that's licensed electrician work (separate cost)
  • Roof repairs or structural work: If we find rot, damaged rafters, or roof leaks during the job, those are add-ons
  • Vapor barriers or secondary air sealing: Sometimes needed; can be $0.15–$0.50/sq ft depending on scope

Always get a detailed estimate that spells this out. "Per square foot installed" can mean different things to different contractors.

How to Use Per-Square-Foot Pricing to Compare Quotes

When you're shopping around in Peoria, here's how to actually compare:

1. Get total square footage measured. Have each contractor confirm the same square footage. If quotes come in at $2.50/sq ft and $3.50/sq ft but they measured different areas, you're not comparing anything real.

2. Specify foam type and thickness. "I want open-cell to R-49" is different from "I want closed-cell to R-49." Make sure all three quotes are specifying the same. One saying open-cell and one saying closed-cell is not an apples-to-apples comparison.

3. Ask what's included and what's separate. Is removal included? Permits? Electrical work? One contractor's $2.00/sq ft might turn into $3.00/sq ft once you add what they didn't include. Another at $2.75/sq ft might actually be fully comprehensive.

4. Check insurance and licensing. A low per-square-foot rate from an unlicensed crew is not a deal — it's a risk. Ask for proof of liability insurance and workers' comp. In Peoria, licensed spray foam contractors carry this as standard.

5. Red flags. If a quote is wildly below market ($0.75/sq ft) or aggressively high ($6.00+/sq ft without an explanation), that's worth questioning. Market rate in Peoria right now is $1.50–$4.50/sq ft depending on type.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average spray foam insulation cost per square foot in Peoria IL?

Open-cell spray foam in Peoria IL typically runs $1.50–$2.50 per square foot installed, while closed-cell foam is usually $2.50–$4.50 per square foot. These prices include labor, materials, and basic cleanup. Most attic jobs fall in the $2.00–$3.50 per square foot range for open-cell.

Why is closed-cell spray foam more expensive than open-cell?

Closed-cell foam has higher R-value per inch (R-6 to R-6.5 vs. R-3.5 to R-3.6), requires less thickness to reach the same R-value, and provides superior moisture and air barrier performance. The material itself is pricier and takes longer to apply, which justifies the 40–80% higher per-square-foot cost.

Does the slope or pitch of the roof affect spray foam pricing in Peoria?

Absolutely. A steep 12/12 pitch roof costs more per square foot than a gentle 4/12 pitch due to increased crew safety requirements, equipment access challenges, and longer application time. High-pitched roofs can add 15–25% to the per-square-foot cost.

Is the per-square-foot cost the same for attic floor vs. roof deck insulation?

Pricing is usually very similar, but roof deck work is slightly more labor-intensive. Attic floor insulation is often a bit cheaper per square foot because the flat floor is easier to navigate. The difference is usually 5–10%.

What other costs should I expect beyond the per-square-foot price?

Old insulation removal can add $0.30–$0.80 per square foot. Electrical work, permits ($75–$200), and air sealing may be billed separately. Always get a detailed estimate that specifies what's included in the per-square-foot rate.

How can I compare spray foam quotes from contractors in Peoria?

Get three quotes ensuring each specifies foam type (open or closed-cell), thickness, target R-value, square footage, and whether removal is included. Verify insurance and licensing. Be skeptical of quotes significantly below $1.50/sq ft or above $5.00/sq ft without explanation.
