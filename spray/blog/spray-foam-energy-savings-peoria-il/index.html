Spray Foam Energy Savings in Peoria IL — Real Numbers From Local Homes

Published June 16, 2026 • By Peoria Spray Foam Pros • Peoria, IL

Whenever a homeowner in Peoria asks about spray foam, the first question after "how much does it cost?" is always "how much will it really save me on my heating bill?" That's a smart question. You're thinking about payback, not just the sticker price. And I respect that. So here's what I've actually seen on energy bills from real jobs we've done in Peoria homes over the last few years.

The Reality: Where Peoria's Energy Loss Actually Happens

Before I give you the numbers, you need to understand that not all insulation does the same thing. Your attic might have batts, your basement rim joist probably has fiberglass batts or nothing at all, and your crawl space is almost certainly a thermal disaster. The energy loss isn't evenly distributed — it's concentrated in the places where air is actually moving.

Here in Peoria, we get brutal winters. Our gas heating bills from November through March can easily hit $200–$300 per month in a 1,500–2,000 square foot home. And a huge percentage of that heat is escaping through air gaps, not through insulation that's doing its job. That's where spray foam makes a difference that you can actually measure on your utility bill.

Why Is the Savings Range So Wide?

You probably noticed that each project has a range — like $320–$480 for rim joist work. That's not because our installs vary; it's because energy savings depend on factors we can't control:

  • Home age and construction: Older homes built in the 1950s–1980s typically have worse air sealing throughout, so the rim joist work has a bigger impact. Newer homes might already have decent rim joists and better attic sealing, so the savings show up more in crawl space work.
  • Thermostat management: Some homeowners keep their homes at 68 degrees in winter; others go 72 or higher. That directly affects how much heating runs. If you drop your thermostat 3 degrees and use a programmable schedule, you're already saving $200–$300 annually before spray foam.
  • Winter severity: A mild winter like 2024 means lower bills overall. A brutal winter like 2023 means higher bills — but also bigger percentage savings, because the spray foam is stopping more air movement.
  • System efficiency: An older furnace running at 80% efficiency will benefit more from air sealing than a newer 95% efficient system. Both see savings, but the savings pattern is different.

The point: expect your savings to fall somewhere in that range. If you're in an older home with already-tight controls and a mild winter, you might hit the lower end. If you're in an older home, don't manage temperature actively, and get a brutal winter, you'll likely hit the higher end. Either way, you're saving real money.

Why Rim Joists Are Your Money-Maker

The single highest ROI project is always the rim joist. I tell every Peoria homeowner this: if you only have budget for one thing, do the rim joists. Here's why:

Your rim joist is the wooden or concrete rim that connects your basement walls to your first-floor framing. It's exposed to the outside on one side and to your heated basement or crawl space on the other. It's almost never properly insulated — it usually has some batts stuffed in there from 1985 that have settled and don't seal anything. So air is constantly leaking in during winter and out during summer.

Two inches of closed-cell spray foam on a rim joist seals that completely. No air movement. Your furnace doesn't have to work as hard to keep that basement/crawl space temperature stable, and by extension your whole house temperature is easier to maintain. We've seen homes where just the rim joist work knocked $40–$60 off a monthly January or February gas bill. Over a heating season (November–March, roughly 150 days), that's $200–$300 in measured savings.

Cost to do rim joists? Usually $2,500–$3,500 depending on the home size and linear footage. Savings of $300–$400 per year. That's roughly an 8–10 year payback. And the comfort improvement you feel on day one is worth something too — your basement doesn't feel cold and drafty anymore, and you don't get that bitter cold feeling near exterior walls on the first floor.

Summer Cooling Savings (Often Overlooked)

Most Peoria homeowners focus on winter heating because that's where the big bills are. But spray foam helps in summer too. A sealed rim joist keeps hot outdoor air from infiltrating your basement in July and August. A sealed crawl space prevents humid outdoor air from condensing on your ducts and making your AC system work harder.

Summer cooling savings are typically 10–20% of your AC bill, which is smaller in absolute dollars than winter savings because AC bills are just naturally smaller than gas heating bills in Illinois. But if you're running AC from June through September, that's still $30–$60 per month back in your pocket. And you get the added benefit of better temperature stability and less humidity in your basement.

The Payback Math That Actually Matters

Let me break this down simply: if you invest $3,500 in rim joist spray foam and save $350 per year, you're looking at a 10-year payback. That sounds long. But consider this:

  • Your furnace won't run as hard, so it'll last longer and need fewer repairs. That's another couple hundred bucks per year in avoided maintenance.
  • Spray foam doesn't settle or degrade. It'll be working 30 years from now. That fiberglass in your rim joint will be worthless in 15.
  • Your home will be more comfortable immediately — less drafts, more stable temperature, fewer cold spots. That's not a financial return, but it's real.
  • If you ever sell, a properly sealed and insulated home with spray foam in the key areas is a selling point, especially to buyers in a cold climate.

The payback isn't just about the direct energy savings. It's about durability, comfort, and the total cost of ownership over decades.

What About Heat Pumps and High-Efficiency Systems?

One thing I hear more often now is: "We're thinking about switching to a heat pump. Do we still need spray foam?" The answer is yes, actually even more so.

Heat pumps are incredibly efficient at moving heat, but they only work well if your home is already reasonably sealed. A leaky, drafty home makes a heat pump work constantly in cold weather, which reduces its efficiency and increases your electricity bill. Spray foam in the rim joist, crawl space, and other air leak points makes your heat pump perform at its rated efficiency. You get the benefit of the high-efficiency system because the building envelope is tight.

If you're planning a heat pump upgrade, do the spray foam first — or at least the rim joists. It'll make the heat pump investment perform better and last longer.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do energy bills typically drop after spray foam in Peoria?

Savings vary based on your home's size and current condition, but we consistently see $300–$600 per year on gas bills alone from spray foam rim joists and crawl spaces in 1,500–2,000 square foot Peoria homes. Homes with poor attic insulation or large air leaks sometimes see $80–$120 monthly reductions in winter. The key is air sealing, not just R-value.

How quickly does spray foam pay for itself in Peoria?

If you invest $3,000–$4,500 in rim joist and crawl space spray foam and save $400 per year on heating, the upgrade pays for itself in roughly 8–10 years. But many homeowners we've worked with reach payback in 5–7 years because they do larger projects — attics, basements, plus rim joists. And you start feeling the comfort improvement immediately.

Do I save more in Peoria winters or summers?

Winter is where the biggest savings happen because our heating bills are 3–5 times higher than cooling bills. But spray foam helps in summer too by keeping the hot air out and the cool conditioned air from leaking. Many homeowners tell us they notice the temperature is more stable in July and August, which can trim AC bills by 10–20% depending on your system and how well your attic is sealed.

What areas of my Peoria home give the best energy savings with spray foam?

Rim joists are the highest-impact area — they're almost always the worst air-leak point in older Peoria homes and a spray foam upgrade there alone can save you $200–$400 per year. Crawl spaces and attic air leaks are second. Basement walls are worth doing if you're comfortable, but the ROI is longer than rim joists. Focus on the rim joist first.

Will the energy savings be noticeable if I can't see the insulation?

Yes, absolutely. A lot of the energy loss in homes happens where you can't see it — rim joists, crawl spaces, band joists. Those areas are driving most of your heating bill, even though most homeowners don't think about them. Spray foam there stops air movement and reduces your furnace runtime, which shows up immediately on your bills.
