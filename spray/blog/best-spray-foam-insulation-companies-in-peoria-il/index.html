Best Spray Foam Insulation Companies in Peoria IL — How to Pick the Right One

May 12, 2026 Contractor Guide Spray Foam

I'll be straight with you: I'm a spray foam contractor in Peoria, so I obviously have a stake in how you think about choosing an insulation company. But I've also been doing this long enough to know that a bad spray foam job — from any company — is a disaster for the homeowner and bad for the trade as a whole. So here's my honest take on how to vet spray foam companies in the Peoria area, including what separates a legit operation from one that'll leave you with problems you won't discover until the next heating season.

Why the Contractor Matters More Than the Material

Spray foam is different from blown-in or batt insulation in one critical way: the installer has a massive impact on the final product. If the foam is applied off-ratio — meaning the two chemical components aren't mixed at the correct 1:1 ratio — you get foam that stays tacky, shrinks away from surfaces, or never achieves proper R-value. The material cost and the product brand matter, but the technician holding the spray gun matters more.

I've seen homeowners get burned by companies that sent inexperienced crews to jobs they weren't ready for. The foam looked fine on day one and was pulling away from rim joists six months later. By then, the company had been paid and moved on. That's a problem you don't want, and it's exactly why doing a little homework before you hire is worth the time.

What to Look For in a Peoria Spray Foam Company

License and Insurance — Non-Negotiable

Any contractor doing work on your home in Illinois needs to be licensed and carry both general liability insurance and workers' compensation coverage. Ask for a certificate of insurance before anyone sets foot in your crawl space. A legitimate company hands this over without hesitation. If they hedge or say "we'll send it later," that's your answer right there.

Verifiable Local Reviews

Google reviews from Peoria-area homeowners carry real weight. Look for companies with reviews that mention specific neighborhoods, specific applications (crawl space, attic, basement, new construction), and specific outcomes. Vague five-star reviews that just say "great job!" tell you almost nothing. Reviews that describe the job, the crew, and the result tell you a lot. Pay attention to how the company responds to the occasional negative review — that tells you how they handle problems.

Name-Brand Foam Systems

Ask what brand of foam they use. Established manufacturers like Icynene, Lapolla, Demilec, or BASF Walltite have years of real-world performance data behind them. Off-brand or discount foam systems are cheaper for the contractor to buy, which is often why certain quotes come in surprisingly low. You want the same quality of foam going in your walls that the manufacturer warranties — not a knockoff that the crew grabbed from a wholesale distributor.

Written Quote With Specifics

A proper spray foam quote tells you exactly what you're getting: the foam type (open-cell or closed-cell), the installed thickness, the finished R-value, and the application area in square footage. If a contractor gives you a one-line quote — "spray foam crawl space, $1,800" — and nothing else, ask for the breakdown. You need to know what's being installed so you can hold them to it after the job is done.

Pro tip: Take photos of the installed foam after the job is complete. If there are visible gaps, thin spots, or areas where the foam hasn't fully adhered to the substrate, those need to be corrected before you pay the final invoice. A reputable company welcomes this kind of walkthrough — it's how they protect their reputation.

Red Flags to Watch Out For

  • No written contract. Verbal quotes and handshake deals are how disputes happen. Get everything in writing.
  • Pressure to decide same-day. A good contractor doesn't need to pressure you. If someone's pushing hard for a signature before you've had time to compare quotes, walk away.
  • Unusually low bid with no explanation. If one quote is 40% cheaper than the others, find out why. It's almost always material quality, installed thickness, or unlicensed labor.
  • Vague about who's doing the work. Some companies quote jobs and then sub them out to whoever is available. Ask directly: is this your crew, or are you subcontracting?
  • No local references. If a company can't point you to a single completed job in the Peoria area, they may not have the local track record you want to bet your house on.

Questions to Ask Before You Hire

Here's a short list of questions worth asking any spray foam contractor before you commit:

  1. How long has the technician applying the foam been doing spray foam specifically?
  2. What brand and product line of foam do you use?
  3. Can you show me a completed job in Peoria I could look at or contact?
  4. What's your process if the foam doesn't adhere correctly or there are voids?
  5. Does the quote include cleanup and disposal of foam overspray?

A contractor who can answer all of these without hesitation is worth a closer look. One who stumbles on basic questions like what brand of foam they use should probably not be in your crawl space.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I look for in a spray foam insulation company in Peoria IL?
Look for a company that is licensed and insured in Illinois, has hands-on experience with both open-cell and closed-cell spray foam, uses name-brand foam systems (not off-brand materials), and is willing to give you a written quote that spells out exactly what product, thickness, and R-value you're getting. Local references and verifiable online reviews matter too.
How do I know if a spray foam contractor is qualified?
Ask to see their certificate of insurance (general liability and workers' comp), verify their Illinois contractor license, and ask how long the person actually applying the foam has been doing it. Spray foam is applied by a trained technician, not just anyone on the crew. If a company can't tell you the experience level of the person holding the spray gun, that's a red flag.
Why are some spray foam quotes much cheaper than others in Peoria?
The biggest driver is usually material quality and yield ratio. Discount contractors often use lower-quality foam with poor off-ratio performance, or they install thinner layers than quoted. A cheap quote that results in underfilled cavities or off-ratio foam that never cures properly is far more expensive to fix than the money you saved upfront. Always ask what brand of foam system they use and what thickness they're installing.
Is spray foam better than blown-in insulation for Peoria homes?
It depends on the application. For rim joists, crawl spaces, and conditioned attics, spray foam is almost always the better choice because it seals air AND insulates in one step. For open attic floors where you just need R-value and air sealing isn't the primary issue, blown-in can be a cost-effective solution. A good contractor will tell you which product is right for your specific situation — not just sell you the most expensive option.
How long does spray foam insulation last in Peoria IL?
Properly installed spray foam insulation is a permanent solution. Closed-cell foam in particular is extremely durable — it doesn't sag, settle, absorb moisture, or lose R-value over time the way fiberglass batts do. Most spray foam manufacturers offer limited lifetime warranties on the product itself. The key word is "properly installed" — off-ratio or undercured foam can degrade, which is why installer quality matters so much.
