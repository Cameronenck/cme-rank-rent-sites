Attic Insulation Cost Peoria IL — What You'll Actually Pay in 2026

May 11, 2026 Cost Guide Attic Insulation

I get the attic insulation cost question multiple times a week, and I understand why. Quotes vary wildly, the internet gives you ranges so wide they're basically useless, and half the time contractors won't even give you a ballpark until they've already been out to your house. So let me just tell you straight what it actually costs to insulate an attic in Peoria.

Most homeowners in the Peoria area pay $1,200 to $4,500 for attic insulation, depending on the type of material and the size of the space. Spray foam jobs run higher — typically $2,000 to $5,500 — because you're getting both air sealing and insulation in a single pass. If budget is the primary concern, blown-in cellulose or fiberglass is your best option, usually landing between $900 and $2,200 for a typical Peoria home.

What R-Value Does a Peoria Attic Need?

This matters a lot, and most people don't know the answer. Peoria sits in DOE Climate Zone 5. The Department of Energy recommendation for Zone 5 attics is R-49 to R-60. When I go into homes around here, I regularly see attics sitting at R-11 or R-19 — batts that were installed 30 years ago and haven't been touched since.

Getting from R-19 to R-49 means adding roughly 5–6 more inches of blown-in material on top of what's already there, or replacing the existing material entirely and starting fresh with spray foam applied to the roof deck. The right approach depends on what you've already got and whether there's moisture or pest damage involved.

Peoria winters are no joke. We regularly see single-digit temperatures in January and February. An under-insulated attic is just straight money walking out through your roof. The difference between R-19 and R-49 in a Peoria home can be $400–$800 per year in heating costs. Most attic insulation upgrades pay for themselves in 3–5 years.

Spray Foam vs Blown-In: Which Is Right for Your Attic?

This is the question I spend the most time on with homeowners, so let me give you the short version.

Choose Blown-In If...

Your attic is a standard unconditioned space — no HVAC equipment up there, no ductwork, nothing but trusses and roof deck. Blown-in cellulose or fiberglass is cost-effective, installs in a few hours, and gets you to R-49 without breaking the bank. It's the right call for the majority of Peoria attics.

Choose Spray Foam If...

You have HVAC equipment or ductwork in the attic. In that case, you want a conditioned attic — spray foam applied to the underside of the roof deck rather than the attic floor. This brings the entire attic space inside the thermal envelope, which means your furnace and ducts are no longer sitting in a 130-degree oven every July or a 10-degree freezer every January. The energy savings on conditioned attics are significant.

Spray foam is also the right move if your attic has a history of ice damming, moisture issues, or air leakage problems that blown-in alone won't solve. The air seal you get with spray foam is something blown-in simply can't match.

What Else Affects the Final Price

Old Insulation Removal

If your existing insulation is wet, moldy, rodent-contaminated, or just flat-out ancient, it needs to come out before we put new material in. Removal runs $300–$800 for a typical Peoria attic depending on volume. We'll tell you upfront whether removal is necessary — we don't recommend it unless there's a real reason.

Air Sealing Before Insulating

Properly done attic insulation includes sealing air bypasses at top plates, around recessed lights, and at plumbing and electrical penetrations before the insulation goes in. Some contractors skip this step to save time. We don't. Air sealing typically adds $200–$500 to the project but makes a significant difference in actual performance.

Attic Accessibility

A nice big 30x54 inch hatch and a clean, open attic floor is an easy day. A 16-inch scuttle hole in a closet ceiling with three feet of clearance and a maze of cross-bracing takes longer. Difficult access adds labor time, which adds cost — usually $150–$400 depending on how bad it is.

Hazardous Material

Homes built before 1980 occasionally have vermiculite insulation in the attic, which may contain asbestos. If we see something that looks like vermiculite, we'll stop and recommend testing before proceeding. Testing runs $30–$50 and takes 3–5 business days. It's not common, but it's important to check.

What the ROI Looks Like

The average Peoria homeowner who upgrades from R-19 to R-49 sees a 15–25% reduction in heating and cooling costs. On a $2,400 annual utility bill, that's $360–$600 per year. A $1,500 blown-in job pays for itself in 2.5 to 4 years. A $3,500 spray foam job might take 6–8 years — but you also get the air sealing benefit, which blown-in doesn't provide.

If you're selling the house, a fresh attic insulation job is a legitimate selling point. Inspectors note it. Buyers notice it. And it costs you a fraction of what other upgrades do while delivering one of the better energy ROIs on the market.

Frequently Asked Questions About Attic Insulation Cost in Peoria IL

How much does attic insulation cost in Peoria IL?
Most Peoria homeowners spend between $1,200 and $4,500 for attic insulation, depending on the insulation type, attic size, and whether old material needs to be removed first. Spray foam runs higher — typically $2,000–$5,500 — but provides both air sealing and insulation in one pass. Blown-in cellulose or fiberglass is the most affordable option at $900–$2,200 for a typical 1,000–1,500 sq ft attic.
What R-value is recommended for attics in Peoria IL?
The Department of Energy recommends R-49 to R-60 for attics in Climate Zone 5, which includes Peoria IL. Most homes we see in the area are sitting at R-19 or less — way below where they should be, especially given Peoria winters. Hitting R-49 with open-cell spray foam requires about 5.5 inches of foam on the attic floor or roof deck.
Should I insulate my attic floor or the roof deck in Peoria?
It depends on whether your HVAC equipment or ductwork is in the attic. If it is, insulating the roof deck (creating a conditioned attic) is almost always the better choice — you keep your mechanicals inside the thermal envelope. If your attic is purely storage space with no mechanicals, insulating the attic floor is the standard, cost-effective approach. We'll walk you through both options when we come out for your estimate.
Does old attic insulation need to be removed before adding more?
Not always. If your existing insulation is dry, undamaged, and free of mold or pest activity, we can often blow new material right on top of it. If it's wet, compressed, contaminated, or if you're switching to spray foam, removal is usually necessary. Removal adds $300–$800 to the project depending on volume, but it's worth doing right rather than sealing problems in.
How long does attic insulation take to install in Peoria?
Most residential attic insulation jobs in Peoria take one day or less. A standard blown-in job on a 1,200 sq ft attic is typically a 3–4 hour project for a crew of two. Spray foam jobs take a little longer because the foam needs time to cure between passes. If old insulation removal is involved, plan for a full day.
