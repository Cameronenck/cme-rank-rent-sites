Open Cell vs. Closed Cell Foam for Washington, IL Homes

Washington's climate — cold winters that dip below zero, humid summers that push into the 80s and 90s — means the choice between open cell and closed cell foam actually matters.

Open Cell Spray Foam

Open cell foam is softer, more flexible, and significantly less expensive than closed cell. It expands aggressively when sprayed, filling cavities, gaps, and irregular surfaces completely. R-value runs about R-3.7 per inch. It's excellent for interior walls, attic underside applications where you're conditioning the attic space, and areas where moisture vapor permeability is acceptable. In Washington, open cell is a popular choice for cathedral ceilings and interior sound dampening.

Closed Cell Spray Foam

Closed cell foam is denser, harder, and provides R-6.5 to R-7 per inch — nearly double the R-value of open cell at the same thickness. It also acts as a vapor barrier and adds structural rigidity to wall panels. In Washington's climate, closed cell is the right choice for crawl spaces, rim joists, and any below-grade or exterior-facing application where moisture intrusion is a real risk. It costs more but performs dramatically better in harsh conditions.

Most Common Spray Foam Projects in Washington, IL

Attic Insulation

Washington homes built before 1990 almost universally have under-insulated attics. Many were built with fiberglass batts that have settled, compressed, or been disturbed by pest activity or HVAC work over the decades. Spraying open cell foam to the underside of the roof deck converts the attic from an unconditioned space to a conditioned one — which means your HVAC equipment and ductwork running through the attic are no longer fighting winter cold or summer heat. This single change often cuts heating and cooling costs by 15 to 30 percent.

Crawl Space Encapsulation

Crawl spaces in Washington are one of the leading sources of energy loss and moisture problems. Closed cell foam sprayed to the crawl space walls and band joists seals out ground moisture, stops cold air infiltration in winter, and prevents the humidity issues that cause mold, rot, and pest activity over time. Combined with a vapor barrier on the ground, a properly encapsulated crawl space transforms what's usually the worst-performing part of the building envelope.

Rim Joist Sealing

The rim joist — the framing member that sits on top of your foundation wall and supports your floor system — is one of the most overlooked air leak points in central Illinois homes. Two inches of closed cell foam sprayed across the full perimeter of your rim joists can have an outsized effect on comfort and heating bills, especially in first-floor rooms above the crawl space or basement. It's often one of the best bang-for-buck upgrades in an older Washington home.
