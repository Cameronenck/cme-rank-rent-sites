Air sealing is the process of closing gaps, cracks, and penetrations in your home's building envelope — the boundary between conditioned indoor space and the outdoors. Done correctly, it reduces drafts, cuts heating and cooling bills, improves indoor air quality, and helps your HVAC system last longer. In Peoria's climate, where winters are long and summers are humid, a tight building envelope pays for itself faster than almost any other home improvement.

What Drives the Cost Up or Down?

Not every air sealing job is the same. Several factors push the total cost in one direction or another:

  • Home size: More square footage generally means more penetrations and a longer labor day.
  • Home age: Older homes have more gaps, more bypasses in the attic framing, and often require more prep work before sealing.
  • Attic accessibility: Finished attics or tight crawlspaces add time and complexity.
  • Number of recessed lights: Each can-light penetration is a direct pathway to the attic and must be sealed individually.
  • Rim joist condition: Rim joists are one of the biggest sources of air infiltration in midwestern homes. If they have never been sealed, expect more material and labor.
  • Blower door testing: If you add a pre- and post-test to verify results, budget an additional $200-$400 for the testing itself.

Where Does Air Actually Escape in a Peoria Home?

Most of our customers are surprised to learn that windows and doors are rarely the biggest leaks in a home. The real culprits are:

  • Attic hatch or pull-down stairs: Poorly sealed hatches can account for more air loss than a window left open 24/7.
  • Rim joists: The wood framing where the floor structure meets the foundation wall is almost never sealed in homes built before 2000.
  • Electrical outlets and switch plates: Every box on an exterior wall or top-floor ceiling is a direct penetration through the envelope.
  • Recessed light fixtures: Older non-IC-rated fixtures have open tops that dump conditioned air straight into the attic.
  • Plumbing and HVAC chases: Pipes running through walls and floors leave gaps that connect conditioned space to unconditioned attics or crawlspaces.
  • Top plates in exterior walls: Where the wall framing meets the ceiling, gaps often run the entire perimeter of the house.

How to Choose an Air Sealing Contractor in Peoria

Not every insulation company offers true building-science-based air sealing. Here is what to look for when comparing quotes:

  • Blower door testing: A contractor who offers a pre- and post-blower-door test is doing the job right. This verifies actual improvement, not just billing hours.
  • Attic and crawlspace access: Make sure the quote includes work above and below the living space, not just caulking around windows.
  • Two-part spray foam vs. caulk: For large gaps around plumbing chases and rim joists, two-part spray foam is far more durable and effective than caulk or foam backer rod.
  • Written scope of work: Any legitimate contractor will provide a line-item quote specifying which areas will be sealed and with what materials.
  • Illinois licensing: Confirm the contractor is licensed and carries general liability insurance in Illinois.

Is Air Sealing Worth the Investment?

For most Peoria homeowners, the answer is yes. The EPA estimates that air sealing combined with proper insulation can reduce heating and cooling costs by 15-20%. On a typical Peoria energy bill of $175/month, that is $315-$420 per year in savings. A $1,200 air sealing project pays back in three to four years — and the benefits compound every year after.

Beyond the financial return, homeowners consistently tell us the comfort improvement is immediate. Cold floors in winter, hot second-floor bedrooms in summer, and persistent dustiness are all symptoms of a leaky building envelope. Air sealing addresses the root cause, not just the symptom.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does air sealing cost in Peoria, IL?

Most Peoria homeowners pay between $850 and $2,500 for a whole-home air sealing project. Smaller spot-sealing jobs start around $300-$500, while large older homes with significant infiltration can run higher.

Does air sealing require a blower door test?

A blower door test is not always required, but it is highly recommended. It costs $200-$400 and tells you exactly where air is escaping so work is targeted and effective.

How long does air sealing take?

A typical whole-home air sealing job in Peoria takes one full day. Larger homes or those needing extensive attic work may take two days.

Will air sealing lower my energy bill?

Yes. The EPA estimates that properly air sealing and insulating a home can cut heating and cooling costs by 15-20%. In central Illinois, where winters are harsh, the savings are often significant.

Can I air seal my home myself?

DIY air sealing with caulk and weatherstripping around doors and windows is possible, but professional contractors reach areas like rim joists, attic bypasses, and recessed light penetrations that most homeowners cannot access safely or effectively.
