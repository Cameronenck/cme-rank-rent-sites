How to Choose a Spray Foam Insulation Contractor in Peoria IL

Published May 2026 • 6 min read • By Peoria Spray Foam Pros

Spray foam insulation is one of the highest-value upgrades a Peoria homeowner can make — but only when it's done right. Bad spray foam installation is worse than no spray foam at all. I've been installing SPF in central Illinois for over 11 years, and here's how to find a contractor you can actually trust.

Certifications and Training Matter More Than Price

Spray foam is not a DIY product and it's not something you learn on the job in a week. Proper installation requires understanding chemistry, equipment calibration, substrate temperatures, and building science. The Spray Polyurethane Foam Alliance (SPFA) offers professional certification programs that trained installers complete.

Ask your contractor directly: "Are your installers SPFA certified or equivalent?" If they don't know what that is or dismiss it as unnecessary, keep looking. In Illinois, you also want to confirm they understand state building code requirements for foam thickness, ignition barriers, and vapor control in different climate zones.

Ask About Their Equipment

Two-component spray foam requires specialized proportioning equipment that mixes the A and B components at precise ratios and temperatures. When this equipment is dirty, poorly calibrated, or running the wrong temperatures, the foam won't cure correctly — and that means a sticky, smelly, underperforming installation that can take weeks to off-gas.

Ask how often they maintain and calibrate their equipment. Ask what they do if they hit a proportioning problem mid-job. A contractor who has clear answers to these questions — and who talks about equipment with some fluency — is running a real operation. See our insulation services for what we do.

Know Which Foam Type They're Recommending and Why

There are two main types of spray polyurethane foam: open cell (lower density, higher R-value per inch of cost, vapor permeable) and closed cell (higher density, true vapor barrier, structural rigidity). The right choice depends on where it's going:

  • Exterior walls, rim joists, crawl spaces, basement walls: closed cell. Moisture control is critical here in Illinois.
  • Attic ceiling cavities, interior walls for sound: open cell is often appropriate and more cost-effective.
  • Cathedral ceilings and unvented attics: closed cell if you need vapor control above the foam layer.

A contractor who recommends the same product for every application regardless of context is either uninformed or just selling what they have on the truck. A good contractor explains the tradeoffs and makes a specific recommendation based on your house and your goals.

Check References From Local Peoria Jobs

Ask for references from completed jobs in Peoria or surrounding communities — ideally jobs that are at least 2–3 years old. You want to know if the foam is still performing, if there were any off-gassing issues, and if the contractor was easy to work with. A confident contractor will hand you a reference list without hesitation.

Get the Air Sealing Picture, Not Just the Foam Pitch

Spray foam insulates and air seals simultaneously — that's its main advantage over fiberglass and cellulose. But a good contractor should be talking about the whole building envelope, not just spraying foam and leaving. Ask if they assess your home's existing air leakage points before recommending where to focus the foam.

The biggest energy savings in most Peoria homes come from sealing rim joists, attic bypasses, and penetrations — not just adding thickness in the middle of a wall cavity. If a contractor doesn't mention air sealing as part of the conversation, they're missing the point of what spray foam does best.

Frequently Asked Questions

What certifications should a spray foam contractor have?

Look for contractors whose installers hold SPFA (Spray Polyurethane Foam Alliance) certification or equivalent training. This means they've been trained on proper mix ratios, temperature requirements, PPE usage, and building code compliance — all of which affect whether the foam performs as advertised.

How do I know if a contractor is using quality spray foam product?

Ask for the brand and product data sheet. Reputable contractors use commercial-grade two-component systems from established manufacturers. Avoid any contractor who won't name their product or is vague about the mix ratio and yield.

How long does spray foam insulation take to install?

A typical attic spray foam job takes 4–8 hours depending on size. Crawl spaces vary more. The foam fully cures in 24 hours, after which it's completely safe and odorless.

Should I use open cell or closed cell spray foam in my Peoria home?

Closed cell for exterior walls, rim joists, crawl spaces, and anywhere moisture control is critical. Open cell for interior walls and sound dampening. Closed cell costs more but provides vapor control that matters in the Illinois climate. We cover this in detail in our open cell vs closed cell article.

What should I watch out for when hiring a spray foam contractor?

Bad mix ratios are the biggest hidden risk — improperly mixed foam won't cure properly and will emit off-gases for weeks. Ask about their equipment maintenance schedule and how they verify mix ratios on each job. Also confirm they're using appropriate PPE and ventilating the space properly during installation.
