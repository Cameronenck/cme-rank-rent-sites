Leaking or damaged ductwork is one of the least visible and most costly problems in Ohio homes. The U.S. Department of Energy estimates that the average forced-air system loses 20 to 30 percent of its conditioned air through duct leaks before it ever reaches the living space. In Ohio's climate — with winters that demand serious heating and summers that push central air hard — that waste shows up directly on your utility bill. Here's what ductwork repair costs in Ohio and what drives the price.

Average Ductwork Repair Costs in Ohio

Ductwork repair in Ohio typically runs between $300 and $2,500 depending on the extent of the damage, how accessible the duct runs are, and whether sealing or physical replacement is needed. Most single-family homes in Ohio fall between $400 and $1,200 for a professional repair and sealing job.

Signs Your Ohio Home Has Duct Problems

Duct damage rarely announces itself dramatically. Most Ohio homeowners notice the symptoms long before they identify the cause. Here's what to look for:

  • High utility bills — if your heating or cooling costs have crept up without a change in usage habits, duct leakage is a common culprit
  • Uneven temperatures — rooms that never get comfortable regardless of thermostat settings often have restricted or leaking supply ducts
  • Dusty vents — ducts with holes or disconnected sections in attics, crawl spaces, or walls pull in unconditioned air and whatever dust is in it
  • HVAC running constantly — when conditioned air leaks before reaching its destination, the system compensates by running longer cycles
  • Visible damage — flex duct that's kinked, crushed, or disconnected at the collar; sheet metal sections with obvious holes, rust, or separated joints

Common Ductwork Issues in Ohio Homes

Disconnected Flex Duct

Flexible ductwork is prevalent in Ohio homes built from the 1980s through 2000s. Flex duct is inexpensive and easy to install but develops problems over time — connections at boots and collars loosen, the inner liner can tear, and sections get crushed during attic or crawl space maintenance. A disconnected flex duct run can dump your HVAC output directly into an attic instead of into a bedroom. Reconnecting and properly securing the connection typically costs $150 to $400 per section.

Leaky Sheet Metal Joints

Older Ohio homes with sheet metal duct systems often have joints that were sealed with standard duct tape decades ago. Contrary to its name, duct tape deteriorates badly in temperature extremes — exactly what attic and crawl space environments deliver. Failed duct tape seals leave gaps at every joint in the system. Re-sealing with mastic compound (the correct material for the job) dramatically reduces leakage across all joints in the system.

Uninsulated Ductwork in Unconditioned Spaces

Duct runs through uninsulated attics or crawl spaces in Ohio lose significant energy to the surrounding environment even when the ducts aren't leaking. In summer, uninsulated supply ducts passing through 130-degree attic air lose cooling before the air reaches the register. In winter, the reverse happens with heat. Adding R-6 or R-8 insulation wrap to uninsulated duct runs in these spaces is one of the highest-ROI HVAC upgrades for Ohio homes with older duct systems.

Duct Sealing vs. Duct Replacement in Ohio

Most Ohio duct systems benefit from sealing rather than replacement. Full duct replacement is only warranted when the system is severely deteriorated — extensive pest damage, significant mold contamination, or sheet metal that's rusting through. For the vast majority of Ohio homes with leaky ducts, professional sealing with mastic or Aeroseal technology restores system efficiency at a fraction of replacement cost.

Aeroseal is a technology where pressurized sealant particles are injected into the duct system and seek out leaks from the inside. It's particularly effective for sealed-ceiling systems where the ducts are inaccessible for direct manual sealing. The cost is higher than mastic application but it reaches leaks that can't be accessed any other way.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does ductwork repair cost in Ohio?

Ductwork repair in Ohio typically costs between $300 and $2,500 depending on the repair type and extent of damage. Mastic sealing on an accessible system runs $300 to $600 per zone. Aeroseal whole-system sealing runs $900 to $2,000. Physical duct repair or partial replacement is typically $200 to $1,800 depending on scope.

How do I know if my ductwork is leaking in my Ohio home?

Common signs include high utility bills, rooms that won't heat or cool properly, dusty or dirty air vents, and the HVAC running constantly without reaching the set temperature. A professional duct pressure test or Aeroseal diagnostic can quantify the leakage level.

Is it worth repairing ductwork or should I replace it in Ohio?

In most cases, repair and sealing is the right choice for Ohio homes. Full replacement is only warranted for severely deteriorated systems with extensive pest damage, mold, or structural failure. Sealing with mastic or Aeroseal restores efficiency at a fraction of replacement cost.

What is Aeroseal duct sealing and is it available in Ohio?

Aeroseal is a technology that seals duct leaks from the inside out — pressurized sealant particles are injected into the duct system and adhere to leak points as they try to escape. It's particularly effective for systems where duct runs are inaccessible for direct sealing. Yes, we offer Aeroseal in Ohio. Cost runs $900 to $2,000 for a typical home.

How long does ductwork repair take in Ohio?

Most ductwork repairs in Ohio are completed in a single day. Mastic sealing of an accessible system takes 3 to 6 hours depending on system size. Aeroseal takes 4 to 8 hours including diagnostics and the sealing process. Full or partial duct replacement may require 1 to 2 days depending on scope.
