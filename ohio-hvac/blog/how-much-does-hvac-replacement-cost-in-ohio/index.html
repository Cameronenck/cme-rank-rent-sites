How Much Does HVAC Replacement Cost in Ohio? (2026 Prices)

Published May 2026 • Ohio HVAC Pros Editorial Team

HVAC replacement is one of the larger home expenses Ohio homeowners face — typically $3,500 to $15,000 depending on what you're replacing and what you're replacing it with. This guide breaks down costs by system type, explains what drives the price in Ohio specifically, and gives you the information to evaluate quotes confidently.

The Bottom Line on HVAC Costs in Ohio

Before getting into details: Ohio HVAC replacement costs sit close to the national average, but labor rates vary meaningfully between urban and rural markets. A new system in Columbus or Cleveland will cost more in labor than the same system in a smaller county like Vinton or Monroe. Equipment costs are roughly the same everywhere.

What Drives the Price in Ohio

System Size (Tonnage)

Tonnage refers to the cooling capacity of an AC or heat pump. Ohio homes typically need 2 to 5 tons of cooling capacity. The right size is determined by a Manual J load calculation — not by the old system's size or a contractor's guess. Oversized systems short-cycle, undersized ones struggle on 95°F days. Expect about $700-$1,000 more per ton as you go up in capacity.

Efficiency Rating (SEER2 for Cooling, AFUE for Heating)

Higher efficiency costs more upfront but saves on monthly utility bills. In Ohio, where you run AC June through September and heat from November through March, efficiency upgrades have real payback periods. A jump from 14 SEER2 to 18 SEER2 might add $800-$1,200 to the purchase price but save $120-$200 per year on electricity. Over 10 years, that's $1,200-$2,000 saved — ahead of the upgrade cost.

For furnaces, a jump from 80% AFUE to 96% AFUE costs $500-$1,500 more and saves 12-20% on gas bills annually. In Ohio, where winter heating bills can run $150-$300/month, that's meaningful savings.

Brand

Premium brands (Trane, Carrier, Lennox) typically run $800-$1,500 more than mid-tier brands (Rheem, York, Goodman) for comparable specifications. The premium buys better warranties and in some cases better reliability data, but mid-tier brands from reputable Ohio contractors are solid equipment. Don't let brand alone drive your decision when installer quality matters equally.

Ohio Labor Market Differences

Columbus and Cleveland Metro HVAC labor runs $85-$125/hour. Cincinnati is similar. Toledo and Akron are slightly lower, around $75-$105/hour. In smaller Ohio markets — Zanesville, Chillicothe, Findlay — rates can be $65-$90/hour. A standard installation that takes 6 hours represents a $500-$700 swing in labor cost between markets.

Ohio-Specific Cost Examples

Columbus (Franklin County)

A 2,200 square foot home in Columbus replacing a 16-year-old 3-ton AC system with a 3-ton, 16 SEER2 Carrier unit: approximately $5,400-$6,200 installed including permit. Same home replacing both AC and a 20-year-old furnace with a matched 3-ton system (96% AFUE furnace + 16 SEER2 AC): $8,500-$10,500.

Cleveland (Cuyahoga County)

Cleveland-area homes often have older systems due to the stock age of housing in many neighborhoods. A 1,400 square foot Lakewood bungalow replacing a failed furnace with a high-efficiency 80,000 BTU unit: $3,200-$4,800. The same home adding central AC where there was none before (new equipment + minimal ductwork modification): $4,500-$6,500.

Cincinnati (Hamilton County)

Cincinnati sees Ohio's warmest summers — July averages 90°F. A 2,800 square foot home in Mason or Blue Ash replacing a complete system with a 4-ton premium unit: $10,000-$14,000 installed. Budget systems for the same home: $7,500-$9,500.

Permit Costs in Ohio

Ohio requires mechanical permits for HVAC replacements. Permit costs vary by municipality:

  • Columbus/Franklin County: $100-$200 for standard replacement
  • Cleveland/Cuyahoga County: $75-$175
  • Cincinnati/Hamilton County: $90-$200
  • Smaller Ohio counties: $50-$150

Your contractor pulls the permit and handles inspection scheduling. This is not optional — unpermitted HVAC work affects homeowner's insurance coverage and creates disclosure issues at resale.

Financing Options for Ohio Homeowners

HVAC replacement doesn't have to be an out-of-pocket emergency expense. Ohio homeowners have several financing paths:

  • Manufacturer programs: Carrier, Trane, Lennox, and others offer 0% financing for 12-24 months through their dealer networks. Ask your contractor which programs apply to your installation.
  • HVAC contractor plans: Many Ohio contractors offer in-house or third-party financing at competitive rates, especially for whole-system replacements.
  • Home equity: If you have equity, a HELOC or home equity loan offers the lowest interest rates for larger projects ($8,000+).
  • Personal loans: For smaller replacements without financing offers, a personal loan from a bank or credit union is straightforward.
  • Utility rebates + IRA credits: AEP Ohio, Columbia Gas, and Duke Energy offer rebates for high-efficiency equipment. The IRA provides up to $2,000 for qualifying heat pump installations and up to $600 for AC units. These don't require financing but reduce out-of-pocket cost immediately.

The 5,000 Rule: Repair or Replace?

A standard contractor rule of thumb: multiply the system's age by the repair cost. If the result is over $5,000, lean toward replacement. A 15-year-old system facing a $400 repair: $6,000 — replacement is worth a quote. A 6-year-old system facing the same repair: $2,400 — repair it.

Context matters: a cracked heat exchanger is always a replacement regardless of the math. A refrigerant leak on a 10-year-old system with good history might still be worth repairing.

HVAC quote variation in Ohio often runs $1,500-$3,000 for the same scope of work. The highest quote is not always the best quality; the lowest is not always the worst. HVAC Cost Questions Answered

How much does HVAC replacement cost in Ohio?
HVAC replacement in Ohio typically costs $5,000 to $15,000 for a complete system (furnace + AC), depending on system size, efficiency, brand, and whether ductwork work is needed. Replacing just the AC runs $3,500 to $8,500; furnace alone $2,500 to $6,500.
What is the average cost of a new furnace in Ohio?
A new gas furnace in Ohio costs $2,500 to $6,500 installed, including equipment, labor, and permits. High-efficiency 96% AFUE units run toward the top of the range but reduce monthly gas bills by 15-20% compared to older 80% AFUE models.
Does Ohio require a permit for HVAC replacement?
Yes. Ohio requires mechanical permits for HVAC replacements in virtually every municipality. Permit costs range from $75 to $300 depending on county. Your licensed contractor handles this — never hire someone who suggests skipping permits.
What financing options exist for HVAC replacement in Ohio?
Options include manufacturer 0% financing programs, contractor payment plans, personal loans, home equity loans, and utility PACE programs. The IRA tax credit (up to $2,000 for heat pumps, $600 for AC) also reduces out-of-pocket cost and doesn't require repayment.
