Most Common Furnace Problems in Ohio

Failed Igniter

The igniter lights the gas burner. When it fails, your furnace attempts to start, fails, and enters lockout mode. You'll hear the furnace try to ignite (usually 2-3 times) and then shut off. Igniter replacement is one of the most common furnace repairs in Ohio — typically $100-$200 for parts and labor.

Dirty Flame Sensor

The flame sensor tells the furnace that the burner actually lit. Dirt buildup prevents it from reading the flame properly, causing the furnace to shut off as a safety measure. A cleaning or replacement costs $80-$150 and takes an experienced technician about an hour.

Failing Blower Motor

The blower motor circulates heated air through your ductwork. Symptoms include weak airflow, unusual squealing or grinding noises, or the heat running but rooms staying cold. Blower motor replacement runs $300-$600 depending on the unit.

Cracked Heat Exchanger

This is the most serious furnace problem and requires immediate action. A cracked heat exchanger allows combustion gases — including carbon monoxide — to mix with your home's air. If a technician diagnoses a cracked heat exchanger, the furnace must be shut down immediately. In most cases, the age of the furnace makes replacement the right call rather than a costly repair.

Pressure Switch Failure

The pressure switch monitors the furnace's draft and combustion process. A failed pressure switch typically prevents the furnace from starting at all. Replacement costs $150-$250.

Repair vs. Replace: When to Walk Away

A rough rule that most Ohio HVAC professionals use: multiply the furnace's age by the repair cost. If the result exceeds $5,000, replacement is usually the better investment. For example, a 20-year-old furnace needing a $400 repair = $8,000 — replacement is likely smarter.

Specific situations that should always point to replacement:

  • Cracked heat exchanger — this is a non-negotiable safety replacement
  • Furnace over 20 years old with a repair over $500
  • Multiple repairs within the same heating season
  • Significantly rising gas bills pointing to efficiency loss
  • AFUE rating under 80% — modern units run 95-98%

Warning Signs Your Furnace Needs Attention

  • Furnace runs but doesn't produce heat
  • Unusual banging, squealing, or rattling sounds
  • Furnace cycles on and off every few minutes
  • Yellow or orange flame instead of blue
  • Uneven heating across different rooms
  • Gas bills rising without weather explanation
  • Carbon monoxide detector alerts
  • Excessive dust or dry air throughout the home

What to Do While Waiting for a Technician

If your furnace fails during a cold Ohio night, these steps help while you wait for a contractor:

  1. Check your thermostat — confirm it's set to heat and above room temperature
  2. Replace the air filter if it's visibly clogged — a blocked filter can trigger safety shutoff
  3. Check the circuit breaker for the furnace
  4. Locate the furnace's power switch (looks like a light switch, usually on the furnace or nearby wall)
  5. Use portable electric space heaters in bedrooms and living spaces
  6. Close interior doors to contain heat in occupied rooms
  7. If you smell gas, leave immediately and call 911 before calling an HVAC contractor

Furnace Repair FAQ for Ohio Homeowners

How much does furnace repair cost in Ohio?
Most furnace repairs in Ohio cost between $150 and $600 for parts and labor. Common repairs like a failed igniter ($100-$200), faulty pressure switch ($150-$250), or blower motor replacement ($300-$600) fall in this range. Emergency after-hours calls typically add $50-$150 to the base cost.
What are the most common furnace problems in Ohio winters?
The most common Ohio furnace problems are igniter failure, dirty flame sensor, failing blower motor, cracked heat exchanger, and pressure switch failure. Many of these are straightforward repairs for a licensed technician and can be completed same-day.
When should I repair vs. replace my Ohio furnace?
A common rule: if the repair cost exceeds 50% of the cost of a new furnace, and your current furnace is over 15 years old, replacement usually makes more financial sense. A cracked heat exchanger is always a replacement scenario — carbon monoxide risk makes it non-negotiable.
Can Ohio furnace repair be done same-day?
Yes. Most contractors in the Ohio HVAC Pros network offer same-day service for emergency furnace calls. If you call before noon, same-day repair is usually possible. After-hours and weekend calls are available through our emergency network across all Ohio regions.
Is a yellow furnace flame dangerous?
Yes. A yellow or orange flame indicates incomplete combustion and can signal carbon monoxide production. Turn off your furnace, open windows, leave the house, and call a licensed Ohio HVAC technician immediately. Do not return to the house until it has been inspected.
